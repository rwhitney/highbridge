EAST BOISE COUNTY AMBULANCE

ADVISORY BOARD MEETING

MINUTES OF FEBRUARY 1, 2001

 

Hal Gross called the meeting to order at 5:35 p.m.  No decisions to be made due to Commissioner Jackson absence.

 

Present: Hal Gross, Tom Wright, Joe Weiss, Cyndi Watson, Dirk Myers and Treasea Long.

 

Guests: Phyllis Weiss and Traci Kinsman

 

Amendments to the Agenda: None.

 

Minutes of December 7, 2000 were reviewed and no corrections noted. Dirk Myers motioned and Tom Wright seconded to approve the minutes as presented.  All ayes.  Motion carried.

 

Disaster Services:  Hal Gross presented each unit with booklets for emergency response and terrorism.  More people will be flowing into Idaho during 2002 Olympics in Salt Lake City and Boise County should be prepared.  He is signing Boise County up for language services provided by AT&T before and after Olympics.  142 languages will be available if needed.  Working on getting a new data recorder for BCSO.  Stated that many grants require “in-kind” matching funds.  Volunteer hours at $10/hr count towards the match.  Each unit should keep track of volunteer hours on time sheets or spreadsheets and copies to the Clerk’s office for insurance purposes.

 

Mission Statements:  A mission statement was presented to the Board.  Dirk Myers motioned and Cyndi Watson seconded to approve the Mission Statement as presented.  All ayes.  Motion carried.

 

Issue Tracking

            Meal Reimbursement Procedures:  Only Mores Creek submitted plan to Hal.  Stated $8/EMT on run sheet, take-out only in Boise, run sheet number on receipt with EMT names.  Was $6 but raised to $8.  Discussion of local vs. on-the-run meals from Boise.  Placerville and Idaho City feel meals are only compensation to volunteers.  What are qualifiers?  Phyllis Weiss suggested $8/person/run capping at $24/run using run sheet number on receipt.  Issue tabled until Commissioner Jackson returns.

            ICRMP:  $1,000 deductible.  Placerville disagrees with BOCC decision to accept policy and information requirement.

            Mutual Aid Agreements:  Drafted copy of what was previously done presented.  Look over and advise.

            Licensing Discussion of pros and cons to one license vs. four currently in use.  Grant dollars should not be issue if grant written well and application clear.  If EBCA does go to one license, then one SOP should be enacted and filed with County Clerk as public information.  Copies of Caribou County SOP are in Clerk’s office and being modified for EBCA Board discussion and approval.  Individual units can still have their SOP’s but must be approved by EBCA Board.  Continue to discuss at next meeting.

 

Tom Wright (Life Flight):  Rumors that Lifeflight does not land at St. Lukes are untrue except in case of trauma patients.  Only St. Alphonsus is trauma certified.  Discussion of Auto Dispatch system at State Com.  EBCA and Lifeflight have agreement for payment but could go to flat fee in near future to be negotiated.  Agreed that most Boise County Residents have Lifeflight memberships and Access Air will not honor those memberships.  Is “time bidding” legal?  Trauma Legislation is in process to make all accountable.  Idaho City requested Lifeflight presence at motorcycle races.  Tom to check and advise.

 

Dirk Myers (Mores Creek): Questioned other units about their satisfaction regarding their membership status.  If units could not fulfill obligations then County would contract out ambulance services so how much would that cost? And could portion of that be allotted to volunteers?  County needs to look for financially prudent way to compensate volunteers.

            Hal discussed Volunteer of the Quarter recommendations.  If problems with solutions were brought to BOCC then would receive more reception.

            Dirk requested more radios.  Per County Clerk, hold off until February to order.  Asked Treasea about run sheet processing.  Also discussed attrition amongst volunteers.  Placerville suggested EMT’s pay for training so they have investment in service and interview perspective EMT’s prior to training.

 

Kristine E. Vollmer (Lowman): Unable to attend due to prior commitment.

 

Joe Weiss (Placerville): Wanted discussion over Placerville volunteer dismissals and equipment return policy.  Hal advised no discussion allowed due to investigation.  Must have EMT’s sign for uniforms if they are to be returned after service ended.  Advised Mores Creek that they are unable to read their schedule as e-mailed.  Traci provided hard copy.  Questioned alternating meeting place to Lowman?  Suggested there be no “official” EBCA Board meeting if no Commissioner.  Joe motioned and Dirk seconded to approve “unofficial” meeting only.  All ayes.  Motion carried.  Questioned why door combo lock on Idaho City ambulance shed has not been changed.  Requested that supplies list sheet be signed in order to track.

 

Cyndi Watson (Idaho City): Re-stated need for new thermometer.  Advised to check with Clerk’s office for approval to order budgeted items.  Advised that the EMT refresher course is Feb. 16,17 & 18, paid by grant so all members who need it should attend.

 

Open To Floor:   Joe Solicited funds from St’ Al’s to purchase turbo-lights for Lifeflight landings.  Cost is $800 for eight, needs about $600.

            Hal distributed EMS service cards to be handed out to volunteers. 

            Joe brought up BCSO extrication truck dispatching procedures need to be changed; in event of MVA, head-on or rollover; extrication truck is automatically dispatched.

            Phyllis Weiss mentioned new CPR changes.  Tom Wright to check on.

 

Joe Weiss motioned meeting be adjourned and Tom Wright seconded to adjourn.  All ayes.  Motion carried.  Meeting adjourned at 8:03 PM.

 

The Advisory Board will resume on March 1st at 5:30pm.
