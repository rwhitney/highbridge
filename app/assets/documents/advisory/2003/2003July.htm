EAST BOISE COUNTY AMBULANCE ADVISORY BOARD MINUTES

Boise County Chambers

July 10, 2003

 

 

The East Boise County Ambulance Advisory Board met in Regular Session on Thursday July 10, 2003 at 6:00 p.m. in the Boise County Commissioner’s Chambers in Idaho City.  Present: Chairman Roger Jackson, Phyllis Weiss of Placerville Ambulance, Cyndi Watson of Idaho City Ambulance, Betty Myers of Lowman Ambulance, Beth Chandler of Mores Creek Ambulance, Tom Wright of Life Flight, and Clerk Rora Canody.

 

CALL TO ORDER: Roger Jackson called the meeting to order at 6:00 p.m.

 

AMENDMENTS TO AGENDA: There were no amendments to the agenda.

 

MINUTES: Deputy Clerk Browning’s full minutes of the regular meeting of June 5th 2003 were presented for approval.  John McCarthy made a motion to approve the June  5th minutes as corrected, seconded by Phyllis Weiss. All ayes.

 

ISSUE TRACKING:

 

SOP’s:   Betty Myers reports that the SOP’s are almost complete.  Deputy Clerk Browning will be making changes and printing the final draft within the next two weeks.  The draft will be mailed to the “SOP Committee” for final comments, then presented to the Advisory Board for approval.  The SOP’s will then go to the Board of County Commissioners for adoption.

MISCELLANEOUS AND CORRESPONDENCE:

 

MIETTA SIBERT, “C.E.R.T.” (Community Emergency Response Training):

Fawn Carey reported in Ms. Siberts absence.  

Bureau of Disaster Services is offering a program to Boise County called C.E.R.T.  This class is for local citizens not first responders.  This gives citizens some basic skills to help them function at an incident until a first responder arrives.  Once a first responder is on the scene the C.E.R.T.  trained citizen would turn the scene over to the first responder.  The training provides basics on first aid, fire and terrorism. The training will begin some time between now and December 2003.  There will be 8 classes which last 2 to 3 hours.  Fawn requests an EBCA member to teach first aid as well as the East Boise County Advisory Board’s approval to pursue this training.  The Board agreed to proceed with C.E.R.T. training. 

 

 

 

 

PROCEDURES FOR COMPLAINTS: 

Chairman Jackson stated that from this time forward any complaints to the Boise County Commissioners will be in writing and signed by the complaining person. Otherwise a complaint will not be acknowledged.  No complaints “phoned in” will be considered.  Complaints brought to the Advisory Board must be in writing and signed by the complainant before the issue will be discussed by the Advisory Board.  Written and signed complaints may be delivered or mailed to Clerk Canody or Deputy Clerk Browning at the Clerk’s office.  The necessary steps to place the issue on the EBCA Advisory Board agenda will then be taken.

 

EXECUTIVE SESSIONS:

Chairman Jackson clarified that any information discussed during an EBCA Advisory Board Executive Session is strictly confidential and handed out copies of I.C.Section 67-2345.  This information can only be discussed within the Advisory Board members.  A substitute Advisory Board Member who is in executive session is not to share information with the member for whom they are filling in; however the absent member may speak with Chairman Jackson to obtain information.

 

COMPUTER LAP-TOP SECURITY :

Commissioner Jackson inquired whether the Lap Top computers were “locked down” in the Idaho City Ambulance.  Randy Smith assured  Chairman Jackson that the computer was locked in place with keys.  Chairman Jackson re-iterated that these computers are not to be used by anyone for anything other than Boise County business. 

 

RUN SHEET MISSING INFORMATION:

Deputy Clerk Browning has reported that she is not receiving completed run sheets from Mores Creek Ambulance.   The Mores Creek unit stated that they were unaware of this problem. Discussion regarding responsibility for obtaining information ensued.  Randy Smith from Idaho City Ambulance reported that Saint Al’s has a sign up sheet and an EMT can fill it out so St. Als will fax the information to the designated fax within about 1 to 1 ½ hours of the ambulance run. It is the responsibility of the ambulance staff to obtain information for billing via the run sheets.

 

RESCUE ONE AND TWO: John McCarthy stated that they have not been filling out separate run sheets for the rescue truck.  Present charges of $200.00 are not sufficient to cover costs.   John McCarthy volunteered to work with Terry Teeter to create a draft fee schedule for the Rescue vehicle.

 

PROTECTIVE GEAR:  Discussion was tabled.  Protective gear will be addressed in the S.O.P.s.  Specific issues shall be discussed by the individual unit.

 

INDIVIDUAL UNITS:

 

PLACERVILLE:   Phyllis Weiss reports on the EMSAC meeting she attended:

1)  EMSAC is offering first responders a short class to become an EMT-B.  The class is 45 to 50 hours and has been approved for all State Certified First Responders.

 

2)  EMSAC states that epi-pens may be used as soon as the EBCA Medical Director writes the protocol and trains the EMTs.  Phyllis has contacted Dr. Koenig. He is writing the protocol now and believes the epi-pens may be in use in one month. 

 

3) A 75 hour class is offered by EMSAC; an EMT-B’ can go directly from being an EMT-B to an EMT-I.   An EMT-I can administer drugs with permission from the Medical Director. This is referred to as the “mother may I” directive.

 

4)  EMSAC has 1.5 million dollars in dedicated grants for equipment only; 85 thousand dollars for training grants.

 

Phyllis reported the Medical Director, Dr. Koenig was unable to attend the meeting.  However, he is working on the medical protocols for EBCA.  These protocols must be approved by the state.  He asks that if you have any questions on comments written on returned run sheets feel free to call him.  He does not want anyone to get upset over any comments. He states that the documentation is getting better. Dr. Koenig will be working with the new sheriff concerning “turn backs”.

 

Phyllis stated EBCA is running short on oxygen tanks and informed the Advisory Board some EMTs have purchased their own oxygen tanks.  A person can go to NORCO, tell them you are with EBCA, and the cost is $52.00 filled and when empty the oxygen tank could be traded out in Idaho City for a full one.  Placerville has recently purchased 8 oxygen tanks.  A purchase of oxygen tanks is up to each unit; it is not required.

 

Phyllis obtained medical guidelines at the EMSAC meeting and gave a copy to each Advisory Board member and one will be mailed to Dr. Koenig. 

 

IDAHO CITY :   Cyndi Watson stated that the Boise County Sheriff’s office gave the ambulance unit a signed “1/2 sheet” for the EBCA. This half sheet contains a typed message regarding the county not being held liable. The patient was gone.  Discussion ensued regarding the need to work with the new Boise County Sheriff on policies and protocol.

 

MORES CREEK AMBULANCE:   John McCarthy reported that Mores Creek Ambulance personnel would like to work as a team to eliminate the rumors which have been circulating.

Responding to the letter received regarding the lack of protective gear being worn, Beth Chandler states that the Mores Creek Unit staff will wear protect gear whenever there are bodily fluids present.

 

LOWMAN:  Betty Myers states that Lowman would like to proceed in procuring 6 “rims” for the tires they own.  These would fit the new ambulance.  Discussion regarding gurneys ensued. 

The light bar on Lowman’s rescue truck is being serviced.  Betty will report on the prognosis at the next meeting.

 

The phone line at Lowman Ambulance will be a shared line with the Lowman Fire Department.  With the new HIPPA laws requiring a dedicated fax line this could be a problem.  This issue needs to be resolved soon. They will be laying the new phone lines in about 2 weeks.   Chairman Jackson gives Lowman Ambulance permission for a 2nd phone line to accommodate HIPPA laws.

 

Tom Brackensick has returned to Lowman Ambulance as an EMT and driver.  Because of time restraints (license lapsing) Lowman paid $203.00 for re-certification for him.  The bill will be to the County so Lowman Ambulance can be reimbursed.

 

LIFE FLIGHT:  Tom presented contract copies for EBCA trainees to do clinical time at St. Als. This will enable St. Als to reserve a block of time.  Phyllis Weiss made a motion that the St. Als contract be sent to the Board of County Commissioners, seconded by Cyndi Watson. All Ayes.

 

In reference to a brochure from Life Flight received by Placerville Ambulance; Tom Wright explained that the brochure simply reiterates the “rotation” call for air ambulances.  Boise County, however has opted out of the rotation so all Ambulance units need to remember that if you want Life Flight you must specify “SAINT AL’S LIFE FLIGHT” to State Com.  Otherwise the rotation will apply. A motion was made by Phyllis Weiss of  Placerville Ambulance to write a letter to State Com. stating that St. Al’s Life Flight will always be the first choice for call out for East Boise County Ambulances, seconded by John McCarthy. All ayes.

 

Tom Wright stated that their Ride a Long program is still in operation.  This is for EMT’s only.   

 

DISASTER SERVICES:   Fawn Carey reported that Boise County will receive about 40 thousand dollars from the Weapons of Mass Destruction Grant.  There is a new 5 million dollar grant available also.  Advisory board members were asked to go back to their previous grant request and create another list.  This grant would cover epi-pens, bandages, radios etc.  Fawn requested a prioritized list from each ambulance unit and Fire Department, lists should be faxed to the Boise County fax at 392-4473. BDS has also approved a 24 thousand dollar grant for a mutual aid agreement between Boise County, Valley County and Gem County.  Fawn has requested EBCA to write the grant for the ambulance. Fawn needs to know by the August meeting if EBCA can accomplish this task. 

 

OPEN TO THE FLOOR:   Each person in attendance at the meeting was offered the opportunity to discuss issues.  Lengthy discussion regarding prior personal accusations and the need for conflict resolution followed and involved most of the people in attendance.  Jan Peterson, Mores Creek Ambulance Unit offered her service to come up with options for mediation.  Jan shared her work and phone numbers with the EBCA members.  

Fawn made a motion to establish a task force to address conflict within the EBCA units;  Cyndi Watson seconded, All ayes, motion carried.

 

Betty made a motion to adjourn the meeting at 8:05pm, seconded by Tom.  All ayes.  Motion carried.
