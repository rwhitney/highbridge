EAST BOISE COUNTY AMBULANCE ADVISORY BOARD MINUTES

Boise County Chambers

December 4 2003

 

 

The East Boise County Ambulance Advisory Board met in Regular Session on Thursday

December 4th, 2003 at 6:00 p.m. in the Boise County Commissioner’s Chambers in

Idaho City. Present: Chairman Roger Jackson Phyllis Weiss of Placerville Ambulance,

Randy Smith (alternate) of Idaho City Ambulance, Betty Myers of Lowman Ambulance,

John McCarthy of Mores Creek Ambulance, Tom Wright of St Alphonsus Life Flight

 Fawn Carey of Disaster Services and Deputy Clerk Jill Browning. Also present; see attached sign in sheet.

 

CALL TO ORDER:

Chairman Roger Jackson called the meeting to order at 6:00 p.m.

 

AMENDMENTS TO AGENDA: Phyllis Weiss asked to add Epi Pen usage to the agenda.

 

MINUTES: Deputy Clerk Browning’s full minutes of the regular meeting of November 6,003 were presented for approval. Betty Myers made a motion to approve the

November 6, 2003 minutes; seconded by Phyllis Weiss All ayes. Motion carried.

 

ISSUE TRACKING:

S.O.P.s

Deputy Clerk Browning reported that the new East Boise County Ambulance District SOPs have been distributed to each ambulance unit. There are some outline errors but due to upcoming unit meetings those errors will be corrected after the initial

distribution.  Betty Myers of the Lowman Unit requested 5 more sets.

 

AIR ST. LUKE’S CORRESPONDENCE:

A letter written by Air St. Luke’s staff was brought to the member’s attention by Deputy

Clerk Browning. The correspondence praised the Mores Creek Ambulance Unit for their

“outstanding competence and professionalism.”

 

ACCOUNTS PAYABLE COORDINATOR REPLACEMENT:

Advisory Board Members returned to this meeting with no new nominations for

Accounts Payable Coordinator. Phyllis Weiss nominated Christy Severns. All members

were in agreement. Christy Sevens will assume the duties in January 2004.

 

AMBULANCE RESPONSE:

Commissioner Jackson received a letter from the wife of a patient who was concerned over ambulance response time. Randy Smith and Deputy Clerk Browning will respond in writing to the concerns.

 

 

OUT OF SERVICE AMBULANCE RESPONSE:

Sheriff Bodie readdressed the issue of “officially out of service” ambulance units being

able to respond to emergencies. After lengthy discussion the majority of the Advisory

Board Members felt that the “units” should be able to communicate between themselves

and respond appropriately to provide the quickest emergency care. There remained some questions regarding State Communications need to “tone out” the transporting unit. Commissioner Jackson will speak with the Boise County Prosecutor and will report at January’s Advisory Board meeting

 

RESCHEDULING OF JANUARY 2004 MEETING:

The January EBCAD meeting would fall on Thursday, January 1st.   The Advisory Board

members were in agreement to reschedule this meeting to January 8th 2003 to avoid the

Holiday.

 

COMMISSIONER’S LETTER TO EMS:

Commissioner Jackson was unclear whether the letter had been sent. He will look into it and report to Phyllis Weiss.

 

MISCELLANEOUS AND CORRESPONDENCE:

 

COMPUTERIZED PCR REPORTS:

Randy Smith from the Idaho City Unit reported that one copy of the program for computerized PCR reports is provided free. There are many issues to address including the price of additional copies. The Boise County Technology Committee will be looking into the various issues.

 

BACK COUNTRY RESCUE-EXTRICATION:

Sheriff Bodie distributed a draft document.  He asked that the members simply look it

over and consider training and other issues. The subject was tabled until the January

2004  meeting. Sheriff Bodie asked that discussion of a “Mobilization Fee be placed on the January agenda

 

AMBULANCE RIDE TICKET:

Deputy Clerk Browning reported that she has heard from a few Boise County residents who were surprised to hear that their EBCAD Ride Tickets had expired. Jill will begin sending the current Ride Ticket Members a reminder one month before expiration of their ticket. All Advisory Board Members were in agreement.

 

RADIO ETIQUETTE:

Joe Weiss from Placerville Ambulance asked all members to remind their Unit Members to adhere to proper radio etiquette. There was a recent incident where two members were speaking on a “secure” channel and inappropriate conversation was overheard.

 

EPI-PENS:

Phyllis Weiss reported that the epi-pens have arrived and can be distributed to the individual units. They are not to be put onto the ambulances yet. Advisory Board members were directed to see Phyllis for the appropriate paperwork. When epi-pens are approved, they will be kept under lock in the ambulance as well as the ambulance buildings. Mores Creek Ambulance will contact Dr. Koenig to set up training times. Tom Wright from St. Alphonsus Life Flight will work with Dr. Koenig toward the EBCAD application for on-line Medical Director.

Tom McGrane from Air St. Lukes will work with Deputy Clerk Browning to obtain an appropriate charge and code for billing of epi-pen use.

INDIVIDUAL UNITS:

IDAHO CITY:

Randy Smith requested that all members start looking at the future of EBCAD. Where should it be in 5 to 7 years?

Randy Smith states that lie will be requesting that the County repair the door bell and the light ballast at the ambulance building.

MORES CREEK:

1. John McCarthy introduced Jan Peterson who will be their Advisory Board member starting January 2004.

2. John reported that the ambulance needs new automated Blood Pressure equipment.

3. John explained that Mores Creek had to buy two new alternators for the ambulance to ensure they could be in service.

4. John requested up to date budget information which would allow them to work on the 2004 budget. Deputy Clerk Browning will mail that to the members immediately.

 

LOWMAN:

1. Betty Myers from Lowman requests that the patient information be removed from the Lowman lap tops.

2. The old ambulance which was sold to Crouch Ambulance will be picked up any day.

3. Lowman’s new ambulance building is still without drywall and heat. The snow is sliding off the roof in front of the entrance door. This problem is being solved by providing a key pad to open the garage doors during heavy snow times.

4. Betty reported that the Lowman Ambulance staff was treated very poorly by St. Luke’s Emergency Department. Betty and Mike McGrane (from St. Lukes) are following up on the complaint.

5. Betty reported that the oxygen leak in the new ambulance has been repaired.

 

 

LIFE FLIGHT:

Tom Wright reported that the scrubs for each ambulance unit are in. Phyllis showed the scrubs to the advisory board.

DISASTER SERVICES:

Fawn Carey asked to be excused from the EBCAD Advisory Board Membership. Fawn’s membership was terminated by Commissioner Jackson.

 

PLACERVILLE:

1. Phyllis Weiss reported that she has purchased 28 oxygen tanks using the money from the Homeland Security Grant. All units except Idaho City should exchange the tanks at Norco when appropriate.

2. Idaho City Ambulance building now has two key pad locks.

3. Phyllis reminded each unit how important it is to be in service over the Holidays.

 

OPEN TO THE FlOOR:

Karen Acker from Mores Creek Ambulance requested EBCAD inventory tags. Deputy Clerk Browning will investigate and report at January meeting.

Montie Page would like to request that ICRMP provide a drivers class for the EBCA

Ambulance Drivers. Deputy Clerk Browning will check with Rora Canody, County

Clerk.

Mores Creek Ambulance staff wished to have risk management advise regarding the present training provided to the rescue (extrication) volunteers.

Having no further business, Phyllis made a motion to adjourn at 8:45pm; seconded by Betty Myers. All Ayes. Motion carried.

                                                Approved this 8th day of January 2003
