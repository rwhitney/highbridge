EAST BOISE COUNTY AMBULANCE ADVISORY BOARD MINUTES

Boise County Chambers

March 6, 2003

 

The East Boise County Ambulance Advisory Board met in Regular Session on Thursday March 6, 2003, at 6:00 p.m. in the Boise County Chambers in Idaho City.  Present: Chairman Roger Jackson, Phyllis Weiss of Placerville Ambulance, Cyndi Watson of Idaho City Ambulance, Betty Myers of Lowman Ambulance, John McCarthy of Mores Creek Ambulance, Tom Wright of Life Flight, Fawn Carey, Disaster Coordinator and Deputy Clerk Jill Browning. Others present were: Joe Weiss, of Placerville Ambulance, and Randy Smith of Idaho City Ambulance, Karen and Terry Acker, Mores Creek Ambulance, Montie Page, EMT Class Coordinator, Commissioner Lawson and his wife, Jan Felty of Lowman and Dr. Michael Koenig.

 

CALL TO ORDER: Chairman Roger Jackson called the meeting to order at 6:00 p.m.

 

AMENDMENTS TO AGENDA: There were no amendments to the agenda.

 

MINUTES: Deputy Clerk Browning presented the full minutes of the regular meeting of January 2nd 2003 for approval. Phyllis Weiss noted that the information given by Chairman Jackson regarding the pay off date for Mores Creek Ambulance building was incorrect.  There are 2 more years left on the lease, not 1 year as previously stated.   Betty Myers made a motion to accept the minutes from January 2nd , Phyllis Weiss seconded, all ayes, motion carried.

 

 

EXECUTIVE SESSION:  6:15 PM, Fawn Carey moved to go to Executive Session pursuant to I.C. § 67-2345(1)(b). Seconded by Phyllis Weiss.

7:05 PM Fawn Carey moved to call the EBCA Advisory Board back into regular session. Seconded by Phyllis Weiss.

 

ISSUE TRACKING: Phyllis Weiss of Placerville Ambulance introduced Dr. Michael Koenig from Garden Valley Health Center as the new Medical Director for EBCA.  Dr. Koenig noted that he would be addressing some issues immediately.  His major concerns are as follows: 1.  Clarifying the dispatch of East Boise County Ambulance.  2. Providing medical input into the creation of the Standard Operating Procedures. 3. Reviewing ambulance run-sheets for medical guidance.

Deputy Clerk Browning noted that Dr. Koenig would receive an official letter within the week regarding his new position as Medical Director.

 

AMBULANCE STORED AT H. GROSSS HOME: The ambulance is now located in Gardena. It has been inspected and is deemed non-useful for patient transportation. It will be used by the Disaster Coordinator as a mobile command unit.

 

COMPUTERS ON BOARD AMBULANCES: Phyllis Weiss, Placerville Ambulance reports that the actual installation (wiring) of the computers into the ambulances will take approximately 2 hours each.  Appointments are being made.  Vicky Turner from Mapping will be making arrangements to install Geo-Media on one system.  Joe Weiss is still researching cost.

AMBULANCE FUEL: Joe Weiss, Placerville Ambulance, voiced concerns about the fuel quality at the Idaho City Sinclair station.  He is no longer buying fuel in Idaho City and reports that he is using the Flying J credit card for the Placerville Ambulance fuel.  He will be checking with Treasea Long regarding a Chevron credit card.

 

EBCA ADVISORY BOARD VICE-CHAIRMAN; Phyllis Weiss from Placerville Ambulance made a motion that Cyndi Watson  serve as the EBCA Advisory Committee Vice Chairman.  John McCarthy seconded the motion. All ayes. Motion carried.

 

LIFE FLIGHT MEMBERSHIPS: Deputy Clerk Jill Browning asked for a final count from each unit for Life Flight memberships.    The total count was 74.  Jill will forward this information to Life Flight and will report back to the Advisory members with a price per membership.  Four checks (one from each unit) will then be sent to Life Flight.

 

EMT CONTRACT REVISIONS: Deputy Clerk Browning reported that the EMT contracts were approved as revised.  Copies were distributed to two of the unit members.

 

STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURES (SOPS): Deputy Clerk Browning reported that Rora Canody, Boise County Clerk is working on a new draft and will call a meeting of Advisory Board Members when she has finished. Dr. Koenig asked to be included.

 

INDIVIDUAL UNITS:

 

PLACERVILLE: Phyllis Weiss voiced concerns that the extrication staffdress and behave professionally. Discussion ensued and the matter was tabled.

 

Phyllis reports that pepper spray carried on ambulances may be out dated. She distributed new supplies. Phyllis passed out disposable booties to the other units for staff to wear over their shoes when entering homes or other clean areas. These booties are especially important when working with blood or other body fluids.

 

The Placerville Ambulance Unit states that it is time for Placerville to locate a building site for their ambulance.

 

Ownership of the Idaho City Ambulance building was discussed.  Chairman Jackson provided historical information and the discussion was tabled.

 

Phyllis brought product information for a Back Raft.  Dr. Koenig feels this would be beneficial in certain situations. (Information attached).

 

IDAHO CITY: Cyndi Watson of Idaho City Ambulance requested inventory tags with EBCA identification.  Deputy Clerk Browning reported that she had been told by the Clerks Office that all tags now identify equipment as belonging to Boise County.  Chairman Roger Jackson was asked to look into this.

 

Cyndi reminded Deputy Clerk Browning that the Advisory Board members would like a monthly accounting of accounts receivable.

 

MORES CREEK: John McCarthy asked for procedural verification to obtain an I.D. card.  The cards may be obtained at the sheriffs office for a fee (paid by the EBCA member) of $5.00.  I.D. cards are optional.

 

 

LIFE FLIGHT: Tom Wright reports that Life Flight is still doing a preceptorship.  The ride along program is still in the works.   He clarifies that if EBCA has a problem with Life Flight, and you are not getting results you may call him.  He should not be the first person to contact but should get involved if needed

 

OPEN TO THE FLOOR: Joe Weiss from Placerville will be purchasing new radios for EBCA units. A total of 40 new radios will be ordered, plus one mobile for the new Lowman Ambulance which will be purchased.  The cost of the radios will be covered by a grant from EMS.

         

Randy Smith from Idaho City Ambulance states that he is applying for a grant from Idaho Grant Makers to purchase updated extrication equipment.  The present equipment is 20 years old.

 

Motion was made by Chairman Jackson at 9 PM to go to Executive Session pursuant to I.C. § 67-2345(1)(b). Seconded by John McCarthy.

9:30 PM Fawn Carey moved to call the EBCA Advisory Board back into regular session. Seconded by Phyllis Weiss.

 

Having no further business, Chairman Jackson adjourned the regular meeting at 9:30  p.m.
