EAST BOISE COUNTY AMBULANCE ADVISORY BOARD MINUTES

Boise County Chambers

June 5th  2003

 

 

 

The East Boise County Ambulance Advisory Board met in Regular Session on Thursday June 5th, 2003 at 6:00 p.m. in the Boise County Commissioner’s Chambers in Idaho City.  Present: Chairman Roger Jackson (6:10 pm), Phyllis Weiss of Placerville Ambulance, Cyndi Watson of Idaho City Ambulance, Betty Myers of Lowman Ambulance, Beth Chandler of Mores Creek Ambulance, Tom Wright of Life Flight, and Deputy Clerk Jill Browning.

Others present were: Rora Canody, Boise County Clerk,  Joe Weiss of Placerville Ambulance, Randy and D’Anna Smith of Idaho City Ambulance, Michael McGraue of Air St. Lukes, Christie Severns of Placerville Ambulance and Boise County Prosecuter Theresa Gardunia.

 

CALL TO ORDER:                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Vice-Chairman Cindy Watson called the meeting to order at 6:00 p.m.

 

AMENDMENTS TO AGENDA: There were no amendments to the agenda.

 

MINUTES: Deputy Clerk Browning presented the full minutes of the regular meeting of May 1st 2003 for approval. Phyllis Weiss made a motion that the May minutes be accepted. Betty Myers seconded. All Ayes.

 

 

 

ISSUE TRACKING:

 

ADA COUNTY-HORSESHOE BEND CORRESPONDENCE:   Rora Canody reports that Boise County will not sign any agreement which would hold Boise County liable for bills. Rora will provide the Advisory Board with a copy of the letter of response when written.  There is no mutual aid agreement with Horseshoe Bend.

 

STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURES: Deputy Clerk Browning asked for one member from each unit to work with her on finalizing the SOP’s for EBCA. A meeting date of June 25th, 2003 at 10 am was set.

 

AMBULANCE COMPUTER UPDATE:  Joe Weiss reports that the EBCA computers are all programmed but when they started the training the 90 day contract had expired.  They are presently shut down and Joe is working with Vicky Turner to solve this problem.

 

EXPLANATION OF VOLUNTEER VS EMPLOYEE STATUS: At the May 1st EBCA meeting Joe Weiss asked for a definition from the Prosecuting attorney from Boise County.  See attached “Advise No.W-0112” from Theresa Gardunia.

 

 

 

EXECUTIVE SESSION:  6:30 pm, Phyllis Weiss  moved to go to Executive Session pursuant to I.C. § 67-2345(1)(b). Seconded by Tom Wright.

 

At 7:00 pm Beth Chandler moved to call the EBCA Advisory Board back into regular session. Seconded by Cindy Watson.

 

                     Phyllis Weiss of Placerville Ambulance  made a motion to address the Boise County Board of Commissioners, recommending drug testing for Ambulance Volunteers. The motion was seconded by Betty Myers.

 

INDIVIDUAL UNITS:

 

IDAHO CITY AMBULANCE: Cyndi Watson reports that June 15th is the Idaho City Arts and Crafts Fair.  IC Ambulance will be conducting a fund raiser.  

 

Cyndi Watson had questioned the EBCA Advisory Board regarding minors on the ambulance.  Can they drive or be on the extrication team if their parents do not sign the waiver? Chairman Jackson will speak with Dave Sasser from ICRMP.

 

Cyndi Watson reports a situation in Idaho City where a 19 year old Ambulance Driver was not allowed into a PUB to assist the EMT who was caring for a patient with chest pain.  The driver is often required to assist the EMT by bringing supplies or equipment from the ambulance to the victim.  Commissioner Jackson states that it is up to the owner of the establishment whether or not to allow a minor to enter for any reason.

 

PLACERVILLE AMBULANCE:  Phyllis Weiss reports that the EBCA needs more oxygen tanks.  There is money in the budget and Phyllis made a motion to buy 10 more tanks to be distributed within EBCA units.  Betty  Myers 2nd the motion, all ayes.  Commissioner Jackson will take the request to the commissioners.

 

In order to avoid any future confusion regarding instructors fees, Phyllis would like to set the following charges for class instruction:

 

For instructors teaching CPR:

 

CLASS COSTS ARE PER STUDENT

 

            Initial CPR instruction, 8 hours class time:  $25.00

            CPR Recertification, 4 hours class time:                 $15.00

 

EBCA PROVIDES MASKS

THE INSTRUCTOR BUYS THE CPR CARDS AT A COST OF $5.00

 

Phyllis Weiss made a motion that these charges be adopted for all CPR Instructors. Seconded by Betty Myers, All ayes, Motion carried.

 

Phyllis made a motion to appoint Betty Myers of Lowman Ambulance to susseed  D’Anna Smith.  She reports that Betty Myers will been working on a simplified Infection Control policy for EBCA.  Motion seconded by Beth Chandler, All ayes, Motion carried.

 

LOWMAN AMBULANCE: Betty Myers reports that they have extra wheels and studded tires, but they will not fit on the new ambulance.  They need to purchase new wheels.  They hope to sell the other rims to Crouch Ambulance who bought the old ambulance.

Betty also states that they need some service on their extrication truck.  The right side of their light bar is not working.

 

Penny Myers is the new secretary for Lowman Ambulance.

 

MORES CREEK; Beth Chandler reports that supplies personally purchased by Ambulance members have been used for patients .  They would like to be reimbursed for these supplies.  After a short discussion the EBCA Advisory Board all agreed that the supplies would be replaced.

 

 

LIFE FLIGHT:  Tom Wright reports that the “Ride Along” program has started.        

He will keep the Advisory Board apprised of the progress so they may apply for the program. 

 

 

OPEN TO THE FLOOR:

 

AIR ST. LUKES:  Mike McGraue reports that St. Lukes has solved the problem of sharing patient information.  If anyone has difficulty obtaining needed information let him know.

 

Joe Weiss of Placerville Ambulance states that the Mores Creek Ambulance needs Air Conditioning service.

 

Phyllis Weiss, Placerville Ambulance made a motion to adjourn. Seconded by Tom Wright of Life Flight. All ayes.  Meeting adjourned at 8:30 pm..

 

 

                                                            Approved this 10th day of July 2003
