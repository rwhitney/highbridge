EAST BOISE COUNTY AMBULANCE ADVISORY BOARD MINUTES

Boise County Chambers

August 7th 2003

 

The East Boise County Ambulance Advisory Board met in Regular Session on Thursday August 7, 2003 at 6:00 p.m. in the Boise County Commissioner’s Chambers in Idaho City.  Present: Phyllis Weiss of Placerville Ambulance, Cyndi Watson of Idaho City Ambulance, Betty Myers of Lowman Ambulance, Terry Acker of Mores Creek Ambulance (alternate), Fawn Carey Disaster Services, Deputy Clerk Jill Browning and Clerk Rora Canody.    Also Present:  Peggy Blodget; Mores Creek Ambulance,  Wendy Osborne; Idaho City Ambulance, Mike McGrane; Air St. Lukes, Montie Page; Placerville Ambulance, Christie Severens: Placerville Ambulance, Delores Guille; Placerville Ambulance and Joe Weiss; Placerville Ambulance.

 

CALL TO ORDER:                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   In Chairman Roger Jackson’s absence the meeting was called to order at 6:30 p.m. by Rora Canody, Boise County Clerk

 

AMENDMENTS TO AGENDA: Betty Myers requested infection control SOPs be added.

 

MINUTES: Deputy Clerk Browning’s full minutes of the regular meeting of July 10, 2003 were presented for approval.  Phyllis Weiss made a motion to approve the July 10, minutes as presented, seconded by Betty Myers. All ayes.

 

ISSUE TRACKING:

SOP’s:   Betty Myers reported that the infection control SOP’s have been sent out to all EBCA Board members.  Dr. Koenig has submitted a few small changes. It is important to note that baseline medical testing for all EMS staff must be completed or Workman Comp Insurance will not cover a claim. Phyllis Weiss made a motion to accept the Infection Control SOPs, seconded by Terry Acker. Ayes; Acker, Myers, Weiss, Watson.  Abstained: Carey. Motion carried.  

Clerk Canody agreed to forward the Infection Control SOPs to the Prosecuting Attorney on Friday August 8, 2003 for her legal opinion.  The Infection Control SOP’s will be added to the Commissioner’s agenda for the Monday August 18th meeting.

 

EXTRICATION BILLING:  Deputy Clerk Browning reported that rescue (extrication) services are not being adequately reported or billed.  Rescue services must have a separate run sheet, clearly marked as “RESCUE”. 

 

 

MISCELLANEOUS AND CORRESPONDENCE:

 

 

BASIN COMMUNITY HEALTH:  Lynn Kuwahara reported that there have been two instances in the past few months when she had treated a patient at her clinic and needed to hand the patient off directly to ALS staff from Life Flight or Air St. Luke’s. She stated that it was inappropriate for a Nurse Practitioner to release a patient to Basic Life Support staff, who in turn would release the patient to Advanced Life Support staff.  Discussion ensued.  Mike McGrane, Director of Air St. Lukes stated that St. Lukes has a written agreement where the BLS staff picks up the Air St. Lukes ALS staff and transport the staff to the patient and then transports the patient and ALS staff back to the air rendezvous point.  The billing is prepared by St. Lukes and payment is made to the BLS service from St. Lukes. The Advisory Board expressed interest in seeing this agreement. Mike will e-mail the “Under Arrangement Agreement” to Deputy Clerk Browning.

Betty Myers made a motion that an Advisory Board member work with the Basin Community Health Clinic and all Air Ambulance service for an “Under Arrangement Agreement”; seconded by Terry Acker. All ayes. Motion carried.

 

 

.INDIVIDUAL UNITS:

 

PLACERVILLE: Phyllis Weiss reported Placerville’s fund raiser Auction earned about $5,000.00;  some of the money will be spent on signage identifying EMTs residences.

 

IDAHO CITY: 

1. Cyndi Watson stated that the Bureau of Hazardous Materials is giving decontamination training on Friday August 8th, 2003 at the I.C. Ambulance building.

2.  Idaho City Ambulance needs to purchase a Fax machine for the Ambulance Building.  Terry Acker from Mores Creek Ambulance stated their unit just purchased one for approximately $200.00.  Phyllis Weiss made a motion that the EBCA purchase a fax machine for those units who operate from an Ambulance “building”; not to exceed $200.00 seconded by Terry Acker.All Ayes. Motioned carried.

 

MORES CREEK AMBULANCE:  No additional input.

 

LOWMAN: 

1.  Betty Myers stated that the Lowman Ambulance building is under construction.  The projected date for occupancy is October 1, 2003. 

2. Betty reported Lowman’s fund raiser will be on Labor Day.  They hope to combine the new monies with last years to finish the inside walls and counters of their new building.

3.  Betty noted that there were two instances of severe bee stings within the last two weeks where epi-pens would have been useful.

4.  Betty stated there are 2 EMT’s that need to take the EMT test.  The EMT’s have already paid their $20.00 testing fee, however there are no upcoming classes in that price range. Phyllis Weiss made a motion that the additional $50.00 each for testing be paid by EBCA ; seconded by Terry Acker.All ayes. Motion carried.

 

DISASTER SERVICES:

Fawn Carey reports that due to time restraints she will write the “Ambulance Planning Grant” on behalf of the ambulance. 

 

 

OPEN TO THE FLOOR:

Peggy Blodgett, Mores Creek Ambulance asked if the SOP’s would address “a death in a public place”. Betty Myers stated that the new state EMS guidelines address this problem, however they have not been published yet; until the guidelines are published, and can be referred to, Phyllis recommended that every ambulance carry a body bag.  The body can be placed up to the neck in a body bag to control possible blood spill and contamination. The body can be placed in the ambulance with permission from law enforcement. Discussion followed regarding length of time until the Coroner can arrive, shielding the body from the public, releasing EMT’s and Ambulance staff for service etc.  EBCA will request a meeting with the Boise County Coroner and the new Boise County Sheriff to discuss these issues.  Deputy Clerk Browning will go onto the internet and research current state guidelines addressing ambulance response to a DOA.

 

Wendy Osborne, Idaho City Ambulance stated their ambulance has no rope.  Phyllis Weiss recommended that (even though they are not a “rope rescue unit”) each ambulance have at least165 feet of rope on board.  Phyllis will see that rope is provided to Idaho City.

 

Mike McGrane Air St. Luke’s director, reported that Air St. Lukes would like to have an equal chance to service the Boise County area as well as have a seat on the EBCA Board, like Life Flight. Phyllis Weiss made a motion that the Advisory Board recommend to the Board of County Commissioners that Air St. Lukes be given a seat on the EBCA Advisory Board; seconded by Terry Acker, All Ayes. Motion carried

 

Montie Page asked to speak about an incident he was involved with at Arrowrock. Montie has forwarded his concerns in writing to the Commissioners as per protocol.  Further discussion was tabled.

 

Joe Weiss reported that he attended the EBCA budget hearing with the Board of County Commissioners and the budget was tentatively approved. Joe verified that fire stand-by charges are still in effect for EBCA billing. .

 

Betty made a motion to adjourn the meeting at 8:05pm, seconded by Cyndi.  All ayes.  Motion carried.
