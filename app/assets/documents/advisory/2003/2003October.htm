Boise County Chambers

October 2, 2003

 

The East Boise County Ambulance Advisory Board met in Regular Session on Thursday October 2, 2003 at 6:00 p.m. in the Boise County Commissioner’s Chambers in Idaho City.  Present: Chairman Roger Jackson, Bill Smith of Placerville Ambulance, D’Anna Smith of Idaho City Ambulance, Betty Myers of Lowman Ambulance, Terry Acker of Mores Creek Ambulance, Fawn Carey Disaster Services, Michael Koenig MD. and Deputy Clerk Jill Browning.    Also present; see attached sign in sheet.

 

CALL TO ORDER:                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Chairman Roger Jackson called the meeting to order at 6:00 p.m. 

 

AMENDMENTS TO AGENDA: The agenda was approved as presented.

 

MINUTES: Deputy Clerk Browning’s full minutes of the regular meeting of September 4, 2003 were presented for approval.  Fawn Carey made a motion to approve the September 2, 2003 minutes, seconded by Betty Myers. All ayes. Motion carried.

 

ISSUE TRACKING:

 

AUTOMATIC ST. ALPHONSUS LIFE FLIGHT CALL OUT POLICY:

Commissioner Jackson met with the Boise County Board of Commissioners on September 22, 2003. Commissioner Jackson reported that there will be no change to the present Automatic St. Alphonsus Life Flight dispatch policy.  .

 

EBCAD  SOP’s:

Betty Myers reported that the East Boise County Ambulance District Standard Operating Procedures are almost ready to present to the Boise County Board of Commissioners and the Prosecuting Attorney for their final approval.  The final draft will be given to each Commissioner at their meeting on October 7, 2003.  A copy will be given to the Prosecuting Attorney on October 6, 2003. The EBCAD Advisory Board expects to hear from the Commissioners and the Prosecuting Attorney following the Commissioners meeting scheduled on Tuesday, October 14, 2003.

 

ADVISORY BOARD SEAT: 

Commissioner Jackson reported that Mike McGrane’s request to be considered for an EBCAD Advisory Board seat, representing Air St. Lukes, has been tabled until January 2004.  All Advisory Board seats will be reviewed at that time

 

SPRING SHORES LETTER: 

The Advisory Board discussed past concerns when Ada County Paramedics responded to the Spring Shores Marina area.  The primary responder for the Spring Shores Marina area is East Boise County Ambulance, ie: Mores Creek Ambulance and Rescue units.  After discussion among the Board Members, Betty Myers motioned that Sheriff Bodie send a letter to Ada County as a simple reminder that EBCAD is the primary respondent to this area, however; good patient care is always our primary objective;  seconded by Terry Acker, All ayes, Motion carried.

 

 

 

EXECUTIVE SESSION:

Betty Myers moved to go to Executive Session pursuant to I.C. § 67-2345(1)(b); seconded by Tom Wright. All ayes. Motion carried.

 

Fawn Carey moved to come out of executive session per I.C. § 67-2345(1)(b); seconded by D’Anna Smith. All ayes. Motion carried.

 

MORES CREEK AMBULANCE MEETING:   

Mores Creek Ambulance and Rescue Unit will be holding their monthly meeting on Thursday October 16, 2003.  Commissioner Jackson has asked to attend.

 

EBCAD RADIO PURCHASE: 

Bill Smith, from Placerville Ambulance reported that the new radios are expected in approximately 2 weeks.

 

LENGTH OF COMMITMENT FOR EBCAD DRIVERS:  

D’Anna Smith noted that the new SOP’s do not include a length of commitment for Emergency Vehicle drivers.  She stated that since the EMT’s are responsible to pay back Boise County for training and immunizations if they do not “serve” a two year commitment, the drivers should also be held responsible.  D’Anna Smith made a motion that the EBCAD drivers be included with the EMTs in the two year commitment of time; seconded by Betty Myers, All ayes. Motion carried.

 

MISCELLANEOUS AND CORRESPONDENCE;

 

STAND-BY AMBULANCE FEES:

Deputy Clerk Browning stated that over the last year only the basic $40.00 fee had been charged for Ambulance standby for fire or “events”.  As per Resolution #2002-12 (attached) the charges will reflect the following:

Ambulance standby, per event, including driver: $40.00 per hour

Of which $30.00 will be paid to EBCAD fund and $10.00 to the standby ambulance unit upon receipt of payment.

EMT standby with the unit ambulance:               $12.00 per hour per EMT (up to two EMTs). To be paid to the standby ambulance unit upon receipt of payment.   Deputy Clerk Browning noted that she will work with Pat Coleman or Michelle Armfield to facilitate the payments to the individual units.

 

EAST BOISE COUNTY AMBULANCE DISTRICT ADVISORY BOARD VICE CHAIRMAN:   

Cyndi Watson, Vice Chairman of the EBCAD Advisory Board has resigned from the Ambulance District.  Fawn Carey, Disaster Services nominated Tom Wright, St. Al’s Life Flight to be the Vice Chairman of the EBCAD Advisory Board; seconded by Betty Myers. All ayes, Motion carried.  

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

INDIVIDUAL UNITS:

 

PLACERVILLE:  

Bill Smith reported that the Placerville EMTs would like to utilize the Idaho City Ambulance building for an occasional lunch or break time.  Eric Cotton, Idaho City Ambulance President assured Bill that they were welcome but requested that they be respectful, clean up after themselves and knock before entering.  He reminded everyone that the building is used as a temporary residence for some EMTs who are on call.

 

IDAHO CITY:

1.  D’Anna Smith reported on the EMSAC Meeting:  New soft ware titled EMSCAN allows the user to complete Patient Care Reports from their computers.  D’Anna was asked to investigate the software.

2.  D’Anna reminded the Advisory Board Members that EMS provides a program titled “Critical Incident Stress Management (CISM)”.  She reminded the staff that if they notice a crew member exhibiting signs of stress, they should call State Com and they can set up a team to help the staff member deal with stress issues.

 

MORES CREEK:   No input.

 

LOWMAN:

1.  Betty reported that the new Lowman Ambulance building now has a roof in place.

2.  Betty reported that they will pick up their new ambulance October 26th or 27th 2003.  She stated that the tires have been exchanged out and the snow tires will be ready when they are needed.

3.  Betty explained that there has been some confusion regarding the boundary between Idaho City Ambulance and the Lowman Ambulance.  Joe Weiss will be distributing a map soon.  The boundary is at mile post 60 on Highway 21.  If   Idaho City Ambulance is out of service then Lowman Ambulance will go to Edna Creek.

 

LIFE FLIGHT:  

1. Tom Wright reported that he has investigated pricing of “scrubs” which will be provided when appropriate, to the EMTs in the EBCAD Ambulances. He will be advising Phyllis Weiss of the costs.

2. Tom assured the Advisory Board that State Communications is very aware of the Automatic dispatch policy.

 

DISASTER SERVICES:

Fawn Carey reiterated that she is not the contact person for grants.  The contact person is Rora Canody, Boise County Clerk.

 

OPEN TO THE FLOOR:

 

Karen Acker, Mores Creek Ambulance, requested confirmation that Tom Wright from Life Flight share applicable information from the EBCAD Advisory Committee Meetings with all appropriate Air Ambulance Companies. Karen also stated that she feels the Boise County Air Ambulance call rotation should include St. Al’s Life Flight and Air St. Lukes. 

 

Mike McGrane, Air St. Lukes stated that he would like to leave discussion open to                                                                        including Air St. Lukes in the Air Ambulance rotation. Commissioner Jackson asked Deputy Clerk Browning to see if she had statistical information showing the number                                                                  of St. Al’s Life Flight dispatches vs. Air St. Lukes dispatches in Boise County.  Deputy Clerk Browning will investigate. 

 

Fawn Carey, Disaster Services made a motion that EBCAD include Air St. Lukes and Access Air in an Automatic Air Ambulance dispatch rotation. St. Alphonsus Life Flight would remain in the rotation. Seconded by Terry Acker, 3 ayes, 2 nays, motion carried.   Commissioner Jackson will present this to the Boise County Board of Commissioners at their October 7, 2003 meeting.

 

 

 

 

Having no further business, Bill Smith made a motion to adjourn at 8:50pm; seconded by Betty Myers. All Ayes. Motion carried.
