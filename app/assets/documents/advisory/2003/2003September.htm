                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    EAST BOISE COUNTY AMBULANCE ADVISORY BOARD MINUTES

Boise County Chambers

September 4, 2003

 

The East Boise County Ambulance Advisory Board met in Regular Session on Thursday September 4, 2003 at 6:00 p.m. in the Boise County Commissioner’s Chambers in Idaho City.  Present: Chairman Roger Jackson, Phyllis Weiss of Placerville Ambulance, Cyndi Watson of Idaho City Ambulance, Betty Myers of Lowman Ambulance, John McCarthy of Mores Creek Ambulance, Fawn Carey Disaster Services, Deputy Clerk Jill Browning and Clerk Rora Canody.    Also present; see attached sign in sheet.

 

CALL TO ORDER:                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Chairman Roger Jackson called the meeting to order at 6:00 p.m. 

 

AMENDMENTS TO AGENDA: The agenda was approved as presented.

 

MINUTES: Deputy Clerk Browning’s full minutes of the regular meeting of July 10, 2003 were presented for approval.  Fawn Carey made a motion to approve the August  7th 2003 minutes as amended, seconded by Betty Myers. All ayes. Motion carried.

 

Phyllis Weiss moved to go to Executive Session pursuant to I.C. § 67-2345(1)(b);  seconded by Tom Wright. All ayes. Motion carried.

 

Betty Myers moved to come out of executive session per I.C. § 67-2345(1)(b);  seconded by John McCarthy. All ayes. Motion carried.

 

 

ISSUE TRACKING:

St. Alphonsus Life Flight automatic call out policy.

Shanna Monroe, E.M.S. Communication Center Manager, addressed the EBCA Advisory Board.  Shanna stated that all ambulances dispatched by State Communications, except for East Boise County Ambulance, adhere to a rotation of all three air ambulance companies.  She agrees that EBCA’s  automatic dispatch policy is excellent, however she would like for the EBCA to adhere to a rotation to include Air Saint Lukes and Access Air in addition to Saint Alphonsus Life Flight.  After discussion, Phyllis Weiss made a motion that we do not use the rotation.  EBCA’s  policy of initially dispatching only to Saint Alphonsus Life Flight, and secondarily to Air Saint Lukes should remain in effect. Motion seconded by Cyndi Watson.  All Ayes. Motion passed.  Tom Wright reminded the committee that the term “Life Flight” has become generic for all Air Ambulances.  St. Al’s Life Flight should be requested as “St. Alphonsus Life Flight”.  Any problems or concerns regarding State Communications should be in writing and addressed to Shanna Monroe.

 

S.O.P’s:  Discussion of the SOP’s was tabled, awaiting input from the ambulance unit members.  Written recommendations must be given to the ambulance unit presidents by September 19th 2003. The SOP Committee will meet on September 23, 2003.  The final draft will be to the advisory board by October 2nd 2003.   

 

 

ADVISORY BOARD SEAT: Mike McGrane has asked to be considered for a seat on the EBCA Advisory Board representing Air St. Lukes. Mike assured the Board that he will continue to attend and participate in the EBCA monthly meetings regardless of the Commissioner’s decision.   Clerk Canody will present the request at the next Boise County Commissioners Meeting.

 

BOISE COUNTY SHERIFF ISSUES:  Sheriff Drew Bodie addressed the Advisory Board to solicit any comments or suggestions regarding the working relationship between the Sheriff’s Department and the EBCAD.  Sheriff Bodie stated that his goal is for all Boise County emergency services to work together. He noted that he is willing to meet with all services to help solve problems.

Montie Page reported that he is still having problems with law enforcement personnel canceling ambulance response. Sheriff Bodie asked that all such problems be reported to him immediately so that he and Dr. Michael Koenig may can address the concerns.   

Randy Smith suggested that all police be trained as first responders.  Phyllis Weiss suggests that CPR masks and gloves be carried in each patrol car and that dispatch follow protocol . An ambulance should be dispatched immediately following a call to the Fire Department. 

Sheriff Bodie stated that he is working on debriefing officers. He asked that the ambulance staff remember “there is only one dispatcher for all calls.”  They can be very busy.  Sheriff Bodie stated that he is available to attend any ambulance unit meetings.  

 

MISCELLANEOUS AND CORRESPONDENCE:

Karen Acker from Mores Creek Ambulance brought a copy of the papers contained in the Mores Creek SOP’s.  She would like to speak to the Commissioners about standardizing the training procedures for the 4 ambulance units. Chairman Jackson approved her request to meet with the Commissioners.   

 

ALTERNATES FOR ADVISORY BOARD MEMBERS:

Betty Myers reminded all Advisory Board Members that they are to appoint only one alternate. She believes that only the designated alternate is approved to vote as part of the EBCA Advisory Board.

Betty also stated that when Chairman Jackson is not available for meetings perhaps the meetings should be rescheduled. After discussion the Board members suggested that when it is known that Chairman Jackson is unavailable, meetings would be postponed until Chairman Jackson is available.

 

Betty Myers suggested that it would be a great asset to have Dr. Michael Koenig as a member of the Advisory Board.  He will consider the request.  Discussion was tabled until October’s meeting.

 

INDIVIDUAL UNITS:

 

TOM WRIGHT, SAINT ALPHONSUS LIFE FLIGHT:

Tom Wright reported that Life Flight is still working toward night vision.  The night vision should be operational within the next 30 days.

Tom asked if any ambulance staff have problems with the automatic dispatch for St. Al’s Life Flight that they call him directly.

 

LOWMAN: 

1.  Betty Myers stated that their new ambulance is due to be delivered October 15th 2003.

2.  Betty noted that they had a helicopter almost land on top of their chain link fence in the dark.  She stated that they may need landing lights.  Betty will ask St. Alphonsus Life Flight pilots for recommendations. 

 

MORES CREEK:

Sheriff Drew Bodie (speaking as a member of the Mores Creek Ambulance staff) stated that the sheriff’s department is now carrying cones with strobe lights. The strobe may help with helicopter landings.

Drew also noted that when responding to a fatality accident the EMT’s job is to cover the body and stand aside.  The site is then a crime scene and should be controlled by the sheriff personnel. Deputy Clerk Browning is in the process of arranging a meeting to include the Sheriff, Coroner and Ambulance staff.  Jan Peterson may be asked to attend as a facilitator.

 

John McCarthy reported that while carrying an ambulance radio on a fire call the radio was broken.  The Wilderness Ranch Fire Department (WRFD) paid to fix the radio.  Last month an EMT/Firefighter was carrying a WRFD radio on an ambulance call and that radio was broken beyond repair.  John asked if EBCA would pay the insurance deductible to replace this radio.  Chairman Jackson stated that the EBCA would pay the insurance deductible to replace the WRFD fire radio. Phyllis Weiss made a motion that the EBCA pay the insurance deductible to replace the Fire Department radio.  Betty seconded. All ayes  

 

PLACERVILLE:

1. Phyllis Weiss requested that the Boise County Commissioners write a letter to Ada County Paramedics stating that they should not be responding to ambulance calls at Spring Shores Marina on Arrowrock road in Boise County.  Phyllis requested that a copy be sent to State Communications to avoid dispatching Ada County paramedics to Arrowrock road.  

2. Phyllis asked the ambulance staff to remember that Central District Health vouchers are not to be signed by the unit President. As the Purchasing agent she needs to sign these vouchers.

3.  Phyllis has spoken with Dr Koenig and reported the following; “A Nurse Practitioner can hand a patient down to a Basic EMT if no medications have been administered.”  Oxygen is not considered a medication.   Erik Cotton would like that information included in the SOP’s.

4.  Phyllis noted that Mike McGrane from Air St. Lukes spoke with Lynn Kuwahara at Basin Community Health Clinic.  Mike stated that there is no safe landing zone closer to the Clinic than the Idaho City Airport.  He feels it is reasonable for the ambulance to pick up the Air Ambulance staff at the airport and transport them to the clinic.  The ambulance can then transport the patient and Air crew back to the airport. 

5.  Phyllis made a motion that the money allotted for meals after an ambulance transport be increased from $8.00 to $10.00 per person.  Seconded by Cyndi Watson; one abstention, all others ayes. Motion carried. 

6.  Phyllis showed the Advisory Board the new signage designating EMT’s homes in Placerville. .

 

DISASTER SERVICES:

Fawn Carey reported that she cannot write the Ambulance Grant due to a conflict of interest. Joe Weiss will write the grant in November.

IDAHO CITY:   No input.

 

OPEN TO THE FLOOR:

1.  Joe Weiss reported that he would like to go before the Boise County Commissioners and request $20 thousand dollars from the EBCA budget for radios.

 

2.  Joe requested an itemization of the twenty thousand dollars budgeted for administrative costs.  Deputy Clerk Browning stated she would forward his request to Clerk Canody.

 

3.  Joe asked for confirmation that the Sheriff’s Department is getting the GIS System at no cost but the Ambulance has to pay. Chairman Jackson stated that he did not believe that to be true.

 

4.  Joe requested an explanation of why Vicky Wilkins in mapping must charge the              Ambulance District for her services.

 

Phyllis made a motion that Joe go to the Boise County Commissioners and ask for twenty thousand dollars worth of radios; seconded by Betty. All ayes. Motion passed.

 

Dr. Michael Koenig stated that he is hoping to have the protocol and training for the epi-pens completed within the next 30 days.

 

Dr. Koenig stated that one of his goals is to form a review board for medical-professional complaints.  This board would consist of 2 or 3 people and would convene as needed. 

 

Wendy Osborne reported that on several occasions she has found the Idaho City Ambulance building unlocked. Discussion ensued regarding the poor condition of the door and the lock.  Idaho City Ambulance stated they will look into replacing either the door or the lock to maintain security. Eric Cotton suggested that the glass inside door be replaced with a solid door and that a combination lock be installed.

 

Cyndi Watson made a motion to adjourn at 8:45pm; seconded by Betty Myers. All Ayes. Motion carried.
