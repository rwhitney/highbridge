EAST BOISE COUNTY AMBULANCE ADVISORY BOARD MINUTES

Boise County Chambers

January 2nd 2003

 

The East Boise County Ambulance Advisory Board met in Regular Session on Thursday January 2nd 2003, at 6:00 p.m. in the Boise County Chambers in Idaho City.  Present: Chairman Roger Jackson, Phyllis Weiss of Placerville Ambulance, Cyndi Watson of Idaho City Ambulance, Betty Myers of Lowman Ambulance, John McCarthy of Mores Creek Ambulance, Tom Wright of Life Flight, and Deputy Clerk Jill Browning. Others present were: Joe Weiss, of Placerville Ambulance, and Randy Smith of Idaho City Ambulance, Montie Page of Placerville Ambulance and Idaho City Ambulance, Cassi Smith of Idaho City Ambulance and D’Anna Smith of Idaho City Ambulance

 

Absent:  Fawn Carey, Disaster Coordinator

 

CALL TO ORDER: Chairman Roger Jackson called the meeting to order at 6:00 p.m.

 

AMENDMENTS TO AMENDED AGENDA: There were no amendments to the agenda.

 

MINUTES: Deputy Clerk Browning presented the full minutes of the regular meeting of December 7th 2002 for approval. Phyllis Weiss moved that the minutes be approved with several amendments.  Cyndi Watson seconded, All ayes. Motion carried.

 

 

AMBULANCE STORED AT H. GROSS HOME: Chairman Roger Jackson reports this ambulance has been moved to the county shop in Gardena. If the Disaster coordinator doesn’t need it, it can be reclaimed by the EBCA.  Chairman Jackson voiced concern that using this ambulance might be subject to budget restrictions: i.e.; vehicle licensing, insurance etc.  Any plans will be tabled until the vehicle condition is determined.

 

MORES CREEK AMBULANCE BUILDING; Chairman Roger Jackson reports that there is only one year left on the contract for the Mores Creek Ambulance Building.  To refinance it would have to be appraised which would cost approximately $400.00 to $500.00 dollars.  The current status of this building should not impact acquiring a new building.

 

COMPUTERS ON BOARD AMBULANCES: Joe Weiss reports that over the Holidays there has been no communication.  Joe will also speak with the Assessor, Linda Blough before the next meeting to insure that Vicki Turner in mapping can help with inputting information into the anticipated GPS mapping system. This discussion was tabled until the February meeting. 

 

RADIO TONES ON RESCUE VEHICLES: Following a short discussion, the East Boise County Ambulance Advisory Board suggested that an entirely new tone be utilized for rescue units in Idaho City, Placerville and Centerville. Phyllis Weiss moved to accept the addition of a new tone to call out the rescue units of Idaho City, Placerville and Centerville.  Cyndi Watson seconded.  All Ayes.  Motion carried.

REPORT FROM DR. KOENIG: Chairman Roger Jackson designated Joe Weiss of Placerville Ambulance to speak to Dr. Koenig about becoming the Medical Director for East Boise County Ambulance.  Joe reports that Dr. Koenig would be pleased to be the medical director and very happy to find that it would include Placerville Ambulance, Idaho City Ambulance, Mores Creek Ambulance and Lowman Ambulance.  Dr. Koenig would like a copy of each “run sheet” from EBCA.  Deputy Clerk Browning will supply these when Dr. Koenig’s position is official.   Tom Wright from LifeFlight has already spoken to Dr. Po Huang and has unofficially informed him that EBAC was looking for a new Director.  Jill will create a letter to be signed by Chairman Jackson , which will be sent to Dr. Huang when Dr. Koenig has officially “signed on”.  Betty Myers made a motion to draft a letter to Dr. Huang and pursue a contract with Dr. Koenig.  Phyllis Weiss seconded the motion. All ayes. Motion carried. Deputy Clerk Browning will notify State EMS when the changes are made. 

 

 

LIFE FLIGHT MEMBERSHIPS: The Corporate memberships (EBCA) are due in April 2003.  After a short discussion the board members decided that the President of each unit would submit a list of EMT’s, Drivers, and rescue workers on their units who wish to purchase a Lift Flight Membership.  Once this is accomplished, the monies will be collected by each unit President and one check will be written to Life Flight for each unit.  Tom Wright stated that 4 checks would be acceptable. The cut off date will be April 1st 2003.

 

 

EMT TRAINING CONTRACT REVISIONS: The revisions initiated at the December meeting of EBCA members was presented to Prosecutor T. Gardunia.  She states that as long as the guidelines for payment or reimbursement are in the Standard Operating Procedures this contract is superfluous.

 The EBCA board noted that depending upon grant  availability and an individual’s ability to pay “up-front” this contract is very important.

 

TOM Wright from Life Flight suggested that additional wording be added to the contract. (copy of new wording attached.)   New draft will be given to Rora Canody Boise County Clerk/Auditor Recorder. Chairman Roger Jackson will present the most recent edition to the Commissioners at their next meeting. 

 

 

MISC. AND CORRESPONDENCE:

 

 

 

 

INDIVIDUAL UNITS;

 

PLACERVILLE:

Phyllis Weiss from Placerville Ambulance reported that she and Joe Weiss attended last month’s EMSAC meeting.  Paramedics are still waiting for the EPI-Pens to be approved for paramedic usage.

 

As the purchasing agent for EBCA Phyllis asked to order the new defibrillator for Lowman Ambulance and the new gurney for Mores Creek Ambulance. Both items were approved for the 2003 budget.  Chairman  Roger Jackson will add these two items to the agenda for the next Commissioner’s meeting.

 

LOWMAN:

Betty Myers from Lowman Ambulance states that Lowman will need a new ambulance before the winter of 2003-2004.  Discussion ensued whether it would be best to trade in the old one, sell it or keep it as a back up.  The new ambulance will be considered for next year’s budget.  The subject was tabled.

 

Betty Myers noted that the EMT class being held in Lowman will begin January 14th 2003 and will include 7 to 10 people.  There will also be a CPR class on January 5th (Sunday) 2003. 

 

Betty reports that there was an instance where Cascade Ambulance refused to respond to a call at Deadwood Outfitters in Valley County.  Betty has spoken to them and now refers this matter to the Boise Co. Commissioners.  Chairman  Jackson will speak with those involved and present the facts to the Boise County Commissioners. 

 

IDAHO CITY:

 

Cyndi Watson reports that Idaho City Ambulance continues to “lose” equipment at St. Alphonsus Hospital.  Tom Wright of Life Flight states he has met twice with the staff at St. Al’s regarding this problem.  He has another meeting with the security department on Friday January 3rd.  Tom will continue to follow this problem.

 

OPEN TO THE FLOOR:

 

Montie Page, EBCA Coordinator, announced that the upcoming EMT class will include 22 to 27 people.  The class will begin this Sunday, January 5th 2003 at the Idaho City High School.  Checks will be made out to East Boise County Ambulance and will be given to Deputy Clerk Browning for deposit into an EMT class account.  

 

D’Anna Smith from Idaho City Ambulance noted that the lease for the Idaho City Ambulance Building expires in January 2003.  She has been told that Boise County is responsible for any repairs needed. Chairman Roger Jackson is aware of the lease agreement and will investigate the repair issue. 

 

D’Anna Smith asked Deputy Clerk Browning to provide her with a copy of the “waiver” of immunizations.  Deputy Clerk Browning was not aware of any “waver” form and will research this request with Treasea Long. 

 

Phyllis Weiss of  Placerville Ambulance made a motion that the EBCA  Advisory Board receives a monthly or quarterly revenue summary along with the expenditure report they already receive. Betty Myers seconded this motion. All ayes. Motion carried. Deputy Clerk Browning will forward this request to Rora Canody, Boise County Clerk.

 

 

Having no further business, Chairman Jackson adjourned the regular meeting at 8:40 p.m.
