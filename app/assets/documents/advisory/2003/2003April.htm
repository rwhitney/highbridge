EAST BOISE COUNTY AMBULANCE ADVISORY BOARD MINUTES

Boise County Chambers

APRIL 18TH 2003

 

The East Boise County Ambulance Advisory Board met in Regular Session on Friday April 18th 2003, at 6:00 p.m. in the Boise County Chambers in Idaho City.  Present: Chairman Roger Jackson, Phyllis Weiss of Placerville Ambulance, Cyndi Watson of Idaho City Ambulance, Betty Myers of Lowman Ambulance, John McCarthy of Mores Creek Ambulance, Tom Wright of Life Flight, Fawn Carey, Disaster Coordinator and Deputy Clerk Jill Browning.

 Others present were: Joe Weiss of Placerville Ambulance, Randy and D’Anna Smith of Idaho City Ambulance, Karen Acker, Mores Creek Ambulance, Jim Bryant Wilderness Ranch and Wendy Osborne.

 

CALL TO ORDER: Chairman Roger Jackson called the meeting to order at 6:00 p.m.

 

AMENDMENTS TO AGENDA: There were no amendments to the agenda.

 

MINUTES: Deputy Clerk Browning presented the full minutes of the regular meeting of March 4th 2003 for approval. Phyllis Weiss noted that the new radios for the ambulances would not be purchased until a grant is approved.  Joe Weiss has received permission to store fuel from Chevron for Placerville Ambulance usage.  Placerville is not purchasing fuel from the Idaho City Sinclair station. Cindy Watson made a motion that the March minutes be accepted as corrected. Phyllis Weiss seconded, all ayes, motion carried.

 

 

EXECUTIVE SESSION:  6:15 PM, Phyllis Weiss moved to go to Executive Session pursuant to I.C. § 67-2345(1)(b). Seconded by Cindy Watson.

6:35PM Betty Myers moved to call the EBCA Advisory Board back into regular session. Seconded by Fawn Carey.

 

John McCarthy from Mores Creek Ambulance motioned that the East Boise County Ambulance District reimburses $110.00 to Idaho City Ambulance for CPR class costs.  Seconded by Betty Myers, five ayes, one member abstained, motion carried.

 

CPR CERTIFICATION AND RE-CERTIFICATION CLASSES: Instructor’s charges for CPR certification and re-certification were discussed. Betty Myers of Lowman Ambulance made a motion that a moratorium is declared on all CPR classes until charges can be researched and put in writing.

 

ISSUE TRACKING: There were no issues.

 

 

TRAINING REIMBURSEMENT CONTRACT: Betty Myers from Lowman Ambulance presented a revised Training Reimbursement Contract to Chairman Jackson. This revised contract was not considered. The existing contract, with a line added for class date and class cost, was given to Betty. Phyllis Weiss of Placerville Ambulance made a motion that anyone not signing this or the previous contract will forfeit the opportunity to test for their EMT certificate under East Boise County Ambulance. Motion seconded by John McCarthy of Mores Creek Ambulance.  Five ayes, one nay entered by Betty Myers of Lowman Ambulance.  Motion passed.  Deputy Clerk Browning will fax a copy of the newer Training Reimbursement Contract to Betty in Lowman. 

 

STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURES: The first half of the East Boise County Ambulance SOPs are near completion.  They will be presented to the advisory board soon.  

 

COMPUTERS ON BOARD AMBULANCES: Vicky Turner (Mapping) has advised Joe Weiss of Placerville Ambulance to present the GIS system to the Boise County Board of Commissioners for purchase. 

 

 

EXTRICATION TEAM MEMBERS:

 Phyllis Weiss of Placerville Ambulance has spoken to members of EMSAC regarding extrication personnel policies. Both EMSAC and Dr. Koenig (EBCA Medical Director) agree that the extrication personnel should be following the same policies as the Fire Department and Ambulance Volunteers.  There is to be no alcohol consumption within 8 hours of a call.   Anyone who has had alcohol within this time should not respond to any emergency call. Discussion was tabled until written policy and procedures are established.  John McCarthy of Mores Creek ambulance and Wilderness Ranch Fire Department will work on these policies. John helped to develop similar policies for Wilderness Ranch Volunteer Fire Department.   

 

LIFE FLIGHT MEMBERSHIPS: The checks for the four ambulance units have been turned in to Deputy Clerk Browning.  She will mail the checks to St. Alphonsus Life Flight immediately.  Family memberships can be purchased on an individual basis by contacting the Life Flight office. 

 

MEDICAL REVIEW: Dr. Koenig was unable to attend.  Phyllis Weiss from Placerville Ambulance reports that Dr. Koenig would like to write the medical protocols for East Boise County Ambulance. Tom Wright from Life Flight cautioned the Board to make sure that protocols written do not increase the County’s liability. 

 

INDIVIDUAL UNITS:

 

PLACERVILLE: Phyllis Weiss of Placerville Ambulance passed out “Sudicon”, an antidote for pepper spray.  This should be carried on each ambulance. 

 

Testing for the new East Boise County EMTs is scheduled for May 10th, 2003.  Phyllis is looking for volunteers to be “patients”.  If the students do not pass their practical test on Saturday May 10th they cannot take their written exams on Monday May 12th. 

 

Phyllis reminded the Advisory Board members that the Race to Robie Creek is this coming weekend.

 

IDAHO CITY: Cyndi Watson voiced concern regarding a copy of a letter from Ada County Board of Commissioners to the Boise County Board of Commissioners. There was significant confusion regarding any impact this information may have on the relationship between Ada County Paramedics and East Boise County Ambulance.  Chairman Jackson will research this and report at the May 1st 2003 meeting. (Letter attached).

Cindy Watson voiced concern that Life Flight was not dispatch for an East Boise County emergency.  Tom Wright from Life Flight reported that State Com. was in error and the problem has been rectified.  Life Flight will be dispatched for East Boise County whenever available.  If they are not available St. Lukes Air will be dispatched.  If both air services are unavailable, Access Air will be dispatched. 

Cyndi Watson requested clarification regarding EMT students responding to emergency calls.  John McCarthy of Mores Creek Ambulance states that students should not be responding to emergency calls unless they are “on duty” as a driver extrication team member or under the direct supervision of an EMT.  Montie Page, training coordinator will be instructed to speak with the volunteers who are in training at this time.  The Advisory Board members will cover this in their individual ambulance unit meetings.

 

MORES CREEK: John McCarthy discussed an incident in Wilderness Ranch where Mores Creek had been out of service but were able to put a “Team” together and responded.  Idaho City Ambulance had responded as requested and was then told to stand down when they were within about a mile of the incident site.  John apologized and assured the board that protocol had not been followed. 

John McCarthy has an extrication class on Saturday April 26th.  He is looking for volunteers to act as victims.  Anyone interested please contact John as soon as possible. 

 

 

LOWMAN:  Betty Myers reports that they received a grant to purchase a RAM.  The delivery has been delayed because the US military has taken possession of all available Rams to be used in the Middle East.  The manufacturer has guaranteed delivery before her extrication class on May 17th 2003.

 

FAWN CAREY: Fawn reports that she will be meeting with Hal Gross regarding fire repeaters.

 

LIFE FLIGHT:  Tom Wright had no additional input.

 

IDAHO CITY AMBULANCE BUILDING LEASE: Chairman Jackson reports that the lease on the Idaho City Ambulance Building will be signed next week.  He reports that the payments will be $6000.00 this year, $3000.00 next year and then $1000.00 per year for the next 20 years.  Maintenance is to be the responsibility of EBCA.

 

 

 

OPEN TO THE FLOOR:

 

 

Joe Weiss from Placerville Ambulance reports that he is applying for an equipment grant.  If he receives this grant he will be buying 40 new radios for the ambulance units.  If there is other equipment needing to be considered for purchase with this grant, speak to Joe.

 

Betty Myers reports that Crouch Ambulance wants to buy Lowman’s old ambulance.  They can reportedly give the County the same amount of money that was promised for trading in the ambulance.   General consensus among board members was favorable.  Chairman Jackson will take this matter to the Commissioners.

 

Phyllis Weiss reports that in December of 2002 a community member requested to train as a volunteer driver for the ambulance district.  That person received the first set of immunizations and PPD from Central District Health.  The person never trained, nor has there been any inquiry.  Boise County was billed and has paid  $97.00 for the injections.  Chairman Jackson will take this matter to the Board of Commissioners.  

 

Joe Weiss of Placerville Ambulance reports that their ambulance is in need of preventative maintenance.  Cost should be approximately $125.00. Chairman Jackson instructed Joe to have this work completed.

 

Randy Smith of Idaho City Ambulance reports that a memo has been sent to all Boise County Dispatchers instructing them “to tone out an ambulance for any vehicle accident regardless of whether injuries have been reported or not.  Once the ambulance has been dispatched, only State Com or EMT’s will cancel (it).  If the accident is a vehicle in the water, over the bank, a head on, people still inside vehicles, or any major damage accident, extrication equipment will also be dispatched.  One fire truck will be dispatched to the accident scene.’’ (See attached memo)   Only State Com or the EMT’s can give an order to “Stand Down”.

 

Jim Bryant from Wilderness Ranch asked if Life Flight and Air St. Lukes were honoring each other’s memberships if one or the other were not available when called.    Tom Wright will check into this and report at the May 1st meeting.
