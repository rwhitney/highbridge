EAST BOISE COUNTY AMBULANCE ADVISORY BOARD MINUTES

Boise County Chambers

March 7, 2002

 

The East Boise County Ambulance Advisory Board met in Regular Session on Thursday, March 7, 2002, at 6:00 p.m. in the Boise County Chambers in Idaho City.  Present: Chairman Roger Jackson, District I Commissioner, Tom Wright of Lifeflight, Hal Gross of Disaster Services, Phyllis Weiss of Placerville Ambulance, Betty Myers of Lowman Ambulance, D’Anna Smith of Idaho City Ambulance and Deputy Clerk Treasea A. Long.  Absent: Beth Chandler of Mores Creek Ambulance.

 

Others Present:  Joe Weiss, Placerville.

 

CALL TO ORDER: Chairman Jackson called the meeting to order at 6:10 p.m.

 

AMENDMENTS TO AGENDA:  The Board added grant information under Miscellaneous.

 

MINUTES: Deputy Clerk Long presented the full minutes of the regular meeting of January 3, 2002, for approval.  Phyllis moved the Board approve the full minutes of the regular meeting of January 3, 2002, as presented; seconded by Betty.  All Ayes.  Motion carried.

 

RESOLUTION #2002-12 FEE SCHEDULE: Clerk Rora A. Canody addressed the Board to discuss the proposed Resolution #2002-12 regarding stand-by fees.  The Board of Commissioners tabled the Resolution until the Advisory Board could make comments.  The County Independent Auditors are concerned that if the units pay the EMT’s or drivers for the stand-by fees, 1099’s will be required.  The Board assured the Clerk that the funds would go into the Units’ accounts.  The Board agreed that Phyllis, as purchasing agent, should review the existing items billed and amounts charged and get back with Treasea to add as an Exhibit “A” to Resolution #2002-12, when adopted by the Commissioners.

 

AUDIT REQUIREMENTS: The Clerk also raised issues regarding the necessity of monthly treasurer reports and Unit budgets for the Independent Auditors.

 

RECEIPT OF EBCA FUNDS: Clerk Canody also reminded the Board that volunteers are not to accept any monies from patients payable to their accounts.

 

DISASTER SERVICES: Hal Gross, Disaster Services Coordinator, brought the Board current on his activities working with the State on upgrading repeaters and reported on events occurring this summer.

 

IDENTIFICATION CARDS:  The Commissioners decided that in response to Captain Steve Bowers’ letter regarding Commissioner authorization and signatures on I.D. cards for EBCA volunteers, unit presidents would be responsible for supplying a current list of members to the Sheriff Department and sign as authorized agent.  The Board discussed the issue at length whether members should be required to get an I.D. card.  Phyllis moved the Board authorize identification cards for active members in good standing; seconded by Hal.  Phyllis, Hal, Tom, Commissioner Jackson, D’Anna ayes, Betty Nay.  Motion carried. The Board further discussed making I.D. cards mandatory but tabled the issue for the Commissioners to decide.

 

EXECUTIVE SESSION I.C. §67-2345(1)(b): Phyllis moved the Board go into executive session for personnel issues; seconded by D’Anna.  All ayes.  Motion carried.  Betty moved the Board come out of executive session; seconded by D’Anna.  All ayes.  Motion carried.

 

MISCELLANEOUS:  Deputy Clerk Long discussed several issues, including no run sheets for February 2002 received, need notations on run sheets to indicate back country rescues, Planning and Zoning notice, and negotiations on Ada County Reimbursement Agreement.  Deputy Long presented a fy2001 Statistics Report and the Expense/Budget report to date.  Each unit received copies of their respective units’ inventory for completion for the Clerk’s Office.

 

ISSUE TRACKING:

Life Flight Membership Lists:  Deputy Long reported that Idaho City is the only unit that has not turned in its LifeFlight Membership monies. 

Northwest Paramedics Association Mutual Aid Agreement:  Deputy Long reported that the Commissioners approved the Mutual Aid Agreement with Northwest Paramedics, and copies were provided.

Minors at the Ambulance Sheds:  the Commissioners have not received information from the Prosecuting Attorney yet.

Class Reimbursement Fees:  Deputy Long reported that Margie Jones has sent letters to past EMT’s who have not fulfilled their two-year training commitment and owe for class fees, books and shots.  Copies provided to each unit.

 

MISCELLANEOUS and CORRESPONDENCE:

Grants:  Deputy Long presented a memo from the Clerk outlining the procedures for applying for grants with County sponsorship.  Additionally, the FY 2003 EMS Grant application was received and turned over to Joe Weiss.

 

The Board reviewed all other correspondence received with no decisions made.

 

INDIVIDUAL UNITS: 

Lowman:  Betty reported that Chairman Foard approved a letter of intent for a Highway Safety Grant to purchase a holmato ramp for the Lowman Unit.

Placerville: Phyllis reminded the group that Hep B shots need to be updated also well as a TB test.  Phyllis urged D’Anna to contact the Idaho City Hall to repair the broken window at the Idaho City Shed and stated that under no circumstances should drivers arrive at a scene prior to an EMT.  Phyllis demonstrated the new child breather bear.  Finally, Phyllis requested that the AT&T long distance account with Cambridge Telephone be cancelled.

Mores Creek: Absent

Idaho City: D’Anna questioned covering childcare costs for a potential EMT while in training.  Deputy Long read the minutes of 10/2/97 during which time the Board approved reimbursing for childcare costs on a 4-month trial basis.  Because the time has elapsed, and childcare costs have not been budgeted, other means to fund the costs would have to be found. 

LifeFlight: Tom reported that there will be no more follow-up letters regarding patients due to privacy issues and stated that although, LifeFlight has new pilots, they are experienced with mountain conditions.

 

OPEN TO THE FLOOR:  Joe Weiss stated that they are having problems with the pumps at Tom’s Service.

 

Having no further business, Hal motioned the regular meeting be adjourned at 9:05 p.m.; seconded by Phyllis.  All ayes.  Motion carried.  The next regular meeting will be held at 6:00 p.m. on Thursday, April 4, 2002.

 

Approved this 4th day of April 2002.
