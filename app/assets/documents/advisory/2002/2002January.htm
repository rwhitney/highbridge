EAST BOISE COUNTY AMBULANCE ADVISORY BOARD MINUTES

Boise County Chambers

January 3, 2002

 

The East Boise County Ambulance Advisory Board met in Regular Session on Thursday, January 3, 2002, at 6:00 p.m. in the Boise County Chambers in Idaho City.  Present: Chairman Roger Jackson, District I Commissioner, Tom Wright of Lifeflight, Beth Chandler of Mores Creek Ambulance, Phyllis Weiss of Placerville Ambulance, Betty Myers of Lowman Ambulance, , D’Anna Smith of Idaho City Ambulance and Deputy Clerk Treasea A. Long.  Absent: Hal Gross of Disaster Services.

 

Others Present:  Joe Weiss, Placerville; Wes Anderson, Nothwest Paramedics.

 

CALL TO ORDER: Chairman Jackson called the meeting to order at 6:07 p.m.

 


AMENDMENTS TO AGENDA: Deputy Long will report on the Grant Applications.  The Board added an Executive Session for personnel, EMT Training Interviews and Arrowrock Reservoir Responses.

 

MINUTES: Deputy Clerk Long presented the full minutes of the regular meeting of November 1, 2001, for approval.  Beth moved the Board approve the full minutes of the regular meeting of November 1, 2001, as presented; seconded by Tom.  All Ayes.  Motion carried.

 

DISASTER SERVICES: Hal Gross, Disaster Services Coordinator, was absent.

 

GRANT APPLICATIONS: Deputy Long stated for the record that any grant applications submitted by the units using the Boise County Federal I.D. Number must have the Board of County Commissioners approval and signature.  The Clerk also needs to have a copy of the grant for budgeting purposes.

 

BCSO TOBOGGAN / SLED: Phyllis outlined a recent call on which the Placerville Ambulance Unit had to retrieve a snowmobiler who went off the groomer trail.  Phyllis requested information on the donated toboggan the Sheriff Office received and whether the EBCA could request the use of it for back country calls.  Deputy Long will contact Sheriff Brown.

 

MINORS AT THE AMBULANCE QUARTERS: Beth requested Board direction on whether minors should be allowed in the ambulance quarters when volunteers are pulling call.  Mores Creek leadership is concerned with the liability issues involved.  The Board discussed the ramifications and options; however, no decision could be made.  The Board deferred any decision to the County Board of Commissioners.

 

MEDICAL DIRECTOR DECISION: Phyllis presented her thoughts and recommendations concerning Dr. Po Huang as the new Medical Director for the EBCA.  The Board reviewed Dr. Huang’s resume and discussed coordinating training events amongst the units to fully utilize him properly.  Phyllis moved the Board approve Dr. Po Huang as the new Medical Director for the East Boise County Ambulance District; seconded by Beth.  All ayes.  Motion carried.

 

 

 

ISSUE TRACKING:

Life Flight Membership Lists:  Deputy Long reported a list of volunteers had been received from Idaho City making the total volunteers wishing to participate in the program at 67.  A LifeFlight Membership program costs $550.00 for 100 people; therefore, the cost for each person will be $8.20 made payable to LifeFlight. 

Northwest Paramedics Association Mutual Aid Agreement:  Wes Anderson and an associate from Northwest Paramedics Association, came before the Board to discuss the Mutual Aid Agreement as received from the Clerk’s Office.  Mr. Anderson presented some changes to the Agreement including changing the rendezvous point to anywhere requested and a flat fee of $50 plus expenditures.  The Board questioned Mr. Anderson on the availability of paramedics, to which he replied that until they are moved to a new location, they are not available 24hrs per day; however, they wish to negotiate an agreement now to provide ALS services and noted that if we don’t collect from the patient, they do not collect from EBCA.  D’Anna moved the Board accept Northwest Paramedics Association as an ALS support service contingent upon a negotiated contract acceptable to the EBCA Advisory Board and the Boise County Board of Commissioners; seconded by Beth.  All ayes.  Motion carried.  Mr. Anderson will forward his proposed changes with a fee schedule to Deputy Long.

 

MISCELLANEOUS and CORRESPONDENCE:

QWest Telephone Service: The Board reviewed the most recent billing to Mores Creek Ambulance for telephone service and agreed that only basic service was necessary.  Phyllis will verify the QWest bill is correct and Beth would make sure only basic service is requested.

 

The Board reviewed all other correspondence received with no decisions made.

 

Chairman Jackson received an inquiry from Boise County Dispatch on who to dispatch in the event of an accident at Arrowrock Dam which may require a repel team.  The Board stated that Dispatch should always send for an EBCA Ambulance Unit and the ADA County Repel Team (SOT).  Chairman Jackson will notify Dispatch.

 

EXECUTIVE SESSION I.C. §67-2345(1)(b): Phyllis moved the Board go into executive session for personnel; seconded by D’Anna.  All ayes.  Motion carried.  Phyllis moved the Board come out of executive session; seconded by Beth.  All ayes.  Motion carried.  D’Anna moved the Board assign the volunteer discussed in executive session, to the Idaho City Ambulance Unit on a four (4) month probationary basis; seconded by Beth.  All ayes.  Motion carried.

 

EMT TRAINING INTERVIEWS:  The Board discussed how to handle potential volunteers who admit during the pre-interview process that they have felonies and/or DUI’s on record.  The State EMS Manual was consulted and it was determined that a volunteer could not be a State certified EMT with felonies on file.  Only a driver’s check with the Idaho Transportation Department would reveal a DUI on record.

 

MISCELLANEOUS :  The Board also reviewed and discussed the current budget status.

 

INDIVIDUAL UNITS: 

Lowman:  Betty reported that the issued fuel cards are not being used in Lowman due to the limited type of fuel they can use and the places which carry said fuel; however, the cards will be in use after the winter months.  Betty stated that the Lowman Unit is ready for a new ambulance next year and an additional $400 (approximately) will need to be budgeted monthly for rent at their new building.  The Board discussed both issues with no decisions made at this time.

 

Placerville: Phyllis demonstrated the new Little Anne mannequin received from the EMS grant.  Phyllis also displayed the hearing device bought at Radio Shack.  Other items of concern were the copier at the Idaho City shed, CPR classes provided by Kim Tkacs, new ice packs and a need for another inventory of Bendix King radios.

Mores Creek: Beth stated that she had nothing new to report other than those items discussed earlier.

Idaho City: D’Anna reported that the Idaho City Unit allows children and pets in the sheds on a case-by-case basis.

LifeFlight: Tom reported no new issues for discussion.

 

OPEN TO THE FLOOR:  Joe Weiss stated that the Idaho City Shed has a malfunctioning light fixture requiring attention, all EMTs that took last year’s training classes must reimburse the County if they are not fulfilling their commitments.  Chairman Jackson recommended the units submit the names, addresses and amounts due to Deputy Long for billing.

 

Having no further business, Chairman Jackson motioned the regular meeting be adjourned at 8:35 p.m.; seconded by Betty.  All ayes.  Motion carried.  The next regular meeting will be held at 6:00 p.m. on Thursday, February 7, 2002.

 

Approved this 7th day of March 2002.
