EAST BOISE COUNTY AMBULANCE ADVISORY BOARD MINUTES

Boise County Chambers

December 5, 2002

 

The East Boise County Ambulance Advisory Board met in Regular Session on Thursday December 5th, 2002, at 6:00 p.m. in the Boise County Chambers in Idaho City.  Present: Commissioner Jackson, Phyllis Weiss of Placerville Ambulance, Cyndi Watson of Idaho City Ambulance, Betty Myers of Lowman Ambulance, John McCarthy of Mores Creek Ambulance, Tom Wright of Life Flight, and Deputy Clerk Jill Browning. Others present were: Joe Weiss, of Placerville Ambulance, and Randy Smith of  Idaho City Ambulance, Montie Page of Placerville Ambulance and Idaho City Ambulance, Karen Acker of Mores Creek Ambulance, Mike McGraue of Air St. Lukes, and Jim Bryant of Mores Creek and Wilderness Ranch volunteer fire department.

 

CALL TO ORDER: Chairman Roger Jackson called the meeting to order at 6:05 p.m.

 

AMENDMENTS TO AMENDED AGENDA: Chairman Roger Jackson will speak for School Superintendent Hampton.

 

MINUTES: Deputy Clerk Browning presented the full minutes of the regular meeting of November 7th, 2002, for approval. Phyllis Weiss moved that the minutes be approved with one amendment.  Betty Myers seconded, All ayes. Motion carried.

 

COMPUTERS ON BOARD AMBULANCES: Kevin Young from Mountain West Communications gave a presentation of Lap Top Computers and GIS versus GPS software.  His suggestions were as follows:

1.      The GIS system being looked over by the Advisory board is “Over- Kill”.  There are less expensive GPS systems which can be expanded by the Boise County Deputy Assessor-Mapping Assistant.  This system will allow the user to add in currently non-mapped roads or trails to be saved and permanently added to the system. High end for a system like this is approximately $200.00.  Depending upon which brand of system is chosen the licensing may include software for all 4 laptops or $200.00 for each laptop.

2.      Kevin is very concerned that the lower cost laptop will not withstand usage in the ambulance and in the field. Kevin estimates that the lower cost laptop would last 12 to 18 months of ambulance usage.  He recommends that by saving money on software a more expensive and thus more durable laptop could be purchased.  Ada County’s laptops include touch screen capabilities.  Tom Wright from Life Flight states that they are presently using a very durable laptop and will work with Joe Weiss to see if we can “piggy back” onto the Ada County contract.  Joe will call Rudy, the purchasing agent for Ada County Paramedics.

 

3.      Kevin Young from Mountain West Communications will get pricing to install the above-mentioned system in the ambulances.  The pricing would include the stands as well as the installation of the actual laptops.

 

Phyllis Weiss made a motion to use the grant money to purchase 4 laptop computers comparable to those being used by LifeFlight, the GPS software demonstrated by Kevin Young, check into piggybacking into the Ada County’s purchasing agreement and have Mountain West Communications install.  Betty Myers seconded. All ayes. Motion carried. .

 

WEAPONS OF MASS DESTRUCTION TRAINING SESSIONS:

Chairman Roger Jackson introduced an invitation from the Idaho Bureau of Hazardous Materials.  They are inviting 5 representatives from Boise County to attend a series of planning sessions and exercises in preparation for weapons of mass destruction event.  The initial meeting will be held on Wednesday, December 18th 2002 from 9:00 a.m. until 3:00 p.m.  One representative is invited from East Boise County Ambulance.  Joe Weiss was selected to attend by those present.  Cyndi Watson made a motion that Joe attend as the EBCA representative.  Betty Myers seconded. All ayes. Motion carried.

 

RE-CERTIFICATION CLASS:

Karen Acker from Mores Creek Ambulance has scheduled a 24-hour refresher course for EMT-B’s.  It will take place on January 10th, 11th, and 12th, 2003 at the Mores Creek Ambulance Building. Michelle Moore will teach this class.  There are 12 people signed up at present.  If there are others who need to attend contact Karen. 

 

RADIOS ON RESCUE VEHICLES:

Joe Weiss from Placerville Ambulance reports that the radios on the rescue trucks (formerly called extrication) will use the present tones for rescue call out. Idaho City will answer to Rescue One. Wilderness Ranch rescue will answer to Rescue Two.   Mountain West will re-program their present radios and pagers with the 2nd tone.  Joe Weiss made a motion that Wilderness Ranch and Idaho City rescue make an appointment with Mountain West Communications to re-program both radios with the second tone as soon as possible.  Seconded by Betty Myers.  All ayes.  Motion carried

 

SCHOOL SUPERINTENDENT VISIT:

Chairman Roger Jackson reported that Mr. Hampton, Idaho City School Superintendent, who was scheduled to speak, would not be in attendance.  Chairman Jackson was to present a plan which would allow a “trade” of school classroom availability for ambulance and EMT standby at events such as football games.

The EBCA board advised Chairman Jackson that classroom space was no longer needed.  The EMT classes were approved to be held above the Idaho City Hall.   Deputy Clerk, Jill Browning will call Mr. Hampton on December 6th 2002 and advise him that the school classrooms will not be needed. 

 

EMT TRAINING CONTRACT REVISIONS:

Phyllis Weiss from Placerville Ambulance presented a copy of the EMT Training Reimbursement Contract approved by the Board of Commissioners in June 2002.  Phyllis asked that several of the references to “Boise County” be changed to read  “East Boise County Ambulance”. She also suggests that the reference to reimbursement specifically include “immunization or associated costs.” Cyndi Watson made a motion to present these changes to the commissioners for approval.  Betty Myers seconded, All ayes, Motion carried.  Commissioner Jackson will try to add this to the Commissioners meeting agenda for December 16th 2002.

 

 

 

EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES ADVISORY COMMITTEE:

Deputy Clerk Browning presented a memo confirming that the EMSAC meeting for December 2002 had been cancelled.

 

AIR St. LUKES:

Mike McGraue introduced himself as representing Air St. Lukes. Mike stated that Air St. Lukes and St. Alphonsus Lifeflight are negotiating some reciprocal agreements within their membership programs.  He will present more information when available. 

 

 

EBCA MEDICAL DIRECTOR:

Phyllis Weiss, Placerville Ambulance raised concern about the availability of the EBCA Medical Director, Po Huang MD.  She sited several occasions when he was unavailable or did not return calls.  Phyllis made a motion to look for a new medical director for the EBCA.  Betty Myers seconded, All ayes, Motion carried. 

Joe Weiss states that he has spoken with Dr. Koenig, a new Physician in the Garden Valley Clinic. Dr. Koenig has offered his services to EBCA and has offered to come teach classes or generally advise any of the EMT’s who might have questions.  The EBCA Advisory Board asked Commissioner Jackson to contact Dr. Koenig and speak to him about a possible director-ship.  Commissioner Jackson has agreed. Deputy Clerk Browning will supply Commissioner Jackson with Dr. Koenig’s phone number and availability.

 

MORES CREEK AMBULANCE BUILDING:

Phyllis Weiss of Placerville Ambulance has asked Commissioner Jackson to research the financing of the Mores Creek Ambulance Building. 

 

LIFE FLIGHT MEMBERSHIPS FOR AMBULANCE VOLUNTEERS:

Randy Smith from Idaho City Ambulance requests information on the “block of Life Flight memberships” to be purchased this year.  He states that last year’s purchase was difficult for Life Flight due to the numerous checks.  Deputy Clerk Browning will check with Life Flight regarding who is eligible and when the money is due.  The advisory board will develop a method of payment so one check for the entire amount will be issued.

 

MISSING AMBULANCE EQUIPMENT:

Betty Myers from Lowman Ambulance reports that equipment is still being lost at St. Alphonsus Hospital.  Tom Wright of Life Flight has been speaking with the Emergency Department Director.  They are working toward better accountability for all ambulance equipment. 

 

EMT CLASS IN LOWMAN:

The Lowman EMT class has been postponed until March 2003.  The instructor has an emergency and will be unavailable.

 

LOWMAN AMBULANCE SIGNAGE:

DeAnna Smith of Idaho City Ambulance will make the signs.

(located at Hal Gross’s home) to the Idaho City Ambulance unit. 

 

AMBULANCE GAS CARDS:

Joe Weiss reported that the gas cards used by Placerville Ambulance do not work in Boise, or they are not using the cards properly. Randy Smith will work with the Placerville unit to resolve this matter.

 

EBCA ACCOUNTING:

Joe Weiss of Placerville Ambulance thanked Deputy Clerk Browning for providing the Advisory Board with the monthly expenditure reports.  He feels the EBCA Advisory Board would benefit by receiving a monthly revenue summary also.  Deputy Clerk Browning will bring this request to Rora Canody, Boise County Clerk/Auditor Recorder. 

 

Having no further business, Chairman Jackson adjourned the regular meeting at 8:50 p.m.
