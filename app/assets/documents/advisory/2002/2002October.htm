EAST BOISE COUNTY AMBULANCE ADVISORY BOARD MINUTES

Boise County Chambers

October 7, 2002

 

The East Boise County Ambulance Advisory Board met in Regular Session on Monday, October 7, 2002, at 6:00 p.m. in the Boise County Chambers in Idaho City.  Present: Chairman Roger Jackson, District I Commissioner; Phyllis Weiss of Placerville Ambulance, D’Anna Smith and Cyndi Watson of Idaho City Ambulance, Betty Myers of Lowman Ambulance, and John McCarthy of Mores Creek Ambulance, Deputy Clerk Jill Browning, and Clerk Rora A. Canody.  Absent: Tom Wright of Life Flight and Hal Gross of Disaster Management.  Others present were: Joe Weiss, of Placerville Ambulance, and Randy Smith of Idaho City Ambulance.

 

CALL TO ORDER: Chairman Roger Jackson called the meeting to order at 6:05 p.m.

 

AMENDMENTS TO AGENDA: The Advisory Board made no amendments to the agenda. 

 

MINUTES: Deputy Clerk Browning presented the full minutes of the regular meeting of September 5, 2002, for approval. Phyllis Weiss moved the Board approve the full minutes of the regular meeting of September 5, 2002, as presented; seconded by Betty Myers  All ayes.  Motion carried.

 

DISASTER SERVICES: Hal Gross, Disaster Service Coordinator was not present for a report.

 

ISSUE TRACKING:

SOP’s: Clerk Canody reported that she would be reviewing the draft SOP’s with the Prosecuting Attorney tomorrow and have more information at the next meeting. Betty Myers from Lowman Ambulance questioned why the Prosecuting Attorney was wasting her time with the SOPs. 

 

Extrication Truck Dispatching Policy: Joe Weiss reported that he has spoken to the Supervisor at State Communications.  State Comm have agreed to dispatch the extrication trucks, once radio numbers are given to State Comm and protocols for dispatching are set.  After discussion by the Advisory Board it was unanimously decided that “extrication trucks “ would be dispatched by State Comm for the following events: 1-head-on accident, 2-vehicle over the embankment, 3-a vehicle in the water, 4-whenever EMT personnel requests the extrication trucks, 5-rollover accidents, 6-high speed vehicle crash, and 7-a motorcycle/vehicle accident.  The Advisory Board agreed to change the title of “extrication truck” to “rescue vehicle”. Phyllis made a motion that Joe and Cyndi coordinate with State Comm the numbering system for the rescue trucks and provide each unit with the updated lists; seconded by John. All ayes.

 

MISCELLANEOUS and CORRESPONDENCE:

 

EBCA State Comm LifeFlight Dispatch Policy: Deputy Clerk Browning presented to the Board the draft policy for review and comment.

 

EMS Provider Form: Deputy Clerk Browning presented the Idaho EMS Provider Form for review by the Board: Deputy Clerk Browning will make copies for all EMT unit files. 

 

P&Z Notice: Deputy Clerk Browning presented the Advisory Board members with a copy of the Notice of Public Hearing regarding a Conditional Use Permit from Don Arrhenius, 3121 Hwy 21, Thorn Creek Area, for an animal crematorium.  The Public Hearing is scheduled for November 21, 2002 at 7:00 p.m. in Idaho City, Commissioner’s Chambers.  Any written comments the Advisory Board may wish to make are due to P&Z by October 10, 2002.

 

EMSAC News Letter: The Advisory Board reviewed the EMSAC newsletter for the month of August 2002.  Phyllis states that both she and Joe attended the EMSAC meeting and relayed the following information: 

1.      EPI-Pens are now approved for ambulance use by the State Board of Medicine. Deputy Clerk Browning will begin researching the billing procedure.

2.      Letters for the Dedicated Grant Funds need to be well written for EMSAC to consider.

3.      Doug Carroll has agreed to read EBCA letters prior to submitting for a grant application.

4.      Next year EBCA should apply for satellite phones.

5.  Other members were urged to attend the next meeting December 11 & 12, 2002.

 

Idaho EMS Association Membership:

Joe Weiss urged volunteer and paid EMT’s to join this organization.  He stated that by organizing it might help to move toward legislative changes. Betty Myers from Lowman Ambulance sited an article titled “Pilot First Responder to Basic”, that she felt if they could involve more people in this and have EBCA pay for their training they could then move into volunteering as EMT’s for EBCA. 

 

Training Opportunities:

Deputy Clerk Browning presented two brochures for upcoming EMT workshops.  Phyllis asked that training and/or grant information be copied and mailed to each unit president as quickly as possible. Deputy Clerk Browning will see that information is mailed as requested. 

 

Vehicle insurance cards: Deputy Clerk Browning presented new County liability insurance cards for each ambulance and rescue truck. The board members from Lowman and Placerville stated that they have not received their new license plates.  Deputy Clerk Browning will follow up on this issue.

 

FY2003 Training Grant Program: Deputy Clerk Browning presented a letter sent to Idaho EMS noting that Boise County Commissioner Chairman Foard has signed both contracts.  Several Advisory Board Members asked that copies of the signed grant applications be mailed to the units.  Deputy Clerk Browning will note this in her procedure book.

 

CDC info:  Phyllis Weiss presented a written report from CDC clarifying the need  for Hepatitis B Vaccination.  Phyllis states that Central District Health suggests that new EMT’s get the “twin” Hepatitis shot.  This covers Hepatitis A and B ,  and consists of a series of 3 shots costing $58.00 each. Current EMT’s, if due for regular blood work, should ask to see if you are still covered for Hepatitis.  If an EMT is exposed, they should get a booster shot that will bring them current.  See information contained on page 3 of the CDC information sheet.

 

INDIVIDUAL UNITS: 

Placerville:

Phyllis inquired if the Mores Creek Ambulance building is listed as collateral for the amount due against the building.  Phyllis reported that there is a high incidence of spider straps being stolen while at the hospital and recommended that each unit keep a close eye on their spider straps.  Phyllis discussed billing for fire standby to a fire in the Placerville area where oxygen was given to three people.  She stated that the Fire Dept. would be billing the property owner and asked if run sheets should be turned in and the three people or the property owner billed for the oxygen?  The Board agreed that the property owner’s homeowners insurance should be billed. The executive session from September 5th Advisory Board Meeting has been put on hold. This issue will be researched.

Joe Weiss informed the Board that one of Placerville’s radios would cost $400.00 to repair and that money had been budgeted in the FY 2003 budget to purchase a new radio. Chairman Jackson instructed Joe to order the new radio and to check to see if there would be any trade in value for the old radio. 

 

Life Flight:  Tom Wright was not present to give a report.

 

Idaho City:  D’Anna Smith reported that the class on Infection Control for Emergency Responders is scheduled in Idaho City at the high school library on October 12th from 8:30 am to 4:30 p.m.  It is important that everyone who signed up attend.  There are 12 signed up, at least 10 must attend.  Law enforcement and Fire Fighters have been invited but she has had no response.  The book “Silent War, Infection Control for Emergency Responders” will be presented.  D’Anna asked that at least one of the Commissioners be present.  Commissioner Jackson stated that it probably would not be possible.

 

Lowman: Betty Myers reported that Lowman has received a $3,600.00 grant for a “RAM”. The EBCA needs to come up with $1.200.00 in additional funds.  The grant money will be available in December or January.  Betty stated that the new Lowman Ambulance/Fire building grant has to readvertise for bids, as the bids all came in too high.  Betty also informed the Board there may be an issue in the spring on the current building housing the Lowman ambulance, she will keep the Board informed

 

Mores Creek: John McCarthy inquired as to the training grant funds and how they will be utilized.  The Board agreed to have Phyllis work with a rep from the Mores Creek unit on the training classes.

 

OPEN TO THE FLOOR: Randy Smith reported the results of the Idaho City unit elections:  Randy Smith-President, Ron Manker-Vice President, Sherril Manker-Secretary and Wendy Vanderburgh-Treasurer. Randy asked if bills for FY 2002 for childcare could be turned in and paid.  Clerk Canody stated unfortunately FY 2002 had been closed out on October 7, 2002.

 

Having no further business, Chairman Jackson adjourned the regular meeting at 7:55pm

 

Approved this 7th day of November 2002.
