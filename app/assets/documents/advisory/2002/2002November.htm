EAST BOISE COUNTY AMBULANCE ADVISORY BOARD MINUTES

Boise County Chambers

November 7th, 2002

 

The East Boise County Ambulance Advisory Board met in Regular Session on Thursday November 7 , 2002, at 6:00 p.m. in the Boise County Chambers in Idaho City.  Present: Phyllis Weiss of Placerville Ambulance, Cyndi Watson of Idaho City Ambulance, Betty Myers of Lowman Ambulance, John McCarthy of Mores Creek Ambulance, Tom Wright of Life Flight, and Deputy Clerk Jill Browning.  Absent:  Hal Gross of Disaster Management.  Others present were: Joe Weiss, of Placerville Ambulance, and Randy Smith of Idaho City Ambulance, Monte Page of Placerville and Idaho City Ambulance, Karen Acker of Mores Creek Ambulance and Jan Felty of Lowman Ambulance.  A phone call was placed at 7PM requesting the presence of Chairman Roger Jackson, District I Commissioner.  Commissioner Jackson arrived at approximately 7:15 PM.

 

CALL TO ORDER: In Chairman Jackson’s absence, Phyllis Weiss of Placerville Ambulance called the meeting to order at 6:30 P.M.

 

AMENDMENTS TO AGENDA: The Advisory Board made no amendment to the agenda.

 

MINUTES: Deputy Clerk Browning presented the full minutes of the regular meeting of October 7th, 2002, for approval.  Betty Myers moved that the minutes be approved with one amendment, Phyllis Weiss seconded All ayes.  Motion carried.

 

DISASTER SERVICES: Hal Gross, Disaster Service Coordinator was not present for a report.

 

FREE AMBULANCE RIDES: Mores Creek Ambulance will be holding a food drive for needy families in their ambulance district.  They would like to offer Ambulance Ride Tickets to the largest contributors of non-perishable food.  After discussion among the board members it was decided that approximately 5 ride tickets could be “presented” providing Mores Creek Ambulance would pay the $25.00 for any tickets redeemed.  Commissioner Jackson approved of this plan.  John McCarthy moved that the board approve this action. Seconded by Betty Myers, One nay. All ayes, Motion carried.

 

ISSUE TRACKING:

 

SOP’s: Phyllis Weiss requests clarification regarding the requested mandatory confidentiality of the SOPs.  Jill Browning presented a plan to meet with one EBCA member and condense the present SOPs into a practical format.   After lengthy discussion Phyllis made a motion that the existing, revised SOPs as well as their copies be returned to the Advisory Board for review.  Seconded by Betty Myers, All ayes, Motion carried.   Jill Browning, Deputy Clerk will present this request to Rora Canody.

 

 

RESCUE Dispatching Policy: Joe Weiss presented a roster of rescue units.  Implementation of the Roster is holding until all portable numbers are received.

 

 

TRAINING GRANTS: Phyllis Weiss of Placerville Ambulance proposed several “creative” ways of maximizing the grant monies for training.  Betty Myers of Lowman stated that she had 4 persons who were committed to taking the EMT class if it were offered in Lowman.  She would lose three of the four people if they had to travel to Placerville or Idaho City for the classes.  Phyllis Weiss confirmed that the people she had enlisted for Placerville Ambulance would not take the class if they had to drive to Lowman.  The EBCA was not able to resolve the matter.  Commissioner Jackson was phoned and his presence requested at the meeting. Upon his arrival Commissioner Jackson listened to all concerns.  Upon reviewing the 2003 budget he directed Betty  and Phyllis to make arrangements for classes in each of their individual areas.  The monies will be available due to the delayed need for rental money  for an Ambulance shed in Lowman. 

 

 

MISCELLANEOUS and CORRESPONDENCE:

 

NEW MAP UPDATES:           Deputy Clerk Browning presented “Quarterly Map Updates” to the Advisory Board.  Phyllis Weiss volunteered to have copies made of the colored maps for all units and will return the originals to the Deputy Clerk.

 

UPDATED EBCA ADVISORY BOARD LIST: Deputy Browning provided each EBCA member with an updated listing of member information.  There were several additional changes needing to be made.  These changes were given to Deputy Clerk Browning.  A revised list will be mailed to members.

 

INDIVIDUAL UNITS;

Placerville:

Phyllis Weiss asked that the monthly Ambulance Expense Activity Sheets be placed in their folders for monthly review.  Deputy Browning will see that they are available.

 

Phyllis Weiss reports that the EPI-Pens are not going to be available for ambulance utilization in the near future as reported last month.  Commissioner Jackson will speak with Senator Fred Kennedy in hopes that he will sponsor this initiative. 

 

Ambulance Computers:  Joe Weiss has checked with Mountain West and feels that the two thousand dollars available for each ambulance computer will cover the cost of the computer and their installation.  He has volunteered to expedite the purchase and installation.

 

All Advisory Board Members and their alternates would like to have notice one week prior to EBCA meetings.  Deputy Browning will see that each member is notified either by e-mail, fax or phone call.

At Commissioner Jackson’s request, he will also be notified in the same manner.

 

Mores Creek: John McCarthy reported that he had money budgeted for 4 snow tires. Commissioner Jackson directed John to purchase Cooper Tires at “Al’s Tire “ in Boise. 

 

John McCarthy of Mores Creek Ambulance requested a protocol for calling a death on the ambulance scene. Tom Wright from Life Flight explained that “if CPR had been started a Physician must be consulted.  If no CPR had begun then EMS could give permission.”

 

 

Lowman Ambulance: Betty Myers reports that the South Fork Lodge near Lowman, is no longer open. The South Fork Lodge parking lot has always been plowed during the winter and snowmobilers have used this plowed area for parking.  Betty is concerned that with this closure and subsequent lack of snowplowing the snowmobilers will try to use the ambulance shed area for parking.  The South Fork Lodge parking lot is private property

 

Idaho City:  Cyndi Watson is concerned over the “release” mailed to her for her son to volunteer on the rescue crew.  Many of the Advisory Board Members believed that there was a newer version of this release completed within the last few months.  Deputy Clerk Browning will check with Treasea Long and will search for a more current release. 

 

Life Flight:  Tom Wright will talk to the EMS Medical Director about writing protocols for EPI

Pens and baby asprin. Due to an extended LifeFlight training program the “ride along” program has been postponed until June or July 2003. 

Tom reminded all EBCA members that there are many instances, especially in the winter, when LifeFlight is unable to fly. 

 

Having no further business, Chairman Jackson adjourned the regular meeting at 8:30PM.

 

                                                                        Approved this 7th day of November 2002
