EAST BOISE COUNTY AMBULANCE ADVISORY BOARD MINUTES

Boise County Chambers

April 4, 2002

 

The East Boise County Ambulance Advisory Board met in Regular Session on Thursday, April 4, 2002, at 6:00 p.m. in the Boise County Chambers in Idaho City.  Present:, Tom Wright of Lifeflight, Hal Gross of Disaster Services, Phyllis Weiss of Placerville Ambulance, Betty Myers of Lowman Ambulance, Dirk Myers of Mores Creek Ambulance and Deputy Clerk Treasea A. Long.  Absent: D’Anna Smith of Idaho City Ambulance and Chairman Roger Jackson, District I Commissioner.

 

Others Present:  Joe Weiss, Placerville.

 

CALL TO ORDER: Vice Chairman Hal Gross called the meeting to order at 6:18 p.m.

 

AMENDMENTS TO AGENDA:  The Board made no amendments to the agenda.

 

Chairman Roger Jackson arrived for the meeting at 6:22 p.m.

 

MINUTES: Deputy Clerk Long presented the full minutes of the regular meeting of March 7, 2002, for approval.  Phyllis moved the Board approve the full minutes of the regular meeting of March 7, 2002, as presented; seconded by Betty.  Betty, Phyllis, Tom, Hal, Chairman Jackson Ayes, D’Anna Absent, Dirk abstained.  Motion carried.

 

DISASTER SERVICES: Hal Gross, Disaster Services Coordinator, reminded the Board of the Governor’s Conference this summer and that Boise County is still in a drought condition.  Mr. Gross advised the Board of plans for Homeland Security and a push for volunteers.  The Board discussed Foot and Mouth Disease.

 

MISCELLANEOUS:  Deputy Clerk Long requested current rosters from the units with the exception of Mores Creek, to cross reference with Idaho EMS rosters. Betty provided a copy of Lowman’s rosters at the meeting. 

            Deputy Long presented for Board approval a revised Training Reimbursement Contract as recommended by the Prosecuting Attorney.  The Board made additional changes to be forwarded to the P.A. for a legal opinion.

            Highway Safety Grants require proof of seatbelt policies, so Mores Creek and Lowman (both received Highway Safety grant funds) were provided with copies of Idaho Code §49-673, mandating the use of seat belt when using County vehicles.

            Deputy Long reminded the units that upon leaving the units, members must turn in their issues gas cards to the Clerk’s Office to be re-issued or destroyed. The Board agreed to collect the gas cards when appropriate.

            The Board discussed the importance of communication between units in order to fully cover the County and agreed that a system of exchanging monthly schedules was critical. Dirk stated that he would send passwords to each unit to access their web site calendar and also e-mail and fax schedules monthly. D’Anna stated that she tries to call Placerville when they go out of service. Phyllis brought up coverage for Horseshoe Bend when they are out of service and that they cannot cover both ends of the County. The Board discussed changing the boundaries for Horseshoe Bend but opted instead to try to enter into a Mutual Aid Agreement. D’Anna moved that each unit be mandated to communicate between Idaho City, Placerville and Mores Creek.  Further discussion ensued and the moved the Board approve died for lack of a second.  Phyllis moved that all unit excluding Lowman, send monthly calendars by the 1st of each month to all other units; seconded by Betty.  All ayes.  Motion carried.

 

EXECUTIVE SESSION I.C. §67-2345(1)(b):  D’Anna moved the Board go into executive session for personnel; seconded by Phyllis.  All ayes.  Motion carried.  Chairman Jackson moved the Board come out of executive session; seconded by Dirk.  All ayes.  Motion carried.

 

SUPPLIES:  Phyllis addressed the Board to discuss ordering supplies in reasonable quantities in order to stay within expiration guidelines.  The Board agreed that some discretion in ordering was necessary.

 


NEW AMBULANCE COSTS:  Joe Weiss, Placerville Unit, presented a quote for a new ambulance for Lowman.  The Board discussed the options for equipment  and cost of approximately $97,915.  The Board stated that it will consider the ambulance financing in the FY 2002-2003 budget but will submit a grant with Idaho State EMS.

 

ISSUE TRACKING:

Life Flight Membership Lists:  Deputy Long reported that Idaho City paid their member LifeFlight fees but there existed a $36.20 shortage but to an underpayment from Placerville.  Phyllis stated that she will make up the difference.  The Board directed Deputy Long to submit the funds to St. Alphonsus LifeFlight for membership.

Minors at the Ambulance Sheds:  The Commissioners have not received information from the Prosecuting Attorney yet.

Identification Cards:  Chairman Jackson reported to the Board that the Board of Commissioners declined to make identification cards mandatory for EBCA volunteers.  The Clerk forwarded each unit president guidelines for obtaining I.D. cards from the Boise County Sheriff’s Department with the use of a voucher or application form. Phyllis moved the Board direct Deputy Long to create a voucher/application for identification cards for the Sheriff Department; seconded by Dirk.  All ayes.  Motion carried.

Inventory Lists:  Deputy Long reminded the units that they need to complete the inventory lists distributed at last month’s meeting as soon as possible.

Budget Review:  The Board reviewed the monthly expense and budget report.

Exhibit “A” to Resolution #2002-12:  Deputy Long presented an amended Resolution #2002-12, with attached Exhibit “A”.  Phyllis and Deputy Long reviewed and updated all EBCA fees for the attached Exhibit “A” but noted that the effective date would be April 2002 thereby negating the stand-by fees to the units for last year.  The Board requested approval from the Board of Commissioners to pay the Idaho City Unit for the stand-by fees from the EBCA Contingency Fund. 

            Supplies and procedures were discussed.  Phyllis moved the EMT’s note on all run sheets the supplies used and the treatment used on the front of the run sheet for billing purposes; seconded by Dirk.  All ayes.  Motion carried.

 

MISCELLANEOUS and CORRESPONDENCE:

The Board reviewed all correspondence received with no decisions made.

 

INDIVIDUAL UNITS: 

Placerville: Phyllis expressed her displeasure with Mores Creek being out of service for a practice burn; however, it was pointed out that the National Fire Protection Agency requires an ambulance unit on site.

 

Idaho City: D’Anna discussed approaching the Duquette Pines Homeowners Association to allow a landing zone in the common area.  Tom stated the necessary dimensions and regulations required for a landing zone and the Board stressed that D’Anna was not to speak on behalf of the EBCA Advisory Board without express written approval but could as an Idaho City Unit member.  The reimbursement of childcare costs was again brought up by D’Anna and requested to be paid by the unit now but reimburse from next year’s budget.  The Board took no action.

Mores Creek: Dirk questioned the use of the extrication truck in Idaho City and suggested moving it to Mores Creek where both EBCA and trained Fire Department volunteers could benefit from its use.  Placerville strongly objected moving the vehicle from its current centralized location.  The issue was tabled until the May meeting, while Hal would check around for any surplus trucks for sale or donation.

Lowman:  Betty requested Board approval to purchase an education and training video for new vehicles.  No decision was made.  Betty requested that a Mutual Aid Agreement between Stanley and EBCA be completed.  The proposed Agreement was given to Deputy Long for Commissioner approval and signatures.  Betty reported that Lowman Ambulance shed currently sits on private property, which is now in receivership, and an extrication class will be held April13th in Lowman.

LifeFlight: Tom reported no issues or concerns outstanding.

Disaster Services:  Hal reminded the Board of the GIS presentation to be held in April and notification of the dates will be forthcoming.

 

OPEN TO THE FLOOR:  Joe Weiss stated his objections to the snowplowing fees for Mores Creek.  The Board took no action.

 

Having no further business, Hal motioned the regular meeting be adjourned at 9:05 p.m.; seconded by Phyllis.  All ayes.  Motion carried.  The next regular meeting will be held at 6:00 p.m. on Thursday, May 9, 2002.

 

Approved this 9th day of May 2002.
