EAST BOISE COUNTY AMBULANCE ADVISORY BOARD MINUTES

Boise County Chambers

September 5, 2002

 

The East Boise County Ambulance Advisory Board met in Regular Session on Thursday, September 5, 2002, at 6:00 p.m. in the Boise County Chambers in Idaho City.  Present: Chairman Roger Jackson, District I Commissioner; Phyllis Weiss of Placerville Ambulance, Betty Myers of Lowman Ambulance, D’Anna Smith of Idaho City Ambulance, John McCarthy of Mores Creek Ambulance and Deputy Clerk Treasea A. Long.

 

Absent: Tom Wright of Lifeflight, and Hal Gross-Disaster Service Coordinator

 

Others Present: Joe Weiss, Placerville; Cyndi Watson, Idaho City; Randy Smith, Idaho City; Beth & Harry Chandler, Mores Creek; and Mitch Tain.

 

CALL TO ORDER: Chairman Jackson called the meeting to order at 6:00 p.m.

 

AMENDMENTS TO AGENDA:  The Advisory Board added the EMS laptop grant receipt to the agenda.

 

MINUTES: Deputy Long presented the full minutes of the regular meeting of August 1, 2002, for approval. Phyllis moved the Board approve the full minutes of the regular meeting of August 1, 2002, as presented; seconded Betty. All ayes.  Motion carried.

 

HWY 21 LIFEFLIGHT CLOSURE:  The Board tabled the issue until Deputy Sheriff Terry Day could attend.

 

EXTRICATION TRUCK PERSONNEL:  Terry Teeter of the Idaho City VFD met with the Advisory Board to discuss developing a protocol for dispatching the extrication vehicles.  D’Anna advised the Board that a meeting was held with Mr. Teeter and Heather Bedal, Boise County Sheriff’s Dept. dispatch, to discuss the situations involved in which an extrication vehicle would be needed.  Chairman Jackson directed Phyllis to draft an extrication protocol for the SOP’s.

            Mr. Teeter recommended that separate pagers issued to extrication truck personnel.  Phyllis moved the Board have the Sheriff Dept. page for extrication truck personnel.  The Board discussed the issue for some time.  Phyllis amended her moved the Board approve that the extrication truck dispatch go through State Comm.  Seconded by Betty .  All ayes.  Motion carried.  Joe Weiss will contact State Comm and Phyllis will ask Rick Marston to head up an Extrication Team, combining Idaho City and Placerville personnel.  D’Anna moved the Board amend the previous moved the Board approve to include Mitch Tain as coordinator with Rick Marston; seconded by Phyllis.  All ayes.  Motion carried.

 

EXECUTIVE SESSION:  Chairman Jackson moved the Advisory Board go into Executive Session pursuant to I.C. § 67-2345(1)(b); seconded by Phyllis. All ayes. Motion carried. Betty moved the Advisory Board come out of Executive Session; seconded by Chairman Jackson.  All ayes. Motion carried.  The issue will be sent to the Prosecuting Attorney for further review.

 

FY2002 BUDGET ANALYSIS:  Deputy Long reported that the FY02 Budget is coming to an end and with the revenue to date, expenditures should be kept to a minimum.

 

STATE COMM RESPONSE PROTOCOLS:  Deputy Long presented the letter from the Board of Commissioners regarding the dispatching of multiple units to an ambulance call and the protocol to be followed.

 

ISSUE TRACKING:

SOPs:  The SOPS are still being reviewed by the Prosecuting Attorney.

Under Age Release for Extrication Truck: Deputy Long reported that the Board of Commissioners decided to allow minors to participate on extrications truck calls contingent upon the Clerk’s Office receiving a Certificate of Completion of an extrication class for each volunteer.

 

MISCELLANEOUS and CORRESPONDENCE:

Deputy Long presented the correspondence received throughout the month of August.  No decisions were necessary.  The Board reviewed the information. 

 

EMS Grant Awards:  The Ambulance was denied; however, four laptop computers were awarded.  The GEO Media software is to installed by the software agent.  Updates are to be installed by the same representative in conjunction with the 3-year service contract.  The Board discussed training personnel to use the software.  Betty offered the services of her son if he was available.  Changes due to new P&Z approved subdivisions also need to be coordinated for installation.  Deputy Long stated that the Clerk is requesting that prior to any purchases, the County and Computer Arts, Inc. are consulted. 

 

Deputy Long left the meeting at 7:30 p.m. due to a prior commitment.

 

INDIVIDUAL UNITS:  Due to recording problems, no minutes were available for the individual Units discussion.

 

Having no further business, Chairman Jackson adjourned the regular meeting at 8:30 p.m.

 

Approved this 3rd day of October 2002.
