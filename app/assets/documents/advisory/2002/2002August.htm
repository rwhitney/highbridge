EAST BOISE COUNTY AMBULANCE ADVISORY BOARD MINUTES

Boise County Chambers

August 1, 2002

 

The East Boise County Ambulance Advisory Board met in Regular Session on Thursday, August 1, 2002, at 6:00 p.m. in the Boise County Chambers in Idaho City.  Present: Chairman Roger Jackson, District I Commissioner arrived late; Tom Wright of Lifeflight, Phyllis Weiss of Placerville Ambulance, Betty Myers of Lowman Ambulance, D’Anna Smith of Idaho City Ambulance, Hal Gross-Disaster Service Coordinator, Beth Chandler of Mores Creek Ambulance and Deputy Clerk Treasea A. Long.

 

Others Present: Joe Weiss, Placerville; Cyndi Watson, Idaho City; Randy Smith, Idaho City; Terry and Karen Acker, Mores Creek; and Stan Rose, LifeFlight.

 

CALL TO ORDER: Vice Chairman Hal Gross called the meeting to order at 6:10 p.m.

 

AMENDMENTS TO AGENDA:  The Advisory Board added billings for Fire Stand-bys to the agenda.

 

MINUTES: Deputy Long presented the full minutes of the regular meeting of June 6, 2002, for approval.  Betty moved the Board approve the full minutes of the regular meeting of June 6, 2002, as corrected; seconded Phyllis. All ayes.  Motion carried.

 

TRAINING REIMBURSEMENT FEES:  Deputy Long requested Board direction on whether to send training reimbursement accounts to the collection agency.  Currently, two of the eight accounts have made payment arrangements.  The Board reviewed the accounts and noted that three notices have been sent to contact the Clerk’s office.  Betty moved the Board approve sending all delinquent reimbursement of training fee accounts to collection after three notices have been issued; seconded by Beth.  All ayes. Motion carried.

 

SOP’S:  The Board tabled the discussion on the SOP’s until the end of the meeting.

 

IDAHO CITY FUEL TANKS:  Deputy Long reported that the Road Superintendent has questioned what the EBCA plans are regarding the 500-gallon fuel tank at the Idaho City shed.  EPA standards need to be met.  The Board discussed options regarding the tank and agreed that if the tank is sold, the funds should go to the EBCA Fund. 

 

MORES CREEK EXTRICATION TRUCK PERSONNEL:  Deputy Long reported that an incident occurred in which a Wilderness Ranch VFD volunteer requested patient information for an extrication run.  The Board stressed that the extrication truck is owned by the County and personnel from Wilderness Ranch VFD are not insured to drive the vehicle nor is the equipment on board insured.  Beth will meet with John McCarthy to review the situation and come up with a plan.

 

UNDER AGE RELEASE FOR EXTRICATION TRUCK:  Deputy Long stated that the Risk Management Dept. had received an inquiry regarding minors serving on the extrication vehicle as long as the waiver form was completed.  The Board discussed the pros and cons of allowing minors to serve.  The Board requested that the Board of Commissioners provide a direction.  The issue was table for Commissioner Jackson to address the Commissioners.

 

 

ISSUE TRACKING:

Budget Review:  The Board reviewed the Expenditure Budget Report.  Joe Weiss questioned why the County put the Mores Creek Ambulance Unit up for collateral for the Mores Creek Ambulance Building per his conversation with Chief Deputy Clerk Pat Coleman.  Commissioner Jackson will follow-up on the issues.

 

Inventory Lists: Deputy Long reported that all inventory lists had been submitted to the Clerk’s Office.

 

Dispatching Policy:  Hal stated that he has contacted Captain Bowers to discuss an extrication truck dispatching policy. According to Captain Bowers, the trucks will be dispatched at the request of the EBCA personnel.  EBCA volunteers need to also request the extrication trucks through State Comm.  Hal reiterated that any problems must be properly documented and submitted to himself or Captain Bowers to investigate.  D’Anna volunteered to work directly with Heather at the Boise County Sheriff’s Office Dispatch Dept. to develop a policy.

 

I.D. Card Forms:  Deputy Long distributed the new I.D. Card forms as approved by the Board of Commissioners.  All unit presidents were present to sign the “Signature Card” for the Boise County Sheriff’s Dept. files as authorized signers on the form.

 

Gas Cards: Commissioner Jackson reported that the Board of Commissioners has approved the use of the Flying J gas cards for use at night and in emergencies.  Deputy Long requested that the units report back the last four digits of the cards currently in the units in order to verify that the cards are activated.  Commissioner Jackson also stated that the Sinclair cards could be used in emergency situations and suggested that #1 fuel sources be investigated in November.

 

MISCELLANEOUS and CORRESPONDENCE:

Resolution #2002-12:  Deputy Long presented Resolution #2002-12 as approved by the Board of Commissioners.  The Resolution is effective FY03.

 

Billing Fire Stand-bys:  Beth questioned the past policy of not billing for fire stand-bys.  The Board discussed billing private owners’ insurance, the USFS, BLM and Idaho Dept. of Lands.  Beth moved the EBCA bill for all fire stand-bys and accepts the payment from the homeowners’ insurance as payment in full; Betty seconded the motion.  Further discussion ensued. All ayes.  Motion carried. 

            Hal noted that during HAZMAT situations, the ambulance units must be specifically requested to attend in order to be reimbursed.

 

Planning and Zoning Notices:  Deputy Long presented to the Board the notices of public hearing and Map Book changes from Planning and Zoning.  No action was taken.

 

American Institute of Health Technology, Inc.:  The Board reviewed the letter from the American Institute of Health Technology offering EMT classes in the Treasure Valley.  Joe pointed out that he inadvertently applied for two EMS grants for training classes and the second grant would pay for classes in the Treasure Valley for EMT’s serving in Boise County. The Board thanked Joe for his application.

 

Idaho City Marshall Letter:  The Board reviewed the letter from the former Idaho City Police Chief regarding ambulance unit speed limits within city limits.  The Board acknowledged his comments.

EMS Notices:  The Board received a notice from EMS regarding the issuance of an air ambulance license to St. Lukes Hospital.  Tom commented on the St. Lukes air ambulance abilities to provide a secondary source of service for the EBCA.  

 

 

INDIVIDUAL UNITS: 

Placerville: Phyllis reviewed the Ride Ticket redemption policy with Deputy Long.  Deputy Long stated that a ride ticket is not redeemed if the insurance carrier fully covers the costs.

            Phyllis reported that the extrication class was a success and that the EBCA needs to have a new classification for these volunteers.  Commissioner Jackson will address the issue with the Board of Commissioners.

            Phyllis thanked the Acker’s for their swift response to calls now that the unit is stationed at their home when they are on duty. 

            Phyllis discussed with Tom the protocols still in place with Life Flight and State Comm regarding dispatching.  Tom introduced Stan Rose, Life Flight Director who addressed the issue stating that St. Lukes will be a back-up position behind Life Flight at State Comm.  Phyllis spoke to St. Lukes and was told that they would honor Life Flight memberships.

            Finally, Phyllis brought up a Mores Creek Driver responding as a first responder without proper certification and giving oxygen improperly.  The Board discussed the County liability.  Beth assured the Board that she would speak to the individual as soon as possible.

 

Mores Creek: Beth informed the Advisory Board that their last fundraising event was successful and they have a water rescue class soon.  Beth requested Joe’s assistance in obtaining landing lights.

 

Idaho City: D’Anna brought to the Board’s attention the loss of a portable Oxygen tank and if any information is available on its whereabouts, please contact the unit. 

            D’Anna stated that new CPR guidelines require a regular and infant sized mannequin per student, which will increase the cost to $30/hour/person.  An Infection Control Class through IEMST will be offered with a minimum ten people required at $10/hour.

            New KEDs for the Idaho City unit have been received.

 

Lowman:  Betty reported that the Lowman extrication volunteers are requesting permission to order gloves.  Phyllis will purchase the gloves. 

            Betty discussed with the Board the call on July 20th requiring the use of horses to get into the backcountry.  Hal informed the Board that the Boise County Sheriff’s Department is responsible for search and rescue efforts and therefore he must be contacted to make the necessary arrangements to locate and retrieve the injured party.

 

Life Flight: Tom introduced the new Life Flight director, Stan Rose. Mr. Rose gave a brief speech and the Board thanked him for attending the meeting.  No other new issues were presented.

 

Disaster Services:  Hal reported that the Crouch ambulance has activated their secondary unit for the Banks area bringing the total number of ambulance units in Boise County to seven.

            Hal thanked everyone for their cooperation for the Governor’s Conference and the 4th of July.  No problems occurred.

            Other issues discussed were the Radio problem at Shaw Mountain and Homeland Security.  An exercise drill is being coordinated with Central District Health but could take some time to actually occur.

 

Miscellaneous  Commissioner Jackson questioned whether the loss of the Lowman Ambulance shed property is an issue.  Betty informed Commissioner Jackson that they will soon be moved into the new building and therefore, the foreclosure on the other property does not affect them.

 

SOP’S:  Deputy Long received the changes to the drafted SOPS’s as requested.  All comments and changes will be incorporated into the draft for the Prosecuting Attorney’s review.

 

OPEN TO THE FLOOR: No further issues were raised.

 

Having no further business, Chairman Jackson adjourned the regular meeting at 8:40 p.m.

 

Approved this ____ day of _______ 2002.
