MCA General Meeting

July 2005

 

Call to Order: 7:21pm

 

Advisory Board Report:

 

Old Business: 

 

New Business:

-There have been supplies and equipment missing and improperly restocked on the ambulance.  This cannot continue as it directly influences our quality of patient care.  Always replace the items you use on a call when you return to the shed.  There will be new inventory sheets kept in the shed and we are responsible for marking down what we take both for the ambulance (mark with an A) and for our personal jump kits (mark with a J). 

            -Don’t pick up anymore sheets or blankets because the ambulance has more then enough.  Trade out pillows if anything.

            -Thanks to Peggy and Sarina the ambulance has been cleaned and restocked and labeled so make it a point to go through the ambulance at least once before your next shift to familiarize yourself with the equipment and location.  Call or email Peggy if you find that we need more of an item and she will pick it up.

            -Anything you buy on your won for your jump kits, EBCA will pay to replace it once it has been used.

            -O2 tanks have been found empty in the jump kits.  Always remember to turn off the O2 tanks and if you use them at all on a call, switch them out with new ones.  Call Terry Acker BEFORE we run out of all new O2 tanks so he can replace them and we will not have a shortage.

            -Dr. Sturkie was asked a question about burn care.  It is OK to put saran wrap over burns lightly, but do not wrap the area.

            -PCR’s are not being filled out correctly and we are having many kicked back to us.  Please doublecheck your PCR’s before turning them in and make sure that all information is correct and that the bubbles have all been filled in correctly and completely.  If you mark ANYTHING in the assessment box, including NONE, and them you do not transport…always mark Treat, No Transport in the destination box (do not mark Pt. Refused).

            -If law enforcement stands down the ambulance, it is up to the crew to decide to continue or not.  If stood down, fill out a PCR and state that you were stood down by law enforcement.  Sheriff Bodie added that law enforcement should not be canceling the ambulance.  If they do, you can downgrade but still continue to the scene.

            -There will be a new radio and GPS soon for the ambulance, and there is a new AED on order through EBCA for our unit.

            -Through MCA, we receive individual memberships to Life Flight.  Family memberships cost $35. A membership will cover you throughout the region.  FYI, St. Luke’s and St. Al’s do not honor ACCESS AIR memberships.  A membership will cover you throughout the region.

            -John McCarthy stated that board involvement seems minimal.  He questioned why the fundraiser was cancelled, and feels that people are feeling let down by the current board.  The unit is not a cohesive unit.  There have been a lot of ideas, but nothing gets done.  People looking to join the unit have had trouble connecting and communicating with the appropriate persons.  Other members joined in the discussion and voiced different complaints and ideas for a more cohesive unit. One idea that was brought up and will be attempted is the organization of the unit into teams to facilitate communication and bring people closer together.  It will also open up the possibility for training get-togethers, team games, and encouragement and support from team members to boost morale and hopefully fill more of our shifts.  Carolyn Bohn, Maureen Ericksen, Molly Kaczmarek and Sarina Gordon volunteered to be team leaders and initiate the team idea.

 

Meeting Adjourned:  Meeting not formally adjourned, as the members broke up into smaller groups to discuss issues and the new team idea.
