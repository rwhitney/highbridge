New Business:

-         We received a thank you from Shawna Gonzales for all of our hard work and help when she was in a car accident.  Theresa Gardenia also expressed gratitude for the work done at a call to her house.

-         Thank you to Terry Acker for changing the tires on the ambulance.

-         Thank you to Gus for all of his past work as Infection Control Officer.  Gus has now resigned and we need someone new to take over the position.  If involves making sure people are current on their shots, occasional training, reviewing protocols on needle-sticks, exposures, etc, filling out paperwork for exposures and being a contact for people to call with questions regarding said protocols.  Thank you to Sarina Gordon for volunteering to take the position.

-         It was asked if drivers are held to a shift if no EMT’s have signed up, or if an EMT is held to a shift if they are signed up with no driver.  It’s great if we can all be proactive and call around and try to fill a shift so that we can stay in service.  Sign up at least 24 hours ahead of the start of a shift if possible.  If the shift starts and no one else is signed up, you are not held to that shift and you are free.  If someone decides to cover part or all of a shift at the last moment, they must call the other people on the shift and make sure they are still available.

-         Dawn Best said that if anyone wants to split day shifts with her on the weekends, she would love to do that.  Jo McCarthy encouraged splitting shifts if it means that we will have more coverage.  To get credit for splitting a shift, just email Jo and tell her who and when.

-         Carolyn Bohn suggested a mentor program be started, with senior EMT’s and senior drivers mentoring new EMT’s and drivers.  Kim and Deb will pursue this in the questionnaire that they will be sending out.

-         A new radio for the shed has been ordered so hopefully we won’t miss any more calls.  Gus has noticed a problem receiving calls in the shed.

-         Peggy will look into getting new MCA placards for EMT’s and drivers.

-         There was a debate about the use of clips for the radios versus holsters.  Both are OK, with the knowledge that if a radio is destroyed because it was used with a clip, MCA will have to pay for it.  FI the radio was holstered, EBCA pays for it.

-         Peggy has 5 books and a tape for CPR training for MCA now.
