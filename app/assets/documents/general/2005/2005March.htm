MCA General Meeting Minutes

March 2nd, 2005

 

Called to Order:  8:08pm

 

Members Present:  Joan McCarthy, John McCarthy, Blaine Whitney, Kim Chanley, Molly Kaczmarek, Dirk Myers, Carolyn Bohn, Maureen Ericksen

 

Old Business: 

     *We were approved in a grant to receive high-powered battery lights and extra batteries and seat belt-style straps for the long boards, but were turned down on the cocoon blankets.  Possible alternatives for the extra money would be a high-powered stethoscope for the ambulance or a roof rack to hold the Stokes stretcher so it’s not in the cab.  Dirk will investigate the cost of a rack and what type we would need so that it wouldn’t block any of the ambulance lights.

 

New Business:

     *Blaine will check with Gordon to find out what items we submitted for in the 2004 Homeland Security grant.

     *Kim and John will work together on applying for the FEMA grant.  EMS has recently been allowed to apply for this grant as well as fire units.

     *There is some confusion on who is sending in the calendar to Statecom each month.  Dirk is going to call them and see if they would accept accessing it online with a password.

     *There was the start of a discussion on the roles and responsibilities of drivers, as well as the need for training on strictly driving issues.  We have many members, both drivers and EMT’s, who arrive on scene feeling ill-prepared due to lack of training and uncertainty about what they should be doing in their role as driver.  Carolyn Bohn suggested that we have a bigger discussion about this at our next general meeting in April.  Dirk is looking into how to add this into our training schedule.

     *Carolyn brought up the question of how we can bring back lapsed members. 

     *We need to improve our quality of care and institute more quality assurance checks.  A possibility may be joint reviews between MCA, fire departments and law enforcement.

     *Terry is looking for someone to take over the coordination of the Race to Robie Creek coverage, as he will be running the race.  Maureen Ericksen expressed interest in helping.

     *Joan is still looking for feedback on the strategic plan and on the idea of Swat teams, and wants to know what teams people are interested in joining.

     *We need to be thinking about fund-raising opportunities.  Several ideas were tossed around, including a duck race down Mores Creek, a Las Vegas night and a care rally at Spring Shores.  Dirk and John said they would look into the different licensing issues and general interest levels of a car rally.

 

Meeting adjourned at 9:14pm.
