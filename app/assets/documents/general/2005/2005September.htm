MCA General Meeting

September 13th, 2005

 

Call to Order: 7:37pm

 

Introduction to New Members:  Lea, Jamie, Casey, Mike, Deanna, Jason, Paul

 

Treasurer’s Report:  Passed around and approved.

 

August Meeting Minutes:  Passed around and approved.

 

Old Business: 

-         Inventory sheets were passed around for everyone to look at and instructions were given on how to use them.

-         New AED’s and new training dummies are coming.

-         Our Life blankets will be coming soon…they will include 2 blankets, a back pack to carry them, liftpads, disposable liners, etc.

-         Peggy will get the ambulance’s windshield repaired next week.  The membership recommended Dave Adams on Orchard and Fairview.

 

Advisory Board Report:

            -The radio for the shed is not here yet.  Gordon Ravenscroft will let us know as soon as it is here.

-         Peggy will get a MCA roster to Gordon and Jill, but will take off the addresses and email addresses of all the members to protect our information.

-          Idaho City is still looking for volunteer EMT’s to help cover the JV football games on Thursday nights and the Varsity games on Friday nights.  We will send out an email that includes Randy Smith;s number, so people can contact him if they would like to volunteer for a game.

-         There will be an EMT class starting up in the fall in Idaho City.  It is MCA’s turn to provide a coordinator.  The person will need to get certified as a coordinator (to our knowledge, this is a quick and easy process)and will need to attend every class.  Some of the members were interested, but they want more specifics before committing.  There were a few members who were interested in being a joint coordinator with another member.

-         State EMS is going through restructuring and there will be many new personnel there.

-         We need to call OxArc to come and inspect our unit’s fire extinguishers.

-         If anyone still has a Bendix-King radio, please bring it to the shed and put it in the box by the door.

-         Randy Smith’s monthly trainings at the Idaho City High School will be held on the second Saturday evening of each month from 7-9pm.

-         The Advisory Board meeting in October will be held in Lowman.  It will be on the first Thursday of the month and will start at 6:30 or 7:00pm.

-         Anyone interested in being the next Advisory Board representative should try to go to a meeting or two beforehand to see how it goes.  The meetings are usually held on the first Thursday of the month at 6pm in the commissioner’s ‘bar’ in Idaho City.

 

New Business:

-         The SOP’s were explained and passed around.  It was explained that we must have a 2/3 vote by the membership to change anything in the SOP’s.

-         There was a proposal to change our shifts to 6 hour shifts and require members to sign up for 4 shifts a month.  After much discussion, the proposal was passed and the goal will be to start the shift change in November, after the technical changes can be made.

-         Peggy told everyone about some possibilities of filling some of our day shifts with Americorps volunteers after we put a local program director in place.  This possibility would require some time and work.  Dirk suggested increasing our recruiting efforts immediately, and proposed starting a recruiting/public relations committee.  He volunteered to be on the committee.  Several ideas were suggested of places to recruit, including NPR, Boise State, colleges and junior colleges, the Idaho Statesman, and local Homeowner’s meetings, including the one for the subdivision that houses our shed. 

-         There was a discussion regarding incentives and the membership was asked if they would like the unit to use some of the money in our account to go towards incentives for the members…based on a variety of criteria including shifts pulled, participation in committees and on the board, shed and ambulance maintenance, serving as officers, extra help, etc.  Some ideas for incentives were a big party for all of us at a nice restaurant, massages, sleigh rides @ Bogus Basin, fishing trips, etc.  Members were encouraged to think about other things they would like to see as incentives and bring up their ideas at the October general meeting.

-         There will be a CPR training soon for everyone who needs to recertify.  An email will be sent out with dates and times.

-         A roster will be sent to the membership listing the teams and team leaders.

-         We have had some shift responsibility issues lately: people not filling shifts that they have signed up for and not communicating properly with their crew members.  Written warnings will be sent to people who are not being responsible for shifts they have signed up for.

-         A proposal was made to set a 48 hour limit on signing up for a shift, unless you communicate with the other crew members and make sure everyone is still on board.  There were many opinions expressed, and due to the lateness of the meeting, the issue was tabled and will be on the agenda for the October general meeting.

-         The board meeting will be held each month on the last Tuesday of the month at 7:00pm at the Ambulance shed.  This will allow the board meeting to be held before the EBCA meeting on the following Thursday, so issues can be discussed that need to be broached at the EBCA meeting.

 

Meeting Adjourned:  9:45pm
