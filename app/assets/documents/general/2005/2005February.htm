Advisory Board Meeting Report

     -There will be a basic trauma life support class offered soon.  It will be a 2-day class and is basically a ‘certificate to your certificate’.  We are a pre-dominantly trauma unit, so this would be a really good training for us to have.  A list was passed around for interested EMT’s to sign up for the class.

     -We are trying to get some equipment from a Homeland Security Grant…such as cocoon blankets, battery-run portable lights and straps for the stoke’s stretcher.

     -We are also trying to get a Highway Safety Grant, and use it for signage along the highways to increase people’s awareness of Mores Creek Ambulance.  Peggy asked about applying for ropes and rescue equipment, or extrication equipment.

     -EBCA is looking into purchasing some C.E. accredited training videos that will be available for check-out from Idaho City. 

     -There will be a driver’s training class in Idaho City coming up for all drivers and EMT’s.

Training & Schedule Changes:

     The new MCA schedule is as follows:

           The board meeting will be held on the first Wednesday of the month at the MCA shed at 7:00pm, immediately followed by the general meeting at 8:00pm.

           Training will be held on the second Tuesday of the month at 7:00pm at the Wilderness Ranch Fire Barn.  Additional training will be held at the same location on the following Saturday, every other month, from 12:00-2:00pm.

 

     -Concerns were raised about the quality of training that will be generated by this new system of team training.  In some cases, people are signed up to train on topics that other people are more suited for.  Some people also expressed that they did not want to present or did not have the time to coordinate a training.  Dawn Best said that people need to call herself or Dirk with their concerns and they can be figured out.

     -There were also concerns raised about the training topics being incomplete; for example, there is no pediatric training on the rotation.

     -To promote teamwork, it was proposed that a driver be added to each training rotation.
