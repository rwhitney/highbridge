Minutes from Mores Creek Ambulance General Meeting

November 18, 2004

 

Called to order: 6:50 pm

 

Secretary Kim Chanley absent.  Molly Kaczmarek taking minutes.

 

Treasurer’s report circulated and approved.

 

Advisory Board Meeting Update: (by Peggy Blodgett, Jan Peterson gone).

§         Drug testing was again discussed.

§         There were some grammatical changes to the SOP’s.  We should be able to vote on any changes made to the SOP’s...EBCA should send out updates when they are changing things and the advisory board rep can bring those updates back to our membership.

§         We have a new furnace, donated by Wilderness Ranch Fire.  The only cost accrued was $1200 for installation.

§         Flu shots are available to EMT’s and drivers.  It is by appointment only, and you will need a voucher.  You must tell them that you are EMS.  The shot will be given to those people who are over 50, or have medical problems.  Anyone under 50 with no medical problems will receive the attenuated live virus.  Dr. Sturkie pointed out that with the live virus, you have a 30% chance of a mild case of the flu and you are contagious for 21 days.  For times and dates you can get the flu shots, contact Peggy.

§         The ALS position on the board, formerly held by Tom Wright from St. Al’s Life Flight, is up for grabs.  It is possible that Dr. Sturkie may take the position.

§         The board is working on the new landing zone for Clear Creek.

§         Dr. Sturkie is looking into a co-directorship with Dr. Koenig within the EBCA.  It is not feasible for EBCA to have 2 different medical directors.

 

Supplies:  We are now ordering supplies directly through Phyllis.  This will save us from going up to Idaho City and from having to wait for the supplies.   We need to figure out where we are going to house the supplies.  Do not take supplies out of the ambulance to stock your personal jump kits.  Peggy is working on a spreadsheet that will list out every supply that we have on hand.

We are looking into getting a grant from Gordon McNamara for more O2 tanks.  A thank you was given to Jeff Arnswald for getting 4 new regulators for the new EMT’s.

 

Coats:  The coats are ordered and should be here in time for the Christmas party.  They will have EMS printed on the back and reflective stripping on the back and on each sleeve.  Each person’s name will be embroidered on the front.

 

Community Issues:  January 1st is the annual Polar Bear Dip.  Anyone who would like to go can volunteer.  This year, Mores Creek Ambulance is actually named in the brochures for the event.

 

Peggy said that she has supply magazine’s at her house, and we can order things from them for 20%off.  The unit should be able to buy shirts again this year, because the coats ended up being mostly free.

 

We will have no December General Meeting.  Instead, we will be having our Annual Christmas Party at the Wilderness Ranch Fire Station on December 4th at 6:30pm.  A list is being circulated for people to sign up to bring food.  The unit will be providing lasagna.

 

Our January meeting may be held on a Friday night so Steve Cole can come in and give training on documentation.

 

Everyone needs to sign up for shifts on the calendar for December.  It is looking pretty bare.
