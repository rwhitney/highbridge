Question & Answer Session with Dr. Murry Sturkie, Medical Director

·        Dr. Sturkie is an Emergency Physician with St. Luke’s.  He is the Medical Director for Eagle Fire Dept., Star Fire Dept., and one of 2 Medical Directors for Ada County.  He has been an EMT, a Paramedic, a volunteer firefighter and a firefighter.  He also is the new owner of CPR Connections.

·        We shared with Dr. Sturkie some of the unique challenges that MCA faces.  We cover a very large area (larger in the winter), we have a low volume of calls (approx. 90 a year), and on bad days we cannot get Life Flight up here.  We struggle to fill our shifts 24/7, particularly because we do not have any retirees and coverage for the weekdays is difficult. 

·        Epipen Training  -  The course is 1-2 hours long and Dr. Sturkie will train us if we cannot get Steve Cole or an Ada County Paramedic to come up and do the training.  It is NOT necessary for every EMT to be trained with the Epipen for MCA to start using them.  However, only the people who have gone through the training can administer them.  The county has already purchased the Epipens and we would like to do the training as soon as possible.

·        Dr. Sturkie plans to come up every couple months to do run reviews and chart reviews.  He wants to keep the quality of care for the patients high, and he would like to build with us from where we are currently.

·        The Advanced level for our EMT’s is attainable, but the Intermediate level would be fairly difficult to attain.  Advanced status allows the EMT’s to use Comby-Tubes (and to intubate in some counties), to start IV’s and to check glucose.  It is a 40-60 hour class.  We would be able to go into the hospitals to do skills maintenance and keep up our skills despite our low volume of calls.  With a special waiver, we could move to Advanced even if we are not able to have all of our EMT’s trained to that level at once.  The waiver would give us time to get everyone certified.  There would always have to be 1 EMT-Advanced on each call.  Question: If we went to the Advanced level, would all of EBCA have to go to that level?

·        Nationally, the EMT-Advanced status will stop in 2 years.  It will be a legislative issue in Idaho to continue it.

·        We should be applying for grants from the EMS bureau, as should all of EBCA.

·        We brought up that we have found it hard in the past to get feedback on our calls from the hospitals.  We would like to be able to get information on the patients and how our interventions worked, etc., for educational purposes.

·        Question:  Can MCA EMT’s work with other EBCA units despite there being 2 different medical directors?  Dr. Sturkie said yes, the EBCA is all under the same license, so we should be able to be EMT’s with other units.

·        Dr. Sturkie will talk to Dr. Koenig about protocols, SOP’s, etc.

·        We should get the new state protocols from the state EMS or off the internet.

·        We need a medical director on the advisory board, either voting or non-voting, and Dr. Sturkie will talk to Dr. Koenig about it. 

·        Many of our drivers expressed an interest in becoming First Responders, which is a 60-hour course.  There will soon be a pilot program that will enable First Responders to do a bridge course to becoming an EMT, instead of having to start from scratch.  The First Responders for the program would be evaluated and selected by the medical director.  First Responders could not pull call, but they could go along in the 2nd EMT position.  First Responder status would also be great for many of the fire departments personnel.  We would have to look into funding for the First Responder course through EBCA.

·        Interest was expressed in wilderness medicine training.  There is no certification in Idaho for this, just additional training.  We are also interested in training on special ops, litter evacs, technical rescue, steep terrain, etc.

·        To contact Dr. Sturkie, email him at msturkie@emidaho.com.
