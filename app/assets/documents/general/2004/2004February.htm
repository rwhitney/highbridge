Advisory Board Meeting Report

  • Roger Jackson (Commissioner), out the month of February
  • Mores Creek Ambulance to have its own medical director. Terry Acker has approached Dr. Casey Murphey, a previous medical director, and two other doctors have expressed interest. Terry asked for suggestions to be forwarded to him. The time requirement suggested is 2-3 trainings per year, minimum, and availability for consultation.
  • A MCA crew may respond to a call regardless of whether or not they are on the schedule; they must contact State Com and let them know they are responding; State Com may not stand down the crew scheduled to respond.
  • Commissioners to determine if the ambulance may be “staged” at a location other than the shed in order to provide better response time, particularly in inclement weather.
  • State is developing an epi-pen training program.  In the meantime, Diane Shoemaker (R.N.) may be qualified to be the instructor for the training, or the new medical director could conduct the training.  The goal is to have MCA EMTs trained prior to the spring insect season.

 

Old Business

       

New Business

  • Idaho City Senior Center needs EMTs to check blood pressure for seniors two Thursdays per month. Contact Peg Blodgett if anyone interested in assisting.
  • There is an EMS conference in Grangeville on March 5&6.  Early registration was due February 20th but registration open until the day of the conference.
  • An EMT class is going to be held this Spring (possibly in Idaho City). Randy Smith may be the instructor.  Also, an East Boise County refresher class will be offered.
  • Mores Creek Ambulance will be working the Robie Creek Race April 17th;  EMTs should let Terry know if they want to provide support/assistance to participants and let him know if you have a jump kit; Terry is also soliciting anyone who has an ATV/quad big enough to carry up to two patients – let Terry know if you are able to assist. Five to twelve people are needed; let Terry know your preference on which part of the route you would like to be placed. Jan Peterson has extra jump kits for EMTs to use if necessary.
  • Fund raising ideas: Terry requested participation from anyone in the group who would be willing to head up fund raising efforts for MCA. He stressed the person would not be responsible to put the whole thing together alone, but needed to coordinate other volunteers.  Jan suggested looking into what other fire and ambulance units have done, including hiring professional fundraisers who take a portion of the profit as payment. Jan also suggested we increase our visibility by getting a booth at the state fair. Raffle tickets were also suggested as a fundraising option.
  • Terry Acker informed the group that MCA was given a box full of polo shirts from another unit. MCA will purchase 100 patches with the unit’s logo to cover embroidery on the shirts.  Joan McCarthy looking into contact who can produce the patches inexpensively.
  • Kim Chanley asked all EMTs to be mindful of submitting properly-completed PCR’s and calling her at her home number (343-1863) to let her know of any runs. She requested particular attention to obtaining proper signatures and being more specific about which supplies were used.
  • Training Schedule: Training will be conducted on AED, CPR, Epi-pen usage (a new medical director must be identified before this training); Peg Blodgett suggested training on how to recognize signs of abuse.
  •  Maureen Ericksen suggested we take time after each monthly general meeting to practice skills. We will start with the next General Meeting in March.
  • It was noted that the WRVFD fund raiser will be held on June 26, 2004 and the Robie Creek fundraiser will be held on the last weekend in September.
