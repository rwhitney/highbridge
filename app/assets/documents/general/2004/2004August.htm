Minutes from Mores Creek Ambulance General Meeting

August 19, 2004

 

 

Call to Order: 7:50 pm

 

Treasurer’s report circulated and approved

 

Advisory Board meeting update:

  • Jo had sent the minutes to the membership via email
  • Additional information: Dr. Sturkie recommended drug testing only of new EMT’s and for cause; Phyllis recommended that random testing was necessary as a deterrent. The policy will be discussed by the medical directors and a joint recommendation will be issued at the next Advisory Board meeting.

 

The unit thanked Ken Wells for his hard work in clearing the parking lot and thanked Peggy Blodgett and Dave Haney for taking the ambulance into town for maintenance

 

Labor Day fundraising idea: Peg Blodgett will head up a fund raiser for Labor Day – an ice cream social and bake sale. Anyone in the unit is welcome to participate by donating baked goods and/or helping to sell them.  Peg will look into getting ice cream and cones donated by a local company.  The WRVFD kindly donated some gift certificate’s to Cactus Pete’s as raffle items.  So far, volunteers include:

§         Jim Bryant

§         Kim Chanley

 

Community service suggestion: providing mobile blood pressure checks, perhaps linking up with the Book Mobile; those working Saturday shifts may wish to spend “down” time participating in this community effort.

 

Terry noted that there were some instances of confusion when EMT’s shifted their shifts around.  The protocol is that someone needs to change or get coverage for a shift that they call everyone on the shift and make sure everyone knows who will be lead EMT on the scene.

 

Terry also confirmed that students don’t need to be reluctant to bring up questions to lead EMT’s on a scene. It’s expected that anyone will speak up if there are questions, whether a student or another EMT.

 

Upcoming training:

§         EpiPens – Steve Cole will train us; Jo is coordinating

§         PCR’s – Kim Martin from State EMS will review changes and provide copies of the windows-based version

Drew Bodie announced that a new dispatch system to be starting around October 1, which will be computer-aided.  A mapping system is anticipated at some point. Drew showed the unit the new mini jump kits now carried by sheriffs and deputies in their cars; they are for EMS personnel to use.

 

The unit voted to remove the laptop from the ambulance, citing safety reasons and difficulty in using the program. With the changes in dispatch capability it was noted that the laptop may be outmoded.

 

St. Luke’s distributed a profit-sharing check of approximately $430 to More’s Creek Ambulance; Kim is writing a note to the program director indicating we will use the funds for training and uniforms. 

 

Phyllis is attempting to get $1,000 allocated for uniforms for each unit.

 

Mores Creek Ambulance received a check for $500 from the grateful mother of a motorcycle accident victim.   We also received a card from another grateful citizen. We will post the cards on the display Carolyn Bohn made for MCA EMS and bring it to fund-raisers/PR opportunities.

 

On August 29th, Mores Creek Ambulance will have it’s annual get-together starting at 1:00pm at Terry and Karen Acker’s house. Softball and BBQ – bring softball equipment, food to share, drinks and something to sit on. Burgers and dogs will be provided.

 

More’s Creek Ambulance was toned out for an Ada County assist last week; even though it is outside of our service area, if you are toned out anywhere, GO and work out the issues with state com after the fact. Don’t ever stand yourself down whether or not it’s outside of your service area.

 

If you are requiring extrication on a call, tone out the RESCUE TRUCK, not “extrication.”  Contact State Com to have them toned out; deputies should not stand down any rescue support requested of State Com by EMT’s.

 

5 new 2140 radios will be coming to Mores Creek Ambulance from the Homeland Security grant received by the state.  The Bendix Kings will be phased out. John McCarthy asked if the old radios could be donated to fire companies in need of equipment; Jan Peterson will check on that.

 

Supplies needed: O2 – 11 D cylinders to be filled; a dozen pen lights; 7 additional O2 tanks needed for new EMT’s.  Deb Arnswald working on getting a deal on regulators from Norco.

 

The 2005 Board Nominating Committee was selected:

Discussion of Landing Zone setup and Incident Command procedures

  • It is the lead EMT’s responsibility to contact State Com and request a landing zone be set up for LifeFlight
  • A specific fire department must be requested by the EMT
  • Anyone acting as IC needs to identify himself/herself on the radio so all involved know who is coordinating
  • More discussion of the role of the IC and who should act as IC forthcoming in future meeings.
