MINUTES OF

MORES CREEK AMBULANCE REGULAR MEMBER MEETING

August 21, 2001

 

Beth Chandler called the Regular Member Meeting of Mores Creek Ambulance to order at 6:52 p.m. The meeting was held at the Mores Creek Ambulance Building.  Officers present: Beth Chandler, President, Dirk Myers, Vice President & EBCA Advisory Board, and Carol Riazzi, Secretary/Treasurer.  Members present: Maureen Ericksen, Dave McGlone, Terry Acker, Rhonda Grindstaff, Gus Catherman, Doug Trosky, Kimberly Tkacs,  Margie Jones, Andrew Smith, Karen Acker, John Nesbit, Mietta Sibert, and Suzanna Smith.

 

CONTEST POINT STRUCTURE – Beth discussed the realignment of the 4 teams.  From August 1 through October 31 (midnight) we will have a competition among the teams.  You can score points by attending meetings, seminars & training; pulling call; organizing an event for the unit; and extra duties (e.g., cleaning the building, etc.).  Team Leaders are responsible for tallying points.  A dinner for the winners will be held on Saturday, November 10.  The second place team will plan the event and menu & purchase the food; the third place team sets up tables and cooks the food & the fourth place team handles clean up.  A flyer will be distributed to team members by the team leaders detailing this event.

 

SEPTEMBER CALENDAR – Karen Acker is handling the schedule while Beve moves.  Beve will still have the same e-mail address & phone #.  Karen distributed the Sept. calendar for shift sign-up.  Beth hinted at a good training event the weekend of September 15—it will be a surprise for those who sign up for shift.

 

NEW MEMBERS --- Beth introduced John Nesbit, a new driver, who works for Sun Valley Express and Wes Toal, another driver, who is a friend of Gus’.  Beth also has 2 more interested drivers from Micron—don’t forget that you also get points for bringing in new members.

 

BY-LAWS, SOP'S & MEMBER HANDBOOK – The binders containing the By-Laws/SOP’s & Member Handbook were distributed to all attendees.  Boise County is to come out with their own version and ours need to fit within their guidelines.  The By-Laws are pretty cut & dried; the SOP’s are less structured.  You need to let your Team Leader know of addendums needed to the SOP’s.  Any comments/concerns should be addressed to your Team Leader or the Board.  (PLEASE NOTE:  Those members who received their binders at the meeting need to complete the “MCA Handbook Acknowledgement” which is located on the back of the first page & return it to their Team Leader or to a Board Member.  Dirk is preparing new title sheets to replace the sheet you will turn in.  Additionally, anyone who takes a binder from the ambulance building, needs to complete this sheet & return it to their Team Leader/Board Member.)

 

MEETING WITH BOISE COUNTY SHERIFF’S DEPARTMENT –

  • Members of Wilderness Ranch Volunteer Fire Department and MCA met with the Sheriff’s Office in the hope of improving our working relationship. Dirk posted his notes from the meeting on the ambulance bulletin board.  He mentioned that Channel 8 on our radios is programmed for EMT’s to communicate with one another, but is actually a U.S. Forrest Service channel---please use it conservatively.  USFS will eventually discontinue our use of this channel.  EMS and fire personnel on scene need to be in uniform or identifiable.  Dirk is working on ID’s for members.  The Sheriff requested that we keep POV response to a minimum (particularly on the highway).  Gary Brown, Boise County Sheriff, extended an invitation to all MCA members to spend a Friday or Saturday evening with the Sheriff Office’s dispatch.  If interested, you can call the Sheriff’s Office and ask for Heather .  Karen mentioned that Monday a.m. is also a busy time.  John Nesbit also suggested that we offer an open-door policy to the Sheriff’s Office to use our MCA facility whenever they might need it.

 

  • Driver Training --  The Sheriff asked that we be conservative in the use of sirens & running code.  On a fire stand-by, there is no reason to use lights or sirens or to drive over the speed limit.  Two sheriff deputies will be taking an Emergency Vehicle Operation Class.  Once they’ve completed the class, they will teach all emergency personnel the course.  More information in this regard will be posted on the website.

 

QUESTIONS – Rhonda asked:

 

  • What happened to the fuel tank that we talked about purchasing for the MCA Building?

 

Beth responded that we’re holding off in the hopes that we can get one from a farm for free.  If anyone knows of a fuel tank that is available, please contact your Team Leader/Board Member.

 

·         What is the code for Carney Lakes gate?

 

We need to use bolt cutters, which we currently do not have in the ambulance.

 

VISIBILITY ISSUES --  MCA will sponsor an Ice Cream Social on September 9 at 3:00 at the Ambulance Building.  Each team will serve different items at different stations.  Beth is preparing a flyer to be hand delivered to the ambulance building neighborhood, Wilderness Ranch Volunteer Fire Department, Robie Creek Volunteer Fire Department, and the Sheriff’s Office.  The flyer will also be posted at the mailbox banks at Mores Creek Rim Ranches, Wilderness Ranch, etc.  You will be hearing more information from your Team Leader.

 

ADVISORY BOARD MEETING – Beth discussed Idaho City Ambulance’s concern regarding our members attending the Idaho City Rodeo as EMT’s, when this is a fundraising event for IC’s unit.  There was some miscommunication & we stepped on some toes.  Beth will post Jan Peterson’s e-mail regarding this issue on the bulletin board.  MCA needs to respect the fundraising needs of all other units in the County.

 

FUNDRAISING COMMITTEE – Maureen reported that the committee has a meeting planned, where they will discuss the raffle for the SW Airlines tickets.

 

COMMUNITY SERVICE PROJECT --  We need to think about a community service project/food drive for sometime in the fall.

 

IDAHO SAFE HAVEN ACT – Beth discussed this act, which allows the parent(s) of an infant (less than 30 days old) who are unable to care for the child, to contact our unit to take the child & turn him/her over to law enforcement.  Gus mentioned that if it is a healthy baby, it should be turned over to law enforcement; however, in the case of a child with health concerns, it would be more practical to turn the child over to a medical facility.

 

TRAINING – Bloodborne pathogens – commenced at 7:27 p.m.  Training by Gus Catherman & Kim Tkacs.  Gus showed a video entitled, “AIDS/Hepatitis and the Emergency Responders”. 

 

QUESTIONS:

·         What do we do with the Bio Hazard bag if we’re not at a hospital facility? John Nesbit offered to bring a bucket for the ambulance bay that can retain the bag until we can take it to the hospital.  It was also suggested that you write a message on the board in the ambulance bay that there are biohazards in the bucket that need to be transported to the hospital.

 

·         If blood is spilled on the ambulance floor, should we use the bucket & mop at the hospital to clean the ambulance?  No—disinfect first with the disinfectant spray in the ambulance, then you can mop the floor.

       

There being no further business, the meeting adjourned at 8:30 p.m.

 

Submitted by Carol Riazzi, Secretary/Treasurer.
