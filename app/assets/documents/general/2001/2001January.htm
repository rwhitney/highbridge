MINUTES OF

MORES CREEK AMBULANCE REGULAR MEMBER MEETING

January 16, 2001

Traci Kinsman called the Regular Member Meeting of Mores Creek Ambulance to order at 7:05 p.m. The meeting was held at the Mores Creek Ambulance Building. Those officers present were: Traci Kinsman, President; Beth Chandler, President-elect; Carol Riazzi, Secretary/Treasurer; and Mietta Sibert, EBCA Advisory Board. Members present: Beve Doolittle, Maureen Ericksen, Beth Gorringe, Gerry & Patty Warton, Drew Bodie, John McCarthy, Dave McGlone, Tim Schaff, Bjorn Burgeson, Karen & Terry Acker, Nance Cooper, Steve Kilpatrick, Rhonda Grindstaff, Kimberly & Eric Tkacs, Jeff & Jodi Pape, Bill & Nancy Bishop and Gus Catherman. Michelle Larsen, who lives on the Robie Creek Mesa, was also in attendance---she’s interested in becoming a driver.

CHAIR DRIVE –Traci suggested bringing something from your home to the next general meeting for the ambulance building---towels, spatulas, dishes, etc. If you donate a chair, you can have your name placed on the back of the chair as a "signature" donation. Drew Bodie suggested that a list be made of what is still needed for the building.

SCHEDULE – Traci is looking for an EMT to join her & Rhonda on the schedule for the Saturday, Jan. 27 a.m. shift. Mietta volunteered. She also asked everyone to review January’s calendar, as there are still some "out of service" shifts. February’s calendar was distributed during the meeting.

Dirk is sending an email to everyone inviting them to join Groups@AOL. It will be used as a central location for MCA information/events/newsletters/pictures, etc., until a permanent website is established.

CHAINS-- The automatic chains have been installed on the ambulance. John McCarthy will show everyone how to use them (red switch on the console) after training.

STATE COMM – When you are toned-out by State Comm, make sure that all of your team has responded before you leave home; if not, landline State Comm and have them re-tone the team members who have not responded.

LANDING ZONE—There is a landing zone for Life Flight at the Mores Creek Rim Ranch entrance---it is marked with a windsock.

JUMP KITS – Traci asked who is currently carrying jumpkits in their personal vehicles---Jodi, Kim, Mietta, Beth C. & Dirk.

FAMILY SUPPORT—Beth Chandler discussed a card she had printed to be used by the EMT’s when a call results in a need for family support due to loss of a family member, etc. The card (which should be completed & left with the family) lists resources for support groups & also the name & phone number of the EMT on call. These cards will be located in a plastic sack in the jumpbox in the ambulance.

EMERGENCY RESPONDER DISCOUNT CARDS – Mietta negotiated with Clear Creek Lodge & the Rocks Lodge for meal discounts for MCA members. The Rocks Lodge offers 15% off any individual or family dining; Clear Creek Lodge’s discount is 10%. See Mietta for these cards---she’s asking for a 50 cent donation to the unit for the two cards.

TEAM LEADERS – Traci reiterated the January Newsletter discussion of the Team Leaders & their purpose. She introduced them and mentioned the committee they will focus on---Rhonda Grindstaff—Recruiting Committee, Maureen Ericksen—Fund Raising Committee, John McCarthy---Equipment/Supplies/Building Maintenance Committee and Jodi Pape—Communications Committee. She mentioned that we need an SOP Committee & that Nance Cooper expressed interest in being on this Committee. She also mentioned the insurance information that the Team Leaders are currently gathering from all members. Bill Bishop mentioned that the County is working with ICRMP to cover volunteers’ personal vehicles being driven to an ambulance call in a "primary" fashion. Further discussion ensued regarding this insurance.

FUND RAISING COMMITTEE REPORT-- Beth Gorringe & Maureen Ericksen reported on the current activities of the Fund Raising Committee. Tentative plans are as follows: Southwest Airlines donated two round trip tickets that will be used for a Classic car rally. The idea is to contact car clubs to bring their cars for display, charging them $10-$20, and whichever car is voted best by the general public, will win the two airline tickets. This is planned for sometime in June, 2001. They will also be soliciting for other prizes for raffles and there will be a concession stand. Potential location: Robie Creek park or Spring Shores Marina. Drew Bodie suggested that the committee check with the other fire departments, etc., in the area, so we are not overlapping dates of fundraising events. Jeff Pape mentioned that he knew several people who may be interested in bringing their cars & Traci suggested he get together with the Committee on this.

RADIOS – Traci mentioned that Ron Knopp of Mountain Search & Rescue may have some used radios for us. We have asked for 50 & once they pass inspection, we’ll at least get the first 15.

TRAINING-- Bill Bishop followed with Radio Operations/Communications Training.

There being no further business, the meeting adjourned at 9:00 p.m.

Submitted by Carol Riazzi, Secretary/Treasurer.
