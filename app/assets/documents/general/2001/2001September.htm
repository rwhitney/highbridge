MINUTES OF

MORES CREEK AMBULANCE REGULAR MEMBER MEETING

September 18, 2001

 

Beth Chandler called the Regular Member Meeting of Mores Creek Ambulance to order at 6:50 p.m. The meeting was held at the Mores Creek Ambulance Building.  Officers present: Beth Chandler, President, Carol Riazzi, Secretary/Treasurer and Bren Parkhurst, Secretary/Treasurer-elect.  Members present: Maureen Ericksen, Terry Acker, Gus Catherman, Kimberly Tkacs,  Margie Jones, Andrew Smith, Karen Acker, John Nesbit, Mietta Sibert, Wes Towle, Rusty Ambrose & John McCarthy.

 

GUESTS, NEW/PROSPECTIVE MEMBERS – Beth introduced Wes Towle, who is now an official driver and John Grubbs, a prospective member.

 

AUGUST MEETING MINUTES --- A motion was made by Gus & seconded by Margie to approve the minutes of the August 21 Regular Member Meeting as distributed.

 

ADVISORY BOARD MEETING --- Dirk was not in attendance, so there were no Advisory Board items to be discussed.

 

UPCOMING TRAINING EVENTS --- Kim discussed several upcoming Training events:

  • September 18, 2001 (tonight’s meeting)---Emergency Vehicle Response training by Dennis Putnam of ADA County Paramedics
  • September 30, 2001 – As a follow-up to tonight’s training, there will be an Emergency Vehicle Obstacle Course being presented by Kimberly Tkacs & Karen Acker at Spring Shores.  Every member needs to stop by that day for a 15-20 minute training.
  • October 16, 2001 @ 7 p.m. (next month’s meeting) – Triage/Incident Command training by Doug Trosky & his son
  • October 27, 2001 @ 9 a.m. at the MCA building – Ropes Rescue training by Dave Ockerman of ADA County Paramedics.  This is an 8 hour class---be prepared to do some rope climbing!
  • November 20, 2001 @ 7 P.M. (November meeting) – AED training by Doug Trosky & Kimberly Tkacs---this class is ARC certified and will last up to 2 hours.

 

Kim also discussed the staged event on Highway 21 the previous Saturday (Sept. 15).

 

RESCUE ME 2001 – Beth mentioned that the applications for this course are available.  It’s a great learning experience.

 

ICE CREAM SOCIAL REPORT --- Beth reported that the Ice Cream Social was a nice event---the Sheriff’s Office didn’t make it, but we had approximately 100 people show.  There’s still ice cream left in the building freezer for anyone interested (store-bought only---all of the good homemade stuff is gone!)

 

TEAM POINT COUNTS – No one had a final count, so Beth will e-mail the current totals to everyone.

 

LANDING PAD CLEAN-UP – On Sunday, October 7, we’re pulling together a clean-up crew to clear the Landing Zone on Highway 21 (near Milemarker 21) at the entrance to Mores Creek Rim Ranches.  Anyone who is available, please show up with your rakes, shovels, etc.

 

OCTOBER CALENDAR --- Karen is passing around the October calendar for shift sign-up.  If there is anyone who can fill some of the remaining empty shifts in September, it would be most appreciated.

 

MISCELLANEOUS BUSINESS ---

  • Beth mentioned that she had sent out an email regarding moving items in the ambulance and not returning them to their original location.  This cannot happen!!!  Too many people rely on items being in certain spots for quick response.  If you have a suggestion for moving an item in the ambulance to a more convenient/practical spot, please forward your suggestion to Beth Gorringe, our Supplies & Equipment Officer & Kimberly Tkacs, Training Officer.
  • Beth Gorringe is checking with the manufacturer to make sure we have the right battery/charger for the portable suction.
  • Boise County is requesting an updated roster for the Sheriff’s Office & Disaster Services---Team Leaders need to get drivers’ license #’s within 1 week & forward them to Dirk to be added to the roster.
  • Hal Gross mentioned that there is a concern that terrorists may be looking to steal ambulances and fire trucks to gain access to emergency situations.  He is looking into a 3-4 hour terrorist training.  We need to “be aware”.
  • Requests for volunteers: 

(1)       Sept. 22 --- BSU game to collect money for the American Red Cross---contact Jennifer Stolley

(2)  Sept. 29 --- Karate Tournament at BSU old gym--- 9:30 to 4--- information on the bulletin board

·         Petty Cash Receipts—We are short $’s---make sure receipts for food (transports only) are left in the petty cash envelope in the binder in the ambulance.

·         Fund Raising Committee – The committee distributed raffle tickets, which are $3 each, for 2 round-trip tickets anywhere Southwest Airlines flies in the U.S.   These are reserved tickets—they expire on June 30, 2002.  The drawing will be held at the next meeting---October 16.  The stub with the name, address & telephone # needs to be returned to the unit, along with the money.

·         Newsletter---these will now be posted on the website---mcaems.org.

 

 

At 7:15 p.m. Dennis Putnam of Ada County Paramedics conducted the Emergency Vehicle Response Training, which concluded at 9:05 p.m.

 

There being no further business, the meeting adjourned at 9:05  p.m.

 

Submitted by Carol Riazzi, Secretary/Treasurer.
