MINUTES OF

MORES CREEK AMBULANCE REGULAR MEMBER MEETING

February 20, 2002

 

Beth Chandler called the Regular Member Meeting of Mores Creek Ambulance to order at 7:02 p.m. The meeting was held at the Mores Creek Ambulance Building.  Officers present: Beth Chandler, President; Terry Acker, Vice President; and Nancy Bishop, Treasurer.  Members present: Karen Acker, Gus Catherman, Doug Trosky, Aimee Hastriter, Carol Riazzi, Joe Rosales and Lindsey Ragsdale.

 

NEW MEMBERS – Beth introduced new members: Joe Rosales and Lindsey Ragsdale, both recent EMT graduates.

 

JANUARY MEETING MINUTES --- Since the Secretary was not in attendance, approval of the January minutes will be held off until the March meeting.

 

CALENDAR REMINDER –

  • Everyone needs to sign up for the March calendar by February 25—the day the calendar will be sent to State Comm (which is 3 days prior to the beginning of the new month).
  • Race to Robie Creek – scheduled for April 20.  There is an organizational meeting for the Race on March 4—Beth will attend.  MCA staffs the area from the summit to the finish line.  We will be staffing in teams of 2.  We will need ATV’s.  Terry will place a sign-up sheet on the web.  Drivers can help, too.
  • May 31 and June 1--- Idaho/S. Oregon Conference at BSU

 

HEP B SHOT & TB TEST – Gus reported that everyone (drivers and EMT’s) is offered the opportunity to receive a HEP B shot and TB test.  If you don’t want to have the shot/test, please complete the refusal form, which will be available on the website.  It takes 6 months for the series of HEP B shots.  The TB test is not an injection, and you have to be checked 42-78 hours following the test.  Both the shot & test can be done at Central District Health (authorization form is also available on the website).

 

FUNDRAISER MEETING – A Fundraiser Committee Meeting was held a week ago.  There is a pink poster in the ambulance building that represents our wish list---new gurney, AED, special teams supplies, etc.  The Fundraiser Committee is proposing a Rubber Duck Race that is being coordinated with Robie Creek & Wilderness Ranch Fire Departments.  We are waiting for permission from DEQ and the Corps of Engineers.  Each unit would receive a number of ducks to sell & the top 3 winners would receive money prizes.  We could also sell raffle tickets, with the person guessing the # and unit of the winning duck, receiving a large prize.  Joe suggested a booth at the River Festival and/or actually having the Duck Race at the Festival.

 

RECENT CALLS --- Terry discussed a recent call concerning a 12 year old overdose/suicide attempt.

 

INCIDENT COMMAND SYSTEM --- Doug discussed the use of name tags for EMS on scenes involving an IC.  Additional information will be forthcoming. 

 

KARNEY LAKES – Doug also mentioned that the entrance at Karney Lakes is being changed---probably to an electric gate that is activated by the wail of a siren for 3 seconds.

 

TREASURER’S REPORT – Nancy Bishop reported a checkbook balance of $2,973.12.  She also mentioned that Stephanie Johnson of the Robie Creek Mesa, donated $250 to the unit.  Beth will prepare a thank you note/letter to Stephanie.

 

At 7:45 p.m. Karen Acker conducted PCR Completion Training, followed by Ambulance Radio Operations by Terry Acker.  The training concluded at 8:20 p.m.

 

There being no further business, the meeting adjourned at 8:20 p.m.

 

Submitted by Carol Riazzi
