They have a crane and a basket that can lift injured people from down below. If it’s after hours, the combination to the gate is in the ambulance.  October through March is when most of the work will be done.

 

WINTER SURVIVAL TRAINING: Aimee Hastriter went through the contents of our survival kit and talked about using the contents.  She also had handouts on how to build snow shelters.  Some of the items are listed below.   The kit contains a full list so you know what should be in it, and Aimee and/or Karen will find a list of recommended contents for personal kits.  As for clothing, silk, polypropylene, and wool are best. Cotton get cold and wet and stays cold and wet.

 

MCA Kit Contents:

* Clothing: wool hats and socks

 * Food: bullion, jolly ranchers, Balance bars, packet of tuna fish; bottled water;

* Aluminum pan to heat things; camp heat (kind of like a Sterno can)

* Fire starting stuff: matches, flint match, fire starter--may want to add a lighter

* Miscellaneous: wire saw, glow stick, whistle w/compass on top, candle, signal mirror, fastening stuff, emergency blankets, blue tarp, flash light that turns into a headlamp, toilet paper in plastic bags.

 

Other things to consider adding:

* Nylon rope and a cheap tarp for a shelter. Or a tube tarp.

* Trash bags or ponchos.

* A plastic dish or small shovel.

* A small piece of foam to sit on or put under the patient.

 

There’s a link to a survival kit web site on the MCA web site.

 

Rule of Three’s:

- You can live without oxygen for 3 minutes.

- You can live for 3 hours without shelter.

- You can live 3 days without water.

- You can live 3 weeks without food

 

Boise County Sheriff has two snow mobiles and a tobogan.
