CALL REPORTS:

  • Dirk, John, Nancy and Doug responded to call at Arrowrock. Two guys drove off the cliff into the lake. Upon arrival one patient was found approximately mile marker 9 in a boat on the back of a trailer. The other patient was nowhere around. Upon further investigation, he was found about 2 miles up the lake still in the water. With the sheriff’s assistance, it turned into a water rescue. As it turns out the two patients had stayed in their vehicle until it had completely filled with water then tried to evacuate. Life flight airlifted both patients.
  • Karen, Terry, Mietta and Billy responded to a call at Spring Shores. Man fell off cliff, about 25-foot drop, then 1.5-foot diameter rock fell onto his butt fracturing his pelvis. He was in a large amount of pain. Life flight airlifted him.
  • Kim, Terry, Eric and Karen responded to Spring Shores Marina. Man fell off bow of Bayliner. The boat went overtop of him and the prop sliced his ankle. Medic in marina was first responder and wrapped ankle. MCA transported him to area ER.
  • Karen, Terry and Billie responded to 4-wheeler rollover at Daggett Creek ridge. Patient was firefighter trying to access fire on ATV and it rolled. Complained of pain in foot and knee. Applied wrap and transported to area ER.
  • Beth, Gus and Jim responded to Spring Shores Marina. Man ran his jet ski into a rock wall. He reported having two beers. Life flight was called but unknown who dispatched them.
  • Doug reported of a 4-wheeler rollover in Robie Creek area. Because of a bad address, it was hard to find the accident scene. State Comm. finally got the correct address to Doug and they were able to respond. One passenger was lifeflighted and other passenger was transported by ambulance to area ER.

 

 

ADDITIONAL CALL REPORT INFORMATION:

  • For future calls to Arrowrock and/or Spring Shores, remember that most patients are already out of the water by the time we arrive. Gather as much information as possible on how they got out of the water.
  • Always evaluate yourself after every call.
  • If toned out, make sure State Comm. knows you’re responding even if it means finding a landline. State Comm. has new employees and because they don’t always answer your response, you don’t know for sure that they’ve heard you. Plus their acknowledgement needs to be heard by the other members of your team so they know you’re coming.
