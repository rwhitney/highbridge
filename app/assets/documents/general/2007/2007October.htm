Incentives for shift sign-up:  The need for a return to shift sign-up incentives was discussed.  The Board has agreed that we would have a drawing each month for a prize.  Members will get one chance in the drawing for every 4 shifts that they have signed up for in a given month.  The more shifts, the more chances.  The prize will be a $25.00 gift certificate of the winner’s choice.  The first drawing will be held at the Nov.  Meeting for shifts in Oct.

 

A reminder to sign up for shifts will be sent via Email 3 or 4 days in advance to remind people to sign up for shifts.

 

The members in attendance felt this was a good idea.

EBCA update, cont.

 

·         Recruitment:  There are plans to offer an EMT class as an elective to High School seniors.

 

·         Clothing:  EBCA delayed discussion of clothing allowance issues at this time.

 

·         ALS Rendezvous:  EBCA has agreements with Air St. Luke’s and Life Flight to provide Advanced Life Support.  There is no agreement with Ada County paramedics.  When requesting an ALS rendezvous we need to request ASL or Life Flight, not “First available”.  If a situation ever comes up where Ada County Paramedics are the only available, we are to completely transfer care of the patient to Ada County (put patient on their ambulance.  We can’t let the Ada County Paramedics on our ambulance.

 

·         Ambulance Weight Issues:  Ron gave a demonstration of a demo that he presented at the EBCA meeting.  What was shown was brake failure caused by the ambulance being overloaded.  The emergency brake cable was pinched causing the left rear brake to not disengage causing the brake shoes, brake drum and wheel cylinder to overheat beyond limits.

 

Ron, Lea and Drew will set up a time to go through the ambulance to remove items to get the load down to acceptable levels.

 

·         ALS Crew Pickup:  EBCA is receptive to the idea of a single crew member picking up the ALS crew at the landing zone and transporting them to the patient and back to the helicopter.

 

Ron will address the issue with EBCA the possibility of Wilderness Ranch Fire personnel becoming associate members of EBCA to enable them to drive the ambulance to a call when only one EMT is available.

 

Training Issues:  Several training issues were discussed.

 

·         Mulage:  The general agreement was that this works well as a training tool.  We discussed having our own “mulager”.  Maureen and Dawn are interested in volunteering.  Todd Boggs indicated that he has done mulage for training in the military.  The group will get together the first part of the year.  Carolyn will get information about purchasing a training DVD.

·         Training Topics to consider:  Ambulance operations, Landing Zone, Chest trauma, OB, Training with QRU to coordinate report.  Other items discussed for consideration include:  Feeding the masses with training, What gets you to a training? What worked? DVD format, hand-outs, posters.  Does training with other units impede our own learning?

·         Mass Casualty Exercise:  Carolyn has a CD of pictures taken during training exercise.  Carolyn will be putting together a summary of the comments received after the training in Sept. It was generally agreed that we need to have another exercise, perhaps on a smaller scale,  to reinforce what was learned.  It was emphasized that every training should incorporate the basics of Incident Command and Triage.

 

Special Olympics:  Dawn discussed the need for volunteers for the Idaho Special Winter Olympics in Jan. and Feb., 2008 at Bogus Basin.
