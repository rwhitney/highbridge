Infection Control Plan- a committee is working on a plan for Boise County.  Gus has put together a two page plan for the interim.  It is posted on the MCA web site.  Terry proposed a vote to accept the plan developed by Gus, second by Beth and Karen, all members approved.  Dr. Koenig can review and modify.

 

An external audit of Boise County found all 4 ambulance units have one license and one tax code, i.e. one “corporation”.  However all have separate checking accounts.  If the 4 units remain as one corporation, the finances would be merged into one overall account.  The county doesn’t really want to manage such an account. MCA Board members voted to incorporate personnel and assets as More Creek Ambulance, Inc.  Jennifer researched and obtained a Tax ID number from the IRS. There is no real impact on our unit, we’ll keep all our funds.  A 501C3 can own property and go after grants.  Beth proposed that we pursue a 501C3 classification, several second including Peggy.  IRS defines a 501C3 classification as a charity or entity of public protection/education.

 

Boise County Sheriff’s Department- any MCA members can volunteer their time at the dispatch office.

 

Upcoming events- Robie Creek Race is April 19th.  MCA has offered to stage EMTs and drivers on 4-wheelers along the route.  E-mail Terry if you are interested and whether you have a 4-wheeler for at least 2 people.  There will be a party at Billie & Jamie Butlers house.
