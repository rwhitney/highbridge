MCA Board Meeting

March 2nd, 2005

 

Call to Order:  7:00pm

 

Treasurer’s Report not circulated:  Deb Arnswald not present.

 

Advisory Board Report: 

     *There are some new SOP’s from EBCA to be approved regarding using the AED on pediatric patients.  After looking through the equipment, we realized that we would not be able to accept the new SOP’s until we have the appropriate pediatric pads to implement with the AED.

     *We discussed suggesting to Dr. Sturkie that we adopt the state-wide protocols on all items.  Blaine will ask at the next adv. board meeting why we do not.

     *We have been approved to purchase the heater for the ambulance and the storage units for the shed.  We need to get the PO’s from Phyllis.

     *We have received our new trauma tags – some have been stocked in the ambulance and the rest will be kept in storage.

     *John McCarthy raised the question of donating our old Bendix King radios to fire departments that are sorely in need of them, such as Valley of the Pines.

     *They are many MCA members interested in the Basic Trauma Life Support class.  We are looking into submitting to the budget the funds to have a class just for MCA members.

 

Old Business:

     *Grant Status: Our 2003 Highway Safety Grant was approved for the generator lights and board straps that we requested.  We are considering using the additional $150 left over to purchase a high-powered stethoscope for on the ambulance or rig up the ambulance with a rack for the stokes stretcher.  We are applying to get signage for along Highway 21 with money from the 2005 Highway Safety grant.  John McCarthy informed us that the FEMA Grant is now open to EMS as well as fire units.  Kim said she would work with John on getting in a grant application for MCA.

     *SOAP documentation:  we will take a look at Drew’s software for this and discuss this issue further in the future.

     *John McCarthy volunteered to submit ads to the Wilderness Ranch newspaper and to the Idaho World asking for people to volunteer with MCA.

 

New Business:

     *Training on Tuesday is cancelled due to the Wilderness Ranch Fire meeting.  The training will still be held on Saturday, the 12th,  from 12-3.

     *Joan will talk to Ken Wells about getting a good map of the Wilderness Ranch area to Terry Acker so he can put it on the website for directions to the fire barn for trainings.  Dirk will email training changes to Terry to put on the website.

     *Joan is talking with Brad Trosky about sending people down to town for an EMT class in the late summer.

     *Molly sent out an email communication regarding the meeting time and date, but it was not received by many of the members.  She will look into this and ensure that future communications are received by all.

     *Is there going to be an Epipen training in Idaho City?  Blaine will ask at the next EBCA meeting.  Blaine will also ask about the driver’s training.

     *Dirk will get in touch with the drivers to find out what kind of training they are interested in and find a way to get them into the training schedule.

 

Meeting went into Executive Session at 7:54pm
