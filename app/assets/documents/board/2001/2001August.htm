MORES CREEK AMBULANCE EXECUTIVE BOARD MEETING

August 14, 2001

 

Beth Chandler called the Regular Board Meeting of Mores Creek Ambulance Executive Board to order at 6:41  p.m. The meeting was held at the MCA Building.  Officers present: Beth Chandler, President; Carol Riazzi, Secretary/Treasurer; and Dirk Myers, Vice President and EBCA Advisory Board.

 

Team Leaders present: Margie Jones & Terry Acker (for 20 minutes).

 

TEAMS  --  John Nesbit & Suzanna Smith have been placed on teams: Suzanna on Maureen’s & John on John McCarthy’s team.  Board members are now also on teams.  Beth has 4 Micron employees interested in serving with the unit.  Margie has several prospects also.  The Point System competition was discussed:  will commence August 1---Beth will send out a flyer on the particulars.

 

RADIOS – Beth confirmed that the 10 new radios we are to receive are the new Kenwood-style radio—Model #TK290.

 

ICE CREAM SOCIAL – Has been scheduled for Sunday, September 9 at 3:00 p.m. at the Ambulance Building.  We will send personal invitations to Wilderness Ranch Fire Department, Robie Creek Fire Department, Clear Creek Fire Department, Ambulance Building neighbors and the Sheriff’s Department.  Beth will prepare a flyer to post at the mailbox locations of the neighboring subdivisions.

 

ADVISORY BOARD MEETING –

·         We need to discuss at next Tuesday’s General Member Meeting the parameters for MCA being involved in County events, where a particular unit may be able to use that event for fundraising purposes.  We need to be sensitive to these issues within the County.

·         Life Flight Memberships—All names need to be turned in to the County for the discounted memberships--$5 for an individual; $35 for family membership.

·         New Medical Director—Tom from Life Flight is to check with the ER doctor’s association to see if they can recommend someone to replace Dr. Murphy.  He may not make next Tuesday’s meeting.  Kim, Gus & Matt are working on blood borne pathogen training in his absence.

 

MEETING WITH THE BOISE COUNTY SHERIFF’S OFFICE – Dirk reported on the meeting, saying that the deputies didn’t participate much, but this meeting was an excellent first step to building a relationship between the SO’s office & MCA.  MCA members were offered to ride along on a Friday or Saturday evening---this will show that we’re interested in what they do.

 

EDUCATION REIMBURSEMENTS – Keith Oliver’s $200 reimbursement for Advanced EMT Class was brought up at the Advisory Board Meeting.  There is a concern about paying out these dollars, without some sort of commitment from the EMT.  Do we want to have some minimum length of time served before we reimburse?  A discussion ensued regarding the advantages/disadvantages of a minimum of 1 year of service before reimbursement vs. $50 reimbursement for the 1st year & full reimbursement after the completed year of service.  (There was no formal agreement on this---do we want to poll the Board & Team Leaders?)

 

There was also discussion of those members who have not been active in the unit:  Jeff & Jodi Pape, Justin & Curran Padour, Roger Spaulding & Jim Bryant.  Margie agreed to call & ask if they’re planning to be active (Dirk will contact Jim Bryant).  We’ll send a letter to those individuals & the County telling them if they need a 6 month leave or are planning to resign, this request needs to be sent to the Board in writing.  Additionally, if they don’t pull call by the end of September, we’ll assume they’re inactive.

 

MISCELLANEOUS—

  • Computer chip for ambulance---if we get the grant, the chip will be included.
  • ID Safe Haven Act---will be discussed at general member meeting next Tuesday & will be in the next Newsletter.
  • Elections – November will be annual elections.  We need to establish a Nominating Committee at the September meeting.
  • SOP’s/By-Laws & Member Handbook – On Sunday, Dirk & family will run off the copies, so they will be available to distribute at Tuesday’s member meeting.  We’ll make sure to mention that these are a “work in progress”.
  • Bren is working on the minutes from the July member meeting.
  • Dirk mentioned that the Commissioners are looking for an EMS Coordinator for Boise County.  The first year it will be a volunteer position.  Dirk is interested & asked if we want to bring this up at Tuesday’s meeting & it was decided that we would.

 

 

There being no further business, the meeting was adjourned at 8:15 p.m.
