MORES CREEK AMBULANCE BOARD MEETING

JANUARY 9, 2002

 

ATTENDEES: Karen Acker, Terry Acker, Nancy Bishop, Beth Chandler, Maureen Ericksen, Rhonda Grindstaff, Dirk Myers, Teresa Snyder.

 

EBCA ADVISORY BOARD JANUARY 8 MEETING

 

Beth Chandler gave an update on the following topics that were discussed at the January 8 Advisory Board meeting:

 

1. Construction site at Arrowrock Dam:  Mores Creek will be handling EMS in the construction area.  Beth asked that they call out us and the Special Ops Team also because it may involve going over the edge. The site sounds dangerous.

 

2. The county is looking into aligning another resource to help in the county--NPA.

 

3. New Medical Director:  We have a new medical director, Dr. Po Huang, from St. Al’s.  His resume is impressive.

 

4. Certification Updates:  Beth gave Karen two-pages from the EMS Bureau to update the Certification Standards Manual sections on Initial Certification and Reversion.

 

5. EBCA Advisory Board Minutes:  Dirk has asked Theresa Long to e-mail the minutes to Dirk and Terry for posting on our web site.

 

6. Upcoming EMS-related classes:  Karen had announcements for three classes to be distributed to members.  Rhonda will try to scan them into an electronic format to distribute and post on the web site.  We will also post hard copies at the building.

 

7. Information for the New EMT Class: There is a clause regarding service commitments that class attendees need to know.

 

8. A member’s letter to East Boise County was discussed.  East Boise County will contact the member to resolve the issue.  The MCA Board had a discussion of procedures for dealing with confidential issues. Nancy gave us a brief summary of how to handle it from a legal perspective.

 

10. The other issue that came up was the issue of children at the shed. The County will look into insurance and liability, but for now the answer is no. MCA members need to understand that there can be no expectation for privacy in the building, but be polite when entering the building if you know someone is staying there.
