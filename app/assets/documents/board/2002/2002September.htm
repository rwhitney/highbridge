MORES CREEK AMBULANCE BOARD MEETING

September 9, 2002

 

Meeting called to order at 7:10pm by Beth Chandler

 

BOARD MEMBERS PRESENT: Beth Chandler, Terry Acker, Nancy Bishop, John McCarthy and Jennifer Haney

 

OTHER MEMBERS PRESENT: Karen Acker, Training Officer

 

EAST BOISE COUNTY AMBULANCE: Treasea Long has resigned her position with Boise County. Although her last day was 9/3, she will be staying through the rest of the month on a part-time basis. Nancy moved, Terry 2nd and motion passed to send her a $50 gift certificate to Murphy’s Restaurant in Boise.

 

NEW MEMBERS: 2 new members –Carolyn Bohn has completed driver’s training and orientation, Cindy Drury has completed driver’s training and will attend orientation 9/10.

 

EMT CLASSES: Have not received grant yet but started training list. Would like to hold classes beginning in October if the details can be worked out. Any missed classes can be made up. John will find out status of grant at the next Advisory Board Meeting.

 

LZ LIGHTS: A set of 8 strobe lights was found for $425. Beth polled the board via e-mail and received unanimous approval. Lights are on order.

 

NOMINATING COMMITTEE: A nominating committee will be formed this month, with John McCarthy as advisor, to select board officer candidates. The nominating committee will present their candidates during the October meeting. Elections will be held in November.

 

WORK PARTY: There will be a work party 9/6 beginning 7pm at the shed to clear the parking area of weeds. All interested workers please show up with weed eaters, mowers and/or gloves.

 

SCAVENGER HUNT: To be held 9/7 beginning 10am at the shed. Since this is a test of one’s map reading skills….John will ask all teams to mark their maps according to the routes taken to hunt sites. Top team will be awarded special prize. There will be a BBQ lunch after teams return.

 

EXECUTIVE SESSION: Board went into Executive Session from 7:55pm to 8:35pm to discuss personnel matters.

 

WRVFD TRAINING: John asked permission to use shed for WRVFD annual refresher course on structure fires, victim search, scba usage and etc. John will alert area neighbors of training and place ‘WRVFD Training’ signs along the road. Terry moved, Nancy 2nd and request for permission passed.

 

FIRE STANDBY: Although the ambulance is toned out for standby during fire suppression activities, it’s not been clear how long the ambulance is required to stay. The board decided the Incident Commander of each situation should make the decision as to when the ambulance can be released back into service.

FIREFIGHTER AS EMT: The board decided that any Firefighter/EMT that is on shift when a fire call is toned out in his/her area may immediately call State Comm to pull out of service and request them to tone out for backup EMTs to step in and complete the shift. Terry will send a letter to Boise County explaining the decision.

 

ADJOURN: With no further business Beth moved, Terry second, motion carried and the meeting was adjourned at 9:10pm.
