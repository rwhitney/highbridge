ARTICLE 1. Name, Purpose and Service Area

 

Section 1. The organization shall be known as Mores Creek Ambulance, herein referred to as MCA.

 

Section 2. MCA shall be a nonprofit, volunteer organization with the purpose of providing basic emergency medical services in a portion of East Boise County Ambulance District (EBCAD). MCA's coverage area is south of mile post (MP) 29 on highway 21 up to the Ada County line; the areas east and west of this section of highway 21, including Grimes Creek, up to the wooden bridge, Clear Creek, Daggett Creek, Wilderness Ranch, Spring Shores Marina, and the Arrow Rock reservoir up to a distance of 15 miles from highway 21; in the winter time this road all the way to Atlanta.

 

Section 3. Any changes in defining the service area shall be handled through the EBCAB.

 

ARTICLE 2. Membership

 

Section 1. Membership in MCA shall be open to basic emergency medical technicians certified by the state of Idaho, and to others who contribute to the purpose of the organization.

 

Members must be at least 18 years of age, and must have submitted a written application to the MCA board and EBCA documenting their qualifications and intent to abide by the MCA bylaws and Standard Operating Procedures (SOP’s). After approval of their application by the MCA Executive Board, members must participate actively in the organization as defined by the SOP's.

 

Section 2. Grievances

 

Grievances, both from within and from outside the organization, shall be resolved by the MCA Executive Board with due process and respect for confidentiality. The MCA Executive Board reserves the right to have any grievance handled by the EBCAB.

 

Section 3. Disciplinary Review

 

The following shall be grounds for disciplinary review by the MCA Executive Board or if so determined by the EBCAB.

 

a)                   If a member endangers the safety of a patient or other MCA team members; or

 

b)                   If a member displays any type of harassment; or

 

c)                   If a member violates the standards of conduct defined by the SOP’s or fails to abide by standards of performance expected of members; or

 

d)                   If a member makes a public statement or acts in a way that degrades or defames EBCA, MCA or any member.

 

 

 

 

Section 4. Right of Appeal

 

A member who disagrees with the findings of an MCA Executive Board disciplinary review may appeal to the EBCAB. The decision made by the EBCAB shall be final.

 

 

Section 5. Termination

 

The MCA Executive Board shall submit notification of termination of membership to the member by certified mail and to the Idaho State Emergency Medical Service Bureau.

 

 

ARTICLE 3. Officers

 

Section 1. MCA Executive Board

 

The MCA Executive Board shall consist of three and up to five officers, whose duties are described in this section. The Secretary and Treasurer positions may be combined. The position of Advisory Board member representing MCA may be combined with any of the officer positions on the MCA Executive Board.

 

a)                   The President, as principle executive officer of MCA, shall supervise all business affairs of the organization, and shall preside at meetings of the Executive Board and of the general membership. The President shall be the chief spokesperson for the organization (acting also as a Public Information Officer).

 

b)                   The Vice President shall serve as President when the president is absent. The Vice President shall also be responsible for membership and recruiting, and shall be a liaison between the Executive Board and various committees.

 

c)                   The Secretary, shall be custodian of MCA’s records. The Secretary shall record minutes of the Executive Board and general membership meetings.

Minutes shall include, but are not limited to, the time, date and location of the meeting, whether regular or special (and if a special meeting, how authorized and notified), the names of attendees and the proceedings of such meetings.

The Secretary is also responsible for the organization’s correspondence, including notifications and mailings specified by these bylaws.

 

d)                   The Treasurer, shall be the custodian of all MCA funds, keeping accounts of all income and expenditures in records belonging to the organization. The Treasurer shall deposit all MCA funds in accounts designated by the Executive Board; and shall disperse, with vouchers, MCA funds upon order of the Executive Board.

 

The Treasurer shall prepare monthly financial statements for distribution at each Executive Board meeting and shall prepare an annual statement at the end of MCA’s fiscal year.

 

 

e)                   The Advisory Board Member, shall be the day to day liaison between MCA and Boise County; shall be the MCA representative on the EBCAB; and shall serve as advocate of the general membership at meetings of the Executive Board.

 

Each office may have an "elect" position determined by member vote, and lasting not less than six months or more than 12 months prior to taking full responsibility for the activities of that office. The purpose of the elect position is to assure a smooth transition of leadership for our unit.

 

Section 2. Delegation

 

An officer may, with approval of the Executive Board, delegate tasks to other officers or members if they accept such delegation.

 

Section 3. Qualifications

 

Any member in good standing is eligible to become an officer of MCA by an election process.

 

Section 4. Term of Office

 

The general membership shall elect officers to serve for one year. The Executive Board shall remain in office until successors have been elected.

 

Section 5. Nomination and Balloting

 

A nominating committee, appointed by the Executive Board, shall coordinate nominations and present a slate of candidates to the membership at least four weeks before elections. Elections shall be held at the general membership meeting in November.

 

The Secretary shall distribute written ballots. Absentee ballots, submitted in sealed envelopes and signed by those absent, shall be accepted. Ballots shall be tallied immediately after the election. A simple majority shall decide the winners; if no candidate receives a majority of the votes, a run-off election by those present at the meeting shall decide the winners.

 

Section 6. Vacancies

 

Vacancies on the Executive Board shall be filled within one month by Executive Board appointment.

 

Section 7. Removal of Officers

 

Officers may be removed from office in two ways:

 

a)                   A quorum of the Executive Board may remove any officer when evidence is presented that the officer has been delinquent in his or her duties, has abused the position, or for any misconduct in office; or

 

 

b)                   The general membership may, at any general or special meeting, remove any officer by majority vote, provided that all members have received at least four weeks’ notice of the proposed recall.

 

 

ARTICLE 4. Meetings

 

Section 1. General Membership Meetings

 

General membership meetings shall be for sharing items of interest among the membership, and should include a medical or technical presentation or training. They shall be held at least six to ten times each year on a regular basis, and require a quorum of 20 percent of the membership.

 

To maintain interest, topics should be limited and varied. Lengthy discussion or detailed problem solving shall be delegated to committees for reports at later meetings.

 

To save time, a proposed agenda shall be distributed at least one to two weeks before each meeting along with the minutes of the previous meeting. Items that committee members or others wish to be included on the agenda should be conveyed to the President at least 10 days prior to the meeting.

 

Section 2. Executive Board Meetings

 

Executive Board meetings are for administering the business of the organization. They shall be held at least six to ten times a year on an as needed basis and require a quorum. All board members should attend, unless excused by absent notice within three days to the President.

 

Any member may attend and participate (but not vote) at these meetings, with the exception of closed sessions described in Section 3 below. The Executive Board may make decisions about routine matters on behalf of the membership at these meetings. Entirely new business shall become proposals to be taken to the general membership meeting.

 

A proposed agenda and minutes of the last meeting shall be distributed to all officers and Team Leaders at least one to two weeks before each Executive Board meeting.

 

Section 3. Closed Sessions

 

Closed sessions of the Executive Board may only be called to discuss personnel matters, and shall occur in the context of an Executive Board meeting described in Section 2.

 

The general topic of any closed session shall be announced publicly in the agenda, and a general summary of any discussions made shall be given immediately after the closed session. Minutes of the closed session must be given to the personnel involved, but may otherwise be kept confidential.

 

Section 4. Notification

 

Regularly scheduled meetings require no notification. Notice of a special meeting, or changes to the regularly scheduled meeting, requires one week’s advance notice to all members. In rare emergencies, meetings may be called with diligent 48-hour notice by telephone to all available members.

 

 

Section 5. Meeting Conduct

 

All meetings shall be conducted in a businesslike manner that fosters participation and interest by all. If procedures are questioned, Robert’s Rules of Order shall prevail.

 

 

ARTICLE 5. Committees and Special Assignments

 

Special Assignment Officers as detailed in Sections 1-10 are appointed by the Executive Board for a term of one calendar year. Notification of the impending end of the term for current Officers, as well as any mid term openings in any Officer position will be made by the Board thirty (30) days prior to commencement of the interview and selection process. Any active member in good standing is subject to consideration for appointment as a Special Assignment Officer. There are no limits on the number of terms a member may serve as a Special Assignment Officer.

 

Section 1. Presentations

 

Committees, having researched and worked out the details, shall make a concise presentation so that the general membership and Executive Board meetings can run more efficiently.

 

Section 2. Ad Hoc Committees

 

Ad Hoc Committees may be appointed by the Executive Board or general membership to perform specific tasks.

 

Section 3. Training Officer

 

A Training Officer shall be assigned to coordinate training for the members of MCA.

 

The Training Officer must follow through with at least six sessions of MCA continuing education for the EMT-Basic’s and other general members.

 

Section 4. Safety/Hazard Communication Officer

 

The Safety/Hazard Communication Officer shall be assigned to act as a liaison and coordinator of information between state EMS and MCA.

 

This officer needs access to basic and up-to-date information regarding any and all blood borne pathogen concerns, emergency response, emergency vehicles, emergency response personnel safety, etc. The Safety Officer shall work closely with the Training Officer(s) and Infection Control Officer to assure that members of MCA are fully aware of safety issues and exercise those skills learned on every call.

 

Section 5. Vehicle Maintenance Officer

 

A Maintenance Officer shall be assigned to arrange for maintenance scheduling on MCA’s ambulance and emergency response equipment and keep a record of all work completed on the ambulance.

 

 

Section 6. Building Maintenance Officer

 

The Building Maintenance Officer shall see that the MCA quarters, office and ambulance bay are kept in proper working order. A bi-weekly check of the premises shall be conducted with particular attention to the level of supplies on hand and any repairs that may be needed. When repairs are needed, the Building Maintenance Officer shall see that they are taken care of so that additional damage is not caused by the original problem.

 

Section 7. Infection Control Officer

 

The Infection Control Officer shall establish protocols for dealing with the hazards of body fluid infections when MCA members are responding to a call. This officer shall advise members when changes occur in infection management, new equipment/supplies become available and educational opportunities become available. The IC Officer shall work closely with Trainers and Safety positions to assure dissemination of information to our members.

 

Section 8. Personnel Officer

 

The Personnel Officer shall maintain current, confidential files for each member beginning with a membership application, reference notations, certification copies, CE certifications, results of DOT driver record search, letters concerning performance (if applicable), and resignation or termination. (NOTE: The responsibilities of this position can be combined with the duties of the Vice President or can be performed as a separate function.)

Section 9. Supply & Equipment Officer

 

The Supply & Equipment Officer shall assure an adequate level of all dispensable supplies in the ambulance at all times. Additionally, he/she shall maintain the EMS equipment in proper working order.

 

Section 10. Team Leaders

 

For the purpose of improved communication and camaraderie, the unit will be divided into Teams. A Team Leader will head each team. The Team Leader will be responsible for working with members of the team to achieve the goals and objective of MCA. The Team Leader will represent their team at all Executive Board meetings. Team Leaders will serve for a period of one year and will be elected by majority vote of team members present at a meeting established and announced with sufficient advance notice for the express purpose of electing a Team Leader. There are no limits on how many times a person may be elected to this position. Disputes and ties will be decided by the Executive Board, whose decision is final.

 

 

ARTICLE 6. Finances

 

Funds belonging to MCA shall be kept in a separate account, and shall be used only to further the purpose of the organization.

 

ARTICLE 7. Bylaw Amendments

 

Proposed amendments to these Bylaws must be distributed to all members, at least four weeks prior to the general meeting at which they will be voted on. Amendments require approval of 75 percent of the total membership. Upon approval, amendments take effect immediately. The Secretary shall distribute a dated revision of the Bylaws to all members within one month.

 

ARTICLE 8. Standard Operating Procedures (SOP’s)

 

Section 1. SOP’s are a collection of written protocols to help MCA operate in a uniform manner. They shall be developed by committees or by individual team members, and shall be approved by the general membership.

 

Section 2. Proposals for new or revised SOP’s must be distributed to all members at least four weeks prior to the general meeting at which they will be voted on. Approval requires a simple majority of those present at the meeting.

 

Section 3. The Secretary shall distribute SOP’s and revisions in a timely manner to all members.

 

 

ARTICLE 9. Dissolution of Mores Creek Ambulance

 

Section 1. A signed proposal to dissolve the organization must be distributed to all members at least two weeks prior to the general meeting at which it will be discussed. At that meeting, if a majority of members present agrees to dissolve MCA, they shall prepare the wording of a written ballot on the issue, to be voted on after a “cooling off” period of at least two weeks.

 

Section 2. All members shall receive such dissolution ballot at least one week before the general meeting at which it will be voted on. Dissolution shall require approval by 75 percent of the total membership and approval of the EBCAB.
